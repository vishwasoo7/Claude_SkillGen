## 1. PEST SELECTION GUIDE

| Pest Type | Best Remedy | Backup Remedy |
|-----------|------------|--------------|
| Sucking pests (aphid, whitefly, mites, thrips) | Neemastra | Dashaparni Ark |
| Caterpillars, armyworm, pod borer | Brahmastra | Neemastra |
| Stem borer, root grub | Agniastra | Brahmastra |
| Fungal disease (powdery mildew, leaf spot) | Dashaparni Ark | Neemastra |
| General prevention (no active outbreak) | Dashaparni Ark | - |
| Severe multi-pest outbreak | Agniastra | Then Brahmastra 5 days later |
| Ants, termites | Neem oil + lime drench | - |

**Rule of thumb:** Start with Dashaparni Ark preventively. Escalate to Neemastra or Brahmastra on first sign of outbreak. Reserve Agniastra for severe attacks.

## 2. NEEMASTRA

**Purpose:** Controls sucking insects - aphids, whiteflies, thrips, mites, and leaf-eating caterpillars. Disrupts molting and feeding.

### Ingredients (per 1 Vigha = 20 litre batch)

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------|
| Fresh neem leaves (crushed) | 5 kg |
| Desi cow urine | 5 litres |
| Desi cow dung (fresh) | 1 kg |
| Water | Fill to 20 litres |

### Preparation Steps
1. Crush neem leaves well (can use a stone or wooden mortar).
2. Soak crushed leaves in 5 litres cow urine overnight (12 hours).
3. Mix cow dung in remaining water, dissolve lumps.
4. Combine all - leaves, urine, dung water.
5. Stir well. Cover with cloth.
6. **Ready in 24 hours.**
7. Strain through cloth before use. Squeeze leaves well.

### How to Apply
- Dilute: Use as-is (do not further dilute - 20 litres = 1 Vigha dose).
- Spray entire plant - top and underside of leaves.
- Apply in **early morning or late evening**.
- **Do not apply in direct sun** - burns leaves.

### Frequency
- Active outbreak: Every 10 days for 3 applications
- Prevention: Every 21 days during pest season (Kharif)

### Shelf Life
Up to **3 months** in a sealed container, stored in shade.

## 3. AGNIASTRA

**Purpose:** The strongest ZBNF pest remedy. Used for severe infestations of stem borers, root grubs, and heavy caterpillar attacks. Acts as a contact and systemic deterrent.

**⚠️ Use only when truly needed. Not a routine spray.**

### Ingredients (per 1 Vigha = 20 litre batch)

| Ingredient | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Desi cow urine | 20 litres | Base liquid - no water added |
| Tobacco leaves | 500 g | Dried and crushed |
| Green chilli (hot variety) | 500 g | Crushed |
| Garlic | 500 g | Crushed |
| Neem leaves | 250 g | Crushed |

### Preparation Steps
1. Boil cow urine in a large vessel.
2. Add crushed tobacco, chilli, garlic, neem leaves.
3. Boil together on low flame for 30 minutes.
4. Remove from heat. Cover and let rest.
5. Boil again the next day (second boil).
6. Rest again. Repeat for a total of **5 consecutive boiling cycles over 5 days** (some traditions say 3 cycles - minimum 3).
7. Cool completely. Strain through fine cloth.
8. Store in sealed clay pot or food-grade container.

### How to Apply
- Dilute: 3 litres Agniastra in 100 litres water per Vigha.
- Spray entire crop - leaves, stems, soil surface near roots.
- Apply **once during severe outbreak**.
- If still needed, repeat after 7 days - maximum 3 applications per season.

### Frequency
Only during **actual outbreak** - not preventively.

### Shelf Life
Up to **6 months** in a cool, sealed container.

**⚠️ Caution:** Wash hands and clothes after preparation. Avoid contact with eyes. Keep away from children.

## 4. BRAHMASTRA

**Purpose:** Controls hard-bodied insects - caterpillars, beetles, borers - that don't respond to Neemastra. Also effective against fungal infections.

### Ingredients (per 1 Vigha batch = 3 litres concentrate)

| Ingredient | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Neem leaves | 3 kg | |
| Custard apple (sitaphal) leaves | 2 kg | |
| Papaya leaves | 2 kg | |
| Pomegranate leaves | 2 kg | If available |
| Guava leaves | 2 kg | |
| Desi cow urine | 5 litres | |

### Preparation Steps
1. Crush all leaves thoroughly.
2. Boil all crushed leaves in cow urine in a large vessel.
3. Reduce to **one-quarter volume** on low flame.
4. Cool completely.
5. Strain through cloth. Squeeze out all juice from leaves.
6. Store in sealed container.

### How to Apply
- Dilute: 3 litres concentrate in 100 litres water per Vigha.
- Spray entire plant surface including underside of leaves.
- Apply **2-3 times, 5 days apart**, during active outbreak.

### Frequency
- Active outbreak: 3 applications × 5 days apart
- Repeat cycle only if pest pressure persists after 15-day gap

### Shelf Life
Up to **6 months** in sealed container, stored in shade.

**Note:** Custard apple leaves are the most critical ingredient - don't substitute. Pomegranate leaves are optional but strengthen efficacy.

## 5. DASHAPARNI ARK

**Purpose:** "Ten-leaf extract." The broadest-spectrum natural deterrent. Repels most insects, suppresses fungal disease, and strengthens plant cell walls. Used primarily preventively.

### Ingredients (per batch = 100 litre extract)

| Ingredient | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Desi cow urine | 10 litres | |
| Desi cow dung (fresh) | 2 kg | |
| Neem leaves | 2 kg | |
| Custard apple leaves | 1 kg | |
| Papaya leaves | 1 kg | |
| Guava leaves | 1 kg | |
| Pomegranate leaves | 1 kg | |
| Drumstick (moringa) leaves | 1 kg | |
| Bitter gourd / karela leaves | 1 kg | |
| Castor (arandi) leaves | 1 kg | |
| Tulsi / basil leaves | 1 kg | |
| Tobacco leaves | 250 g | |
| Green chilli | 500 g | Crushed |
| Garlic | 250 g | Crushed |
| Water | Fill to 100 litres | |

*(Use any 10 leaves from the above list if all not available - neem is mandatory)*

### Preparation Steps
1. Crush all leaves and mix with cow dung, cow urine, chilli, garlic.
2. Dissolve in water to make 100 litres total.
3. Cover with breathable cloth.
4. Stir daily.
5. **Ready in 30 days.**
6. Strain well before use.

### How to Apply
- Dilute: 3 litres in 100 litres water per Vigha (1:30 ratio).
- Spray entire plant surface preventively once every 7-10 days.

### Shelf Life
Up to **6 months** sealed in shade.

## 7. SPRAY CALENDAR GUIDE

### Kharif Season (July-October)
- **Week 1 after sowing:** Dashaparni Ark preventive spray
- **Every 10 days:** Dashaparni Ark
- **On pest sighting:** Neemastra immediately
- **Severe outbreak:** Agniastra → wait 7 days → Brahmastra

### Rabi Season (November-March)
- **Every 21 days:** Dashaparni Ark preventive
- **On fungal signs:** Neemastra + increase aeration (mulch management)

### General Rules
- Always spray **early morning (before 8 AM) or evening (after 5 PM)**
- Never spray before expected rain (within 6 hours)
- Rotate preparations - don't use same remedy more than 3 cycles consecutively
- Log every spray in AgStack Farm Calendar (see `05-agstack-integration.md`)
