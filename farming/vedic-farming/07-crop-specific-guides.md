## 1. GROUNDNUT (MUNGPHALI)

**Season:** Kharif (sow June-July, harvest October-November)
**Importance for you:** Primary Kharif cash crop in Junagadh district. APMC Keshod
has active groundnut market.

### Seed Preparation
- Treat with Beejamrut 30 minutes before sowing
- Dry in shade - do not delay sowing beyond 6 hours after treatment
- Seed rate: 25-30 kg per Vigha (shelled kernels)
- Sow on Shukla Panchami or Ekadashi (see `03-lunar-calendar.md`)

### Jeevamrut Schedule (per Vigha)
| Stage | Timing | Dose | Method |
|-------|--------|------|--------|
| Pre-sowing | 3 days before sowing | 200L | Soil drench |
| Seedling | 15 DAS | 200L | Drench near root zone |
| Branching | 30 DAS | 200L | Drench + 10L foliar |
| Flowering | 45 DAS | 200L | Drench |
| Pod formation | 60 DAS | 200L | Drench |
| Pod filling | 75 DAS | 200L | Drench |

Total Jeevamrut per season per Vigha: approximately 1,200 litres

### Panchgavya Application
- 45 DAS (at flowering): 3L concentrate diluted 1:30 - sprayed as foliar
- Panchgavya at flowering is particularly important for groundnut - improves pod set

### Pest Management (Groundnut-Specific)
| Pest | When | Remedy | Dose/Vigha |
|------|------|--------|-----------|
| Leaf miner | 20-30 DAS | Neemastra | 20L |
| Aphid | Kharif onset | Neemastra | 20L |
| White grub (root) | Pre-sowing | Neem cake soil drench | 50 kg |
| Tobacco caterpillar | 40-60 DAS | Brahmastra | 3L (1:3 diluted) |
| Tikka leaf spot | 45+ DAS | Dashaparni Ark preventive | 3L (1:10 diluted) |

**Critical for groundnut:** White grub is a serious soil pest in Junagadh.
Apply neem cake at 50 kg/Vigha during soil preparation before sowing.
This is a non-negotiable step - do not skip it.

### Mulching for Groundnut
- Groundnut is a peg crop (pods form underground) - do not mulch too close to stem
- Mulch between rows only, leaving 6-inch clear zone around plant base
- Use groundnut husk from previous season or dry leaves

### Harvest Indicators
- Leaves begin yellowing, lower leaves drop
- Rub a pod - inner skin should be dark pink-brown
- Harvest on Krishna Dashami to Chaturdashi for best shelf life (see `03-lunar-calendar.md`)

## 2. CUMIN (JEERA)

**Season:** Rabi (sow November-December, harvest February-March)
**Importance for you:** Highest APMC value-per-kg crop you can grow in Rabi.
Light feeder, short duration (100-110 days), suits Saurashtra winter.

### Seed Preparation
- Cumin seed is small and delicate - use diluted Beejamrut (50% strength)
- Dilute standard Beejamrut 1:1 with water before treating cumin
- Spread seeds on dry cloth, sprinkle diluted Beejamrut, toss gently
- Dry in shade 1 hour, sow same day
- Seed rate: 2-3 kg per Vigha (broadcast or line sowing)

### Soil Preparation (Critical for Cumin)
- Cumin needs fine, friable seedbed - clods will block germination
- Apply Ghanjeevamrut 50 kg/Vigha as base dressing, rake in well
- Then a light Jeevamrut drench (100L only - do not over-wet the fine seedbed)

### Jeevamrut Schedule
| Stage | Timing | Dose | Method |
|-------|--------|------|--------|
| Pre-sowing | 3 days before | 100L (half dose) | Light drench |
| Seedling | 15 DAS | 100L | Light drench |
| Branching | 30 DAS | 100L | Drench |
| Umbrella (flower heads forming) | 50 DAS | 100L | Drench + foliar 5L |
| Seed fill | 70 DAS | 100L | Very light drench |

**Caution:** Cumin is sensitive to over-irrigation and over-application.
Use half the standard Jeevamrut dose (100L vs. 200L per Vigha).
Excess moisture at root zone in Rabi = fungal death.

### Panchgavya for Cumin
- Apply at umbrella stage (50 DAS): 3L concentrate diluted 1:30
- Critical - improves essential oil content (directly affects APMC price and aroma)

### Pest Management (Cumin-Specific)
| Problem | When | Remedy |
|---------|------|--------|
| Aphid (most common) | From 30 DAS | Neemastra 20L/Vigha every 10 days |
| Powdery mildew | Cool damp periods | Dashaparni Ark 3L diluted 1:10, every 7 days |
| Blight (Alternaria) | Late season | Increase air circulation - avoid dense sowing |

**Prevention is everything for cumin.** Start Dashaparni Ark preventive spray
from 20 DAS regardless of whether pests are visible.

### Mulching for Cumin
- Light mulch only - cumin seedlings are tiny and can be smothered
- Use fine dry sand mixed with vermicompost as surface layer instead of heavy straw
- After 30 DAS when plants are established, light straw mulch between rows is fine

### Harvest
- Harvest when 50-60% of umbels turn brown - do not wait for full brown
- Cut in early morning - essential oils highest before heat of day
- Dry in shade (never direct sun - bleaches seeds, reduces APMC grade)
- Thresh when fully dry

## 3. SESAME (TIL)

**Season:** Kharif or Summer (June-September Kharif; March-June Summer)
**Importance for you:** Very low input, tolerates poor soil well - ideal for
Year 1 detox field section or low-water areas of your 3 Vigha.

### Seed Preparation
- Sesame seed is tiny - use diluted Beejamrut (50% strength, same as cumin)
- Seed rate: 1.5-2 kg per Vigha (broadcast, very light coverage)

### Jeevamrut Schedule
| Stage | Timing | Dose |
|-------|--------|------|
| Pre-sowing | 3 days before | 100L |
| 15 DAS | Seedling | 100L |
| 40 DAS | Branching/flowering | 150L + 5L foliar |
| 60 DAS | Capsule fill | 150L |

### Pest Management (Sesame)
- Leaf roller: Brahmastra 3L (1:3 diluted) at first sign
- Phytophthora blight: Dashaparni Ark preventive from 20 DAS
- Sesame is generally hardy - pest pressure lower than groundnut or cumin

### Harvest
- Harvest when bottom capsules begin cracking (do not wait - seeds fall)
- Harvest early morning - sesame shatters easily in heat

## 4. BAJRA (PEARL MILLET)

**Season:** Kharif (sow June-July, harvest September-October)
**Importance for you:** Best Year 1 detox crop. Extremely hardy, deep roots
break compaction, fodder value for neighboring cattle, APMC market exists.

### Seed Preparation
- Standard Beejamrut treatment, full strength
- Seed rate: 2-3 kg per Vigha

### Jeevamrut Schedule (Bajra is very forgiving)
| Stage | Timing | Dose |
|-------|--------|------|
| Pre-sowing | 3 days before | 200L |
| 20 DAS | Seedling | 200L |
| 45 DAS | Tillering/panicle formation | 200L |

### Pest Management
- Stem borer (most serious): Brahmastra at first sign - 3L per Vigha, 1:3 dilution
- Aphid: Neemastra 20L per Vigha
- Bajra is one of the most resilient crops - ZBNF works extremely well on it

### Notes
- Excellent companion for cowpea (sow cowpea between bajra rows)
- Bajra stalk is valuable mulch material after harvest - shred and spread

## 5. VEGETABLES (MIXED / KITCHEN GARDEN)

For growing leafy vegetables, tomato, brinjal, okra, gourd, or chilli on a portion
of your 3 Vigha - typically 0.5-1 Vigha dedicated to vegetables.

### General ZBNF Protocol for Vegetables

**Soil preparation (very important for vegetables):**
- 500 kg vermicompost per Vigha worked into top 6 inches
- 50 kg rock phosphate mixed with vermicompost before broadcasting
- Pre-sowing Jeevamrut drench: 200L per Vigha

### Seedling Nursery Preparation
- Fill nursery trays or beds with: 50% cured vermicompost + 50% field soil
- Treat nursery medium with diluted Jeevamrut (10L diluted in 20L water) before filling
- Treat all transplant roots in Beejamrut for 20 minutes before transplanting

### Jeevamrut for Vegetables
- Every 10-15 days (more frequent than field crops - higher nutrient demand)
- Dose: 200L per Vigha drench + 10L foliar every application
- Panchgavya: every 15 days from first flowering - critical for fruit crops

### Pest Management (Vegetable-Specific)
| Pest | Crop | Remedy |
|------|------|--------|
| Aphid, whitefly | All | Neemastra 20L, every 10 days |
| Fruit borer | Tomato, brinjal, okra | Brahmastra 3L (1:3) |
| Red spider mite | Tomato, cucumber | Neemastra + Panchgavya combination |
| Fungal wilt | Tomato, brinjal | Dashaparni Ark preventive - every 7 days |
| Caterpillar | Gourd, leafy veg | Brahmastra |

### Staking and Support
- Naturally grown plants on Jeevamrut tend to be stockier with thicker stems
  than chemically grown - require less staking
- For climbing gourds: bamboo trellis preferred over wire (wire conducts heat in Gujarat summer)

### Harvest and Post-Harvest
- Harvest vegetables in early morning before heat - retains freshness longer
- Chemical-free vegetables command 15-30% premium over APMC standard at
  direct sale points - document your zero-input record via INATrace for buyers

## 6. KALI JEERI (BLACK CUMIN / NIGELLA SATIVA)

**Season:** Rabi (November-February in Keshod area)
**Relevance:** This is your current crop being harvested as of April-May 2026.

### Post-Harvest Soil Management
When Kali Jeeri is harvested, its residue is medicinal - has pest-deterrent value.
- Do NOT burn the stubble and residue
- Chop and spread as mulch layer
- Kali Jeeri residue releases volatile compounds that suppress some soil nematodes
- This gives you a natural head start on soil health before Kharif sowing

### If Growing Kali Jeeri Again in Future Rabi
- Very similar requirements to cumin - follow cumin protocol above
- Half-dose Jeevamrut (100L per application)
- Extremely sensitive to waterlogging - raised beds or ridges recommended
- Powdery mildew is the key threat - Dashaparni Ark preventive mandatory from 20 DAS
- Seed rate: 3-4 kg per Vigha

## 7. INTERCROPPING PATTERNS FOR KESHOD

Intercropping improves soil health, reduces pest pressure, and increases income
per Vigha. These are tested combinations for Saurashtra conditions.

### Kharif Combinations
| Main Crop | Intercrop | Ratio | Benefit |
|-----------|-----------|-------|---------|
| Groundnut | Cluster Bean (Guar) | 4:2 rows | Guar fixes N, repels some pests |
| Bajra | Cowpea | 2:1 rows | Cowpea fixes N, provides shade at base |
| Sesame | Cowpea | 4:2 rows | Cowpea provides ground cover |
| Bajra | Moth Bean | 2:2 rows | Both are drought-tolerant |

### Rabi Combinations
| Main Crop | Intercrop | Ratio | Benefit |
|-----------|-----------|-------|---------|
| Cumin | Coriander (Dhaniya) | 4:1 rows | Coriander deters cumin aphid |
| Chickpea | Mustard | 6:1 rows | Mustard traps aphids away from chickpea |
| Wheat | Methi (Fenugreek) | 4:2 rows | Methi suppresses weeds |

### Intercropping Rules for ZBNF
- Never mix crops with very different water needs in the same bed
- Match Jeevamrut timing to the more sensitive crop (use the more conservative schedule)
- Log intercropping layout in AgStack Farm Calendar with separate parcel entries
- At harvest, record yield of both crops separately to measure system productivity
