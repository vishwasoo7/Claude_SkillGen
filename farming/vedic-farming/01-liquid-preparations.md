## 1. JEEVAMRUT (Liquid)

**Purpose:** Activates and multiplies soil microorganisms. Restores biological fertility. The single most important input in ZBNF.

### Ingredients (per 1 Vigha batch = 200 litres)

| Ingredient | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Desi cow dung (fresh) | 10 kg | Same-day fresh preferred |
| Desi cow urine (fresh) | 5-10 litres | Collected morning |
| Jaggery (old / dark) | 2 kg | Black jaggery preferred; activates fermentation |
| Pulse flour (besan/toor dal flour) | 2 kg | Any local pulse works |
| Forest/garden soil (undisturbed) | 1 handful | From under a tree or hedge - carries native microbes |
| Water (non-chlorinated) | Fill to 200 litres | Well water or rainwater |

### Preparation Steps
1. Add all ingredients to a 200-litre clay or food-grade plastic container.
2. Fill to 200 litres with water.
3. Mix thoroughly by hand or wooden stick - break all lumps of cow dung.
4. Cover loosely with a jute/cotton cloth (not sealed - must breathe).
5. Place in shade, away from direct sunlight.
6. **Stir clockwise twice daily** - morning and evening.
7. **Ready in 48 hours** (summer) or 72 hours (winter).
8. Strain through jute cloth before applying.

### How to Apply
- **Drench method:** Dilute strained Jeevamrut 1:1 with water. Apply 200 litres per Vigha directly to soil near root zone via drip, furrow, or watering can.
- **Foliar method:** Dilute 10 litres strained Jeevamrut in 100 litres water. Spray on leaves, especially underside. Apply early morning or after sunset.

### Frequency
- First 3 months: Every 15 days
- Soil stabilized: Monthly
- After rainfall: Apply within 24 hours to amplify microbial surge

### Shelf Life
Use within **3 days of preparation**. Discard and make fresh if left longer.

## 2. JEEVAMRUT (FOLIAR SPRAY)

Same recipe as above. For foliar use only:
- Strain twice through fine cloth
- Dilute 1 litre Jeevamrut in 10 litres water
- Spray per Vigha: 10 litres concentrate → 100 litres final spray volume
- Apply only when no rain expected for 4-6 hours

## 3. BEEJAMRUT

**Purpose:** Seed/root treatment. Protects against soil-borne fungal disease. Improves germination rate and early root strength.

### Ingredients (treats seed for 1 Vigha)

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------|
| Desi cow dung (fresh) | 5 kg |
| Desi cow urine | 5 litres |
| Unslaked lime (chuna) | 50 g |
| Water | 20 litres |
| Soil from tree base | 1 handful |

### Preparation Steps
1. Dissolve lime in 1 litre water separately. Let settle.
2. Mix cow dung in 20 litres water. Stir well.
3. Add cow urine and soil.
4. Carefully add dissolved lime water (slowly, while stirring).
5. Let rest for 24 hours in shade.
6. Strain before use.

### How to Apply

**For seeds:**
- Spread seeds on a cloth/tarpaulin
- Sprinkle Beejamrut over seeds until all are lightly coated
- Gently mix by hand
- Dry in shade for 30 minutes
- Sow immediately or within 6 hours

**For seedlings / transplants:**
- Dip roots in Beejamrut solution for 20-30 minutes
- Plant immediately after

**For sets / tubers (onion, garlic, potato):**
- Soak in Beejamrut for 30 minutes
- Dry in shade, plant same day

### Shelf Life
Use within **3 days**. Make fresh for each sowing event.

## 4. PANCHGAVYA

**Purpose:** Complete plant tonic - boosts immunity, promotes flowering, fruit development, and overall vigor. Rich in growth hormones and micronutrients.

### Ingredients (makes approx. 20 litres concentrate)

| Ingredient | Quantity | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Desi cow dung (fresh) | 3 kg | |
| Desi cow ghee | 500 g | Desi only - not commercial |
| Desi cow urine | 3 litres | |
| Desi cow milk (raw) | 2 litres | |
| Desi cow curd (fresh) | 2 litres | |
| Coconut water (fresh/tender) | 3 litres | Optional but beneficial |
| Ripe banana (mashed) | 12 nos | Any variety |
| Sugarcane juice or jaggery water | 3 litres | |

### Preparation Steps
1. Mix cow dung and ghee first. Stir vigorously for 10 minutes until ghee is fully incorporated.
2. Add urine, milk, curd.
3. Add banana (mashed), coconut water, sugarcane juice.
4. Mix all together in a clay or wooden vessel.
5. Cover with breathable cloth.
6. **Stir daily** - both clockwise and counterclockwise - for **30 days**.
7. Fermentation is complete when aroma is earthy-pleasant (not foul).

### How to Apply
- Dilute 3 litres concentrate in 100 litres water (1:30 ratio).
- Apply 3 litres per Vigha (before dilution) as foliar spray.
- Spray on entire plant - leaves, stems, fruit.
- Apply **early morning** (before 8 AM) or **after 5 PM**.
- Avoid midday application - evaporates too fast.

### Frequency
- Vegetative phase: Every 15 days
- Flowering and fruiting: Weekly
- Do not apply during or just after rain

### Shelf Life
Up to **6 months** stored in a cool, shaded place. Stir weekly during storage.

## 5. GHANJEEVAMRUT (SOLID)

**Purpose:** Dry, slow-release version of Jeevamrut. Used as base dressing before planting. Excellent for long-duration crops and fruit trees.

### Ingredients (per 1 Vigha = 50 kg batch)

| Ingredient | Quantity |
|-----------|---------|
| Desi cow dung (fresh or dried) | 40 kg |
| Desi cow urine | 4 litres |
| Jaggery | 500 g |
| Besan (pulse flour) | 500 g |
| Forest/garden soil | 1 kg |

### Preparation Steps
1. Mix all dry ingredients together - cow dung, flour, soil.
2. Dissolve jaggery in cow urine, then pour over dry mix.
3. Mix thoroughly until all dry matter is slightly moist.
4. Form into loose heap or spread in thin layer.
5. Cover with jute bag/cloth. Maintain in shade.
6. **Ready in 2-3 days** (summer) or 4-5 days (winter).
7. Should smell earthy, slightly sweet - not foul.

### How to Apply
- Spread 50 kg evenly across 1 Vigha.
- Mix lightly into top 2 inches of soil before planting.
- For standing crops: apply in ring around plant at drip-line.
- Apply **before rain** or irrigate immediately after.

### Shelf Life
Up to **3 months** when stored dry. Keep away from direct sun and rain.

## 6. COMMON PREPARATION MISTAKES

| Mistake | Effect | Fix |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Using Jersey/HF cow dung | Low microbial count | Use only desi (indigenous) breed |
| Sealing container airtight | Kills aerobic microbes, fermentation fails | Always use breathable cloth cover |
| Using chlorinated tap water | Kills microbes before they multiply | Use well water or stored rainwater |
| Applying Jeevamrut in hot sun | Microbes die on leaf surface | Apply before 8 AM or after 5 PM |
| Leaving Jeevamrut beyond 3 days | Turns anaerobic, harmful to soil | Always prepare fresh batches |
| Not stirring Panchgavya daily | Uneven fermentation | Stir every day for all 30 days |
| Using old/rancid ghee in Panchgavya | Disrupts fermentation | Use freshly made desi ghee |
