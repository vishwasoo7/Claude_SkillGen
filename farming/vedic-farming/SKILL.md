## COW RESOURCE CONSTRAINTS

Your farm has **limited desi cow access**. You rely on the neighboring farming family.
This is the most common ZBNF implementation challenge. Here is how to manage it.

### Minimum Cow Resources Needed Per Full Season (3 Vigha)

| Preparation | Cow Dung Needed | Cow Urine Needed |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Jeevamrut (8 batches x 3 Vigha) | ~240 kg dung | ~120 litres urine |
| Beejamrut (2 sowing events) | ~30 kg dung | ~30 litres urine |
| Ghanjeevamrut (1 per season) | ~120 kg dung | ~12 litres urine |
| Panchgavya (1 batch) | ~9 kg dung | ~9 litres urine |
| **Total per season** | **~400 kg dung** | **~170 litres urine** |

One healthy desi cow produces 10-15 kg dung and 3-5 litres urine per day.
**One cow = your entire season's supply.** One arrangement solves everything.

### How to Source Without Owning a Cow

**Neighbor arrangement (your current path):**
Formalize with the neighboring farming family. One cow covers your 3 Vigha.
Offer crop share, small daily payment, or reciprocal labor exchange.

**Local gaushala:**
Junagadh and Keshod area gaushalas often give dung and urine free to natural farmers.
Gir cow gaushalas in the Taluka are the best source. Gir cow dung has highest Bacillus count.

**If supply is irregular -- prioritize in this order:**
1. Jeevamrut first (cannot substitute)
2. Beejamrut second (critical for each sowing)
3. Ghanjeevamrut (can substitute with vermicompost + wood ash if needed)
4. Panchgavya (can defer to Season 2 if dung is very scarce in Season 1)

**Preparations requiring NO cow dung (always makeable):**
- Agniastra (cow urine only)
- Brahmastra (plant leaves + cow urine)
- Dashaparni Ark (plant leaves + cow urine + cow dung -- but dung is small portion)

**Desi breed is mandatory:** Gir, Kankrej, or local desi only.
Jersey and HF cow products have 10x lower microbial counts. Do not substitute.

## FARM-SPECIFIC NOTES (Keshod, Rangpur Village)

- **Cow source:** Desi breeds only (Gir, Kankrej). Not Jersey / HF. See constraint section.
- **Water for preparations:** Use borewell water. Avoid chlorinated municipal water.
- **Shade:** Ferment all preparations in shade. Never direct sun.
- **Stirring:** Stir Jeevamrut clockwise 7-10 times before each use.
- **Containers:** Clay pots or food-grade plastic. Never aluminum or iron.
- **Kali Jeeri residue:** Do not burn after harvest. Spread as mulch -- medicinal compounds suppress soil nematodes.
- **Year 1 reality:** Accept 30-50% lower yield as soil recovers from chemical use. Yield climbs from Season 2. Do not compare to chemical farm output.
- **Saurashtra soil:** Loamy-clay compacts easily in summer heat. Mulching is non-negotiable at all times.
