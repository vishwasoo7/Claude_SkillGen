## 1. SAURASHTRA SOIL PROFILE - WHAT YOU'RE WORKING WITH

Rangpur village, Keshod Taluka falls in the Saurashtra region. Typical soil characteristics:

| Parameter | Typical Range | Implication |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| Soil type | Loamy-clay to medium black cotton | Retains moisture well; compacts in summer |
| pH | 7.5 - 8.5 (slightly alkaline) | Limit phosphorus availability; monitor |
| Organic Matter | Often 0.3 - 0.8% (low at start) | Priority: build OM through every cycle |
| Drainage | Moderate - waterlogging risk in depression areas | Raised beds or ridges help |
| Summer hardening | High - forms hard crust if exposed | Mulching is non-negotiable |
| Micronutrient deficiency | Common: Zinc, Boron, Sulfur | Address with organic amendments |

**Your primary soil goals (Phase 1):**
1. Raise organic matter above 1.5% within 3 seasons
2. Break summer hardpan - mulching + minimal tillage
3. Build microbial diversity - Jeevamrut + green manure + vermicompost
4. Improve water retention in root zone

## 2. MULCHING

**Purpose:** Retains soil moisture, suppresses weeds, moderates soil temperature, adds organic matter as it decomposes. Most important physical intervention in ZBNF.

### Best Mulch Materials for Keshod (in order of preference)

| Material | Availability | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Groundnut shell / husk | Excellent - abundant in Junagadh | Free or very low cost after harvest |
| Dried groundnut stalks | Excellent | Shred if possible |
| Dry leaves (any tree) | Good | Collect from farm boundaries |
| Paddy straw | Good if available | Sourced from neighboring farms |
| Bajra (pearl millet) stalks | Good | Available post-Kharif harvest |
| Sugarcane trash | Moderate | If available nearby |
| Dried grass / weeds | Good | Cut before seeding |

### Application

**How much per Vigha:**
- 2-3 inch deep layer across all open soil
- Approx. 500-800 kg dry material per Vigha (varies by material density)
- For tree crops / fruit trees: 6-inch deep ring around each tree, 1-foot radius

**When to apply:**
- Immediately after sowing (leave seed row exposed 1 inch)
- After each rain event (top up if washed thin)
- Before summer onset (March) - heaviest application of the year
- After harvest - cover all exposed soil immediately

**Critical rules:**
- Never leave soil bare - ever
- Keep mulch 2-3 inches away from plant stem (prevent rot)
- Wet mulch if it blows in wind (Saurashtra winds in summer)

## 3. GREEN MANURE CROPS

**Purpose:** Fixes atmospheric nitrogen, adds organic matter, breaks soil hardpan, provides habitat for beneficial insects.

### Best Green Manure Crops for Keshod

| Crop | When to Sow | Days to Incorporation | N Fixed per Vigha | Notes |
|------|------------|----------------------|-------------------|-------|
| **Dhaincha** (Sesbania) | May-June (pre-Kharif) | 40-50 days | 20-30 kg | Best for Saurashtra - tolerates alkaline soil |
| **Sunn Hemp** (Sanai) | June | 45-60 days | 25-35 kg | Fast-growing, deep root breaks compaction |
| **Cowpea** (Lobia) | June-July | 45 days | 15-20 kg | Can also harvest young pods for income |
| **Cluster bean** (Guar) | June-July | 50-60 days | 20 kg | Common in Junagadh, dual-purpose |
| **Horsegram** (Kulthi) | October (Rabi) | 50-60 days | 15 kg | Use in Rabi fallow beds |

### How to Incorporate
1. Sow broadcast or in lines across field
2. When in full flower (just before seeding) - this is peak nutrient content
3. Cut / mow the entire crop
4. Leave on surface 2-3 days to wilt
5. **Do not burn** - incorporate by shallow turning (2-4 inches) or leave as mulch-layer
6. Apply Jeevamrut immediately after incorporation (activates decomposition)
7. Wait 15-20 days before sowing main crop

### Per Vigha Seed Requirement
- Dhaincha: 4-5 kg seed
- Sunn Hemp: 8-10 kg seed
- Cowpea: 6-8 kg seed
- Guar: 8-10 kg seed

## 4. VERMICOMPOST

**Purpose:** Most concentrated organic amendment. Provides humus, beneficial microbes, enzymes, plant hormones, and slow-release nutrients. Especially valuable in alkaline Saurashtra soil.

### Making Vermicompost (On-Farm)

**Setup per 1 Vigha (annual requirement = 500 kg):**
- 2-3 vermi-beds (each 1m wide × 3m long × 30cm deep)
- Shade structure (thatched roof or shade net - critical in Gujarat heat)
- Eisenia foetida (red wriggler) or local earthworm species

**Inputs into vermi-bed (layered):**
1. Bottom layer: Dry straw/leaves (4 inches)
2. Middle layer: Cow dung (fresh or semi-dry) + kitchen waste + crop residue
3. Top: Thin layer of garden soil (inoculates with microbes)
4. Keep moist (like a wrung-out sponge) - spray water daily

**Timeline:**
- 45-60 days to first harvest
- Harvest when top 2 inches turn dark chocolate-brown and crumbly
- Sieve out worms, return to bed, bag the compost

### Application Rate per Vigha

| Crop Type | Vermicompost per Vigha |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Vegetables, intensive crops | 500-600 kg |
| Groundnut, field crops | 250-300 kg |
| Fruit trees (per tree) | 5-10 kg per tree |

**When to apply:**
- Pre-sowing soil preparation (mix into top 6 inches)
- Side-dressing at 30 days after germination
- Top-dressing around fruit trees before flowering

## 5. WOOD ASH

**Purpose:** Provides potassium and calcium. Raises pH slightly (good for acidic patches). Repels some surface insects (ants, slugs).

### Application per Vigha
- **Broadcast:** 50-100 kg per Vigha (broadcast and rake in lightly)
- **Ring application (trees):** 500g per tree around drip-line
- Best applied before irrigation or rain - washes into soil

**Caution:** Don't over-apply - Saurashtra soil is already alkaline (pH 7.5-8.5). Test soil first. Wood ash in excess worsens alkalinity. Use sparingly - once per season maximum.

---

## 6. ROCK PHOSPHATE

**Purpose:** Slow-release phosphorus source. Critical for flowering, fruiting, and root development. Organic and safe.

### Application per Vigha
- 50 kg per Vigha per season
- Mix into soil during pre-sowing preparation
- Most effective when mixed with vermicompost before application (microbes solubilize phosphorus)

**Where to source:** Agricultural input shops in Junagadh/Keshod. Also available from GSFC (Gujarat State Fertilizers & Chemicals) as organic product.

## 7. LIME APPLICATION (pH CORRECTION)

**Purpose:** Corrects very high pH if above 8.5. Provides calcium. Improves soil structure in heavy clay.

**First - test your pH.** Keshod soil often runs 7.5-8.5. If pH is below 8.5, lime is not needed.

| Soil pH | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Below 7.0 | Add lime: 50 kg/Vigha |
| 7.0 - 8.5 | No lime needed - normal Saurashtra range |
| Above 8.5 | Add sulfur: 20-25 kg/Vigha + gypsum: 50 kg/Vigha |

**Organic alternatives to correct alkalinity (pH >8.5):**
- **Gypsum (calcium sulfate):** 50 kg/Vigha - lowers pH, adds calcium and sulfur
- **Farm compost + green manure:** Long-term correction through organic acid production
- **Neem cake:** 50 kg/Vigha - also corrects pH and adds nitrogen
