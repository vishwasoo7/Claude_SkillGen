## 1. WHY LUNAR TIMING MATTERS IN ZBNF

In ZBNF and traditional Indian farming, the moon's gravitational pull affects groundwater levels, sap movement in plants, and soil microbial activity - similar to how it controls ocean tides.

**Key observations from traditional practice:**
- Water in soil rises toward the surface during Shukla Paksha (waxing moon) - better seed germination
- Purnima (full moon) maximizes soil moisture and microbial activity - ideal for Jeevamrut application
- Amavasya (new moon) - soil is dry and dormant - avoid sowing and transplanting
- Certain Nakshatras activate specific plant organs (roots, leaves, flowers, fruits)

**Practical benefit for your farm:** Aligning major activities with favorable Tithis reduces input waste and improves outcomes. It doesn't replace good soil management but amplifies it.

## 2. THE 30 TITHIS - QUICK REFERENCE

Each lunar month has 30 Tithis - 15 in **Shukla Paksha** (waxing, after Amavasya) and 15 in **Krishna Paksha** (waning, after Purnima).

| # | Tithi Name | Paksha | General Nature |
|---|-----------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Pratipada | Both | Moderate - safe for most activities |
| 2 | Dwitiya | Both | Good for sowing |
| 3 | Tritiya | Both | Good for sowing, transplanting |
| 4 | Chaturthi | Both | Avoid - inauspicious for planting |
| 5 | Panchami | Both | Excellent for sowing (Shukla) |
| 6 | Shashthi | Both | Good for leaf crops |
| 7 | Saptami | Both | Good for flowering crops |
| 8 | Ashtami | Both | Moderate |
| 9 | Navami | Both | Avoid - considered harmful for seeds |
| 10 | Dashami | Both | Good for root crops |
| 11 | Ekadashi | Both | **Excellent - one of the best for all activities** |
| 12 | Dwadashi | Both | Very good for Jeevamrut and soil prep |
| 13 | Trayodashi | Both | Good, especially Shukla |
| 14 | Chaturdashi | Both | Moderate to avoid (Krishna Chaturdashi especially) |
| 15S | Purnima | Shukla | **Peak Jeevamrut, microbial activity. Avoid sowing.** |
| 15K | Amavasya | Krishna | **Avoid all major activities** |

## 3. BEST TITHIS FOR EACH FARM ACTIVITY

### Sowing Seeds / Direct Planting
**Best:** Dwitiya, Tritiya, Panchami (Shukla Paksha), Ekadashi, Dwadashi
**Good:** Pratipada, Shashthi, Saptami (Shukla Paksha)
**Note:** Shukla Paksha (waxing moon) strongly preferred for sowing - soil moisture rising, germination rate higher

### Transplanting Seedlings
**Best:** Tritiya, Panchami, Ekadashi (Shukla Paksha)
**Good:** Dwitiya, Dwadashi
**Avoid:** Amavasya, Purnima, Chaturthi, Navami

### Applying Jeevamrut (Drench)
**Best:** Purnima (full moon - peak microbial activity), Ekadashi, Dwadashi
**Good:** Dashami, Trayodashi (Shukla)
**Note:** Applying Jeevamrut on Purnima amplifies microbial multiplication in soil. Many ZBNF farmers consider this the most important timing rule.

### Applying Beejamrut (Seed Treatment)
**Best:** 1 day before planned sowing. Use the Tithi favorable for sowing.
**Rule:** Treat seeds on Dwadashi → Sow on Trayodashi (Shukla Paksha) is a common sequence.

### Applying Panchgavya (Foliar)
**Best:** Ekadashi, Purnima, Dwadashi
**Good:** Panchami, Saptami (Shukla Paksha)

### Pest Spray Application (Neemastra, Dashaparni, etc.)
**Best:** Any day **except** Purnima and Amavasya
**Preferred:** Ashtami, Chaturdashi, Krishna Paksha generally
**Note:** Pest sprays are more effective during Krishna Paksha (waning moon) - pests are more active and exposed.

### Pruning / Cutting Plants
**Best:** Krishna Paksha (waning moon) - plant sap is lower, less stress on plant, wounds heal faster
**Avoid:** Purnima (maximum sap - heavy bleeding of wounds)

### Harvesting
**Best:** Krishna Paksha, especially Dashami to Chaturdashi
**Good:** Dwitiya, Tritiya (Krishna Paksha)
**Note:** Harvesting during waning moon = lower moisture in plant tissue = better post-harvest shelf life, less rotting

### Soil Preparation / Deep Tilling
**Best:** Amavasya or Krishna Paksha (soil is drier, easier to work)
**Note:** Deep tilling on Amavasya, then allow to rest 2-3 days before Jeevamrut application as soil activity ramps up in Shukla Paksha

### Applying Green Manure / Compost
**Best:** Purnima, Ekadashi, Dwadashi (Shukla Paksha)

## 4. TITHIS TO AVOID

| Tithi | What to Avoid | Why |
|-------|--------------|-----|
| **Amavasya** (New Moon) | Sowing, transplanting, Jeevamrut | Soil microbial activity at minimum |
| **Purnima** (Full Moon) | Sowing, pruning, harvesting | Excess sap, poor shelf life if harvested |
| **Chaturthi** | Planting new crops | Traditional inauspicious day |
| **Navami** | Seed sowing | Traditional - poor germination |
| **Krishna Chaturdashi** | Any new beginning | Most inauspicious in the fortnight |

## 5. NAKSHATRA GUIDANCE

Beyond Tithi, Nakshatras (lunar constellations) influence which plant organ is most active. This matters for what you apply and when.

| Nakshatra | Planet Organ Activated | Best Activity |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------|
| Rohini, Mrigashira, Ardra | Leaf / Stem | Apply foliar sprays (Panchgavya, Jeevamrut foliar) |
| Hasta, Chitra, Swati | Flower | Apply flowering stimulants, avoid pruning |
| Vishakha, Anuradha | Fruit | Harvest, apply potassium-rich inputs |
| Uttara Phalguni, Uttara Ashadha, Uttara Bhadrapada | Root | Apply Jeevamrut drench, root-zone treatments |
| Ashwini, Bharani, Krittika | Seed | Sow seeds, apply Beejamrut |

**How to use:** Cross-reference Tithi + Nakshatra before scheduling major operations. When both are favorable - strong outcome. When Tithi is good but Nakshatra is against (e.g., root Nakshatra on a leaf-spray day) - prioritize Tithi.

## 6. HOW TO FIND TODAY'S TITHI

**Best free resources:**
- **Drik Panchang** - https://www.drikpanchang.com - Enter location (Keshod, Gujarat) for daily Tithi, Nakshatra, Vara
- **Astrosage Panchang** - https://www.astrosage.com/panchang/
- Any printed Gujarat Panchang (available at local stationery shops)

**What to look for on the page:**
- **Tithi** - the lunar day number and name
- **Paksha** - Shukla (bright/waxing) or Krishna (dark/waning)
- **Nakshatra** - current lunar constellation
- **Sunrise/Sunset** - to time early morning applications correctly

## 7. PRACTICAL EXAMPLE - MONTHLY PLANNING (Kharif)

Say you are planning to sow groundnut in early July (Ashadha/Shravana month):

1. Open Drik Panchang, set location to Keshod
2. Find the next **Shukla Panchami or Ekadashi** in the first two weeks of July
3. Plan Beejamrut preparation **2 days before** that date
4. Sow on that Tithi
5. Plan first Jeevamrut drench on the **Purnima** of that month (approx. 2 weeks after sowing)
6. Plan Dashaparni Ark preventive spray on any **Ashtami** (Shukla or Krishna)
7. Schedule Panchgavya foliar on **Ekadashi** of the following fortnight

This simple pattern gives you a rhythm of 4-5 scheduled activities per month, all in harmony with lunar cycles.
