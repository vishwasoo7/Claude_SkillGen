## 1. WHAT CHEMICAL ABUSE DOES TO SOIL

When a farm runs on heavy synthetic inputs for multiple seasons, it leaves behind:

| Problem | What It Means Practically |
|---------|--------------------------|
| Dead microbial community | Soil cannot cycle nutrients without Jeevamrut stimulation |
| Compacted structure | Roots struggle, water sits on surface instead of infiltrating |
| Salt accumulation (EC rise) | Osmotic stress on plant roots - stunted germination |
| Herbicide residue | Some herbicides persist 6-24 months - interferes with germination |
| Disrupted fungi network (mycorrhizae) | Plants cannot access phosphorus even when it is present |
| pH drift | Often pushes pH above 8.5 in Saurashtra alkaline soil |
| Earthworm absence | No biological tillage happening - soil stays hard |

**The honest truth for Year 1:** Your first season is soil recovery, not maximum yield.
Accept 30-50% lower yield than a healthy natural farm. Do not compare to the
chemical yields of the previous farmer. Yield will climb from Season 2 onward.

## 2. YEAR 1 TRANSITION - WHAT TO EXPECT

### Timeline of Soil Recovery

```
Season 1 (Kharif 2026)
  Jeevamrut applications begin
  First earthworms appear (usually 45-60 days after first Jeevamrut)
  Salt levels begin dropping if EC was high
  Germination may be patchy - herbicide residue zones

Season 2 (Rabi 2026-27)
  Microbial population stabilizing
  Soil smell begins to change - earthy, less acidic/chemical
  Root depth improving
  Earthworm count increasing visibly

Season 3 (Kharif 2027)
  Soil structure visibly improved (hand test: crumbles, not cracks)
  Organic matter rising in lab test
  Germination rate normalizing
  Yield approaching healthy natural farm baseline
```

## 3. DETOX PROTOCOL - SEASON-BY-SEASON

### Pre-Kharif 2026 (April-May - Your Starting Point)

This is the critical window. Kali Jeeri is being harvested. Act immediately after harvest.

**Step 1 - Deep exposure (1 week after harvest)**
- Leave all crop residue on soil surface - do not burn
- Do one shallow tilling pass (4-6 inches only) to expose compacted sub-layer to sun
- Allow to sun-bake for 7-10 days - UV kills some pathogens and breaks down surface chemical residue

**Step 2 - First Jeevamrut shock dose (immediately after sun exposure)**
- Apply Jeevamrut at double dose: 400 litres per Vigha (instead of 200)
- This is the single most important action you will take in Year 1
- Reintroduces microbial life that chemicals wiped out
- Do this even before deciding what to grow

**Step 3 - Cover crop sowing (within 2 weeks of Jeevamrut)**
- Sow Dhaincha (Sesbania bispinosa) broadcast at 5 kg per Vigha
- If Dhaincha seeds unavailable, use Sunn Hemp or Cowpea
- Cover crop fixes nitrogen, breaks compaction with deep roots, adds bulk organic matter
- See Section 5 for full cover crop guide

**Step 4 - Green manure incorporation (40-45 days after sowing)**
- Cut cover crop at flowering stage
- Leave wilted 2-3 days on surface
- Incorporate with shallow turning (4 inches)
- Apply Jeevamrut immediately (200L/Vigha) to activate decomposition

**Step 5 - 15-day rest before main crop sowing**
- Do not rush to sow. Let the green manure break down.
- Apply second Jeevamrut dose on Day 10 of rest period

### Kharif 2026 (Main Crop Season)

- Apply Jeevamrut every 15 days without fail - this is the core intervention
- Mulch entire field heavily (600-800 kg/Vigha) - reduces herbicide volatilization from soil
- Expect some germination irregularities in patches - that is herbicide residue zones
- Do NOT panic and apply chemicals - note the patches, apply extra Jeevamrut in those zones
- Sow cover crop on any bare/failed patches rather than leaving soil exposed

### Rabi 2026-27 (Second Season)

- Soil should show first clear signs of recovery by now
- Reduce Jeevamrut to every 21 days (microbiome stabilizing)
- Get a lab test at the end of Rabi - compare EC, OC, and pH to baseline
- If EC is still high (above 1.5 dS/m): apply gypsum 50 kg/Vigha pre-season
- Introduce vermicompost this season (250 kg/Vigha) - adds humus layer

## 4. BIOREMEDIATION INPUTS - ORGANIC DETOX TOOLS

These specific inputs have documented bioremediation effects on chemically degraded soil.

### Jeevamrut (Primary Tool - Already in Your Arsenal)
**Why it detoxes:** Bacillus and Pseudomonas species in Jeevamrut break down organochlorine
pesticide residues and neutralize excess fertilizer salts through ion exchange.
**Detox-specific protocol:** Double dose for first 3 applications (400L/Vigha each time).
Return to standard dose (200L/Vigha) from 4th application onward.

### Neem Cake
**Why it helps:** Contains azadirachtin, which inhibits nematode reproduction and also
encourages beneficial fungi. Adds slow-release nitrogen as it decomposes.
**Application:** 50 kg per Vigha. Broadcast and incorporate into top 3 inches before sowing.
**Source:** Junagadh APMC agricultural input shops. Very affordable.

### Humic Acid (from Vermicompost)
**Why it helps:** Humic acids chelate (bind and neutralize) heavy metal residues and
synthetic fertilizer salts, making them less bioavailable.
**Application:** 250 kg vermicompost per Vigha worked into pre-sowing soil. If bought
vermicompost is available before your on-farm beds are producing, source from
KVK Junagadh or organic input suppliers in Keshod.

### Wood Vinegar (Kashtha Saar / Pyroligneous Acid)
**Why it helps:** Promotes beneficial soil fungi, inhibits soil-borne pathogens, and
accelerates breakdown of chemical residues. Less known but highly effective.
**How to get it:** Produced as a byproduct when making biochar. If any village-level
biochar production exists nearby, collect the condensate.
**Application:** Dilute 1:500 with water (2 mL per litre). Apply as soil drench
once per season alongside Jeevamrut.

### Gypsum (Calcium Sulfate)
**Why it helps:** Displaces sodium from soil exchange sites (common in chemically
abused alkaline Saurashtra soil), improving drainage and reducing EC.
**Application:** 50 kg per Vigha. Broadcast and irrigate in. Apply once per season
for first 2 years only.

## 5. COVER CROPS FOR SOIL HEALING

These are not income crops - they are soil medicine. Do not skip them in Year 1.

| Cover Crop | Why It Heals Chemically Abused Soil | Sow When | Seed/Vigha |
|------------|------------------------------------|-----------|-----------:|
| **Dhaincha** (Sesbania) | Deepest roots (4-6 ft) break compaction. Tolerates waterlogging and high pH. Fixes 25-30 kg N/Vigha. Best for Saurashtra. | May-June | 5 kg |
| **Sunn Hemp** (Sanai) | Very fast biomass. Roots release organic acids that help mobilize locked phosphorus from fertilizer residue. | June | 8-10 kg |
| **Cowpea** (Lobia) | Lighter crop - can also harvest early pods for income. Fixes 15 kg N. Tolerates partial salinity. | June-July | 6-8 kg |
| **Cluster Bean** (Guar) | Guar gum root exudates improve soil aggregation. Common in Junagadh - easy to source seed. | June-July | 8 kg |

**Critical rule:** Incorporate cover crop at flowering - before it sets seed.
After incorporation, the above-ground biomass feeds microbes. The roots
decompose slowly and leave channels for water and air.

## 6. WHAT NOT TO DO IN YEAR 1

| Temptation | Why to Resist |
|-----------|--------------|
| Apply organic certified inputs at high doses to compensate for low yield | Adds salt load on already-stressed soil. Patience is the input. |
| Burn crop residue | Destroys organic matter and microbial inoculum at surface. Always chop and spread instead. |
| Deep till to "loosen" compacted soil | Destroys remaining fungal networks. Use cover crop roots for deep loosening instead. |
| Stop Jeevamrut if no visible improvement after 2 applications | Soil recovery is not visible until Month 2-3. Trust the process. |
| Compare yield to chemical farm output | Chemical yields are borrowed from future soil health. You are building real fertility. |
| Apply any synthetic pesticide for pest outbreak | Resets the microbial work done. Use ZBNF pest remedies only - see `02-pest-remedies.md`. |

## 7. PROGRESS INDICATORS - HOW TO KNOW IT IS WORKING

Check these monthly. Record each in your farm journal.

| Indicator | How to Check | Healthy Sign |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Earthworm count** | Dig 1 sq. ft, 6 inches deep. Count worms. | 5+ worms/sq. ft by Season 2 |
| **Soil smell** | Pick up a handful and smell it | Earthy, pleasant (like forest floor) - not sour or chemical |
| **Soil crumble** | Press moist soil ball, release | Crumbles when released - not sticky or rock-hard |
| **Water infiltration** | Pour 1 litre in one spot, time it | Absorbs in under 2 minutes |
| **EC (lab test)** | End of each season | Dropping toward 0.75 dS/m or below |
| **OC% (lab test)** | Once per year minimum | Rising above 0.5%, heading toward 1.0% |
| **Germination rate** | Count germinated vs. sown seeds | Above 80% by Season 2 |
| **Root depth** | Uproot a plant, measure root length | Deeper roots each season |

## 8. REDUCED-EXPECTATION CROPS FOR YEAR 1

Some crops tolerate chemical residue better than others and are better suited
for a transition farm's first season.

| Crop | Why Suitable for Year 1 | Kharif or Rabi |
|------|------------------------|---------------|
| **Bajra** (Pearl Millet) | Extremely hardy. Deep roots help break compaction. Low input needs. | Kharif |
| **Sesame** (Til) | Tolerates poor soil. Light crop. Low irrigation demand. | Kharif |
| **Cowpea** (Lobia) | Fixes nitrogen while also producing a harvest. | Kharif |
| **Cluster Bean** (Guar) | Low input, common in Junagadh, market at APMC. | Kharif |
| **Cumin** (Jeera) | Light root zone. Rabi crop. High APMC value per kg. | Rabi |
| **Methi** (Fenugreek) | Very tolerant. Short duration (60-70 days). Locally marketable. | Rabi |

**Avoid in Year 1:** Groundnut (sensitive to residue, high input needs), Cotton
(needs very healthy soil biology), Wheat (high water demand).

**After Kali Jeeri harvest (your current situation):** The Kali Jeeri itself is
a good sign - it is a hardy crop that survived the chemical-to-natural transition
of the field. Its presence means the soil is not completely dead. You have a
working foundation to build on.
