# PGS-India Certification Roadmap
## Keshod Farm -- Day 1 Logging to First Certificate

PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System) is the right first certification for your
demonstration farm. It is peer-reviewed, costs under Rs 2,000 for the entire process,
and is legally recognized at all APMCs and government schemes.

This skill is the step-by-step action plan. For background on what PGS is, how it
compares to NPOP and third-party certification, and Farm Calendar log entry formats,
read `agstack-farming` -> `references/10-apmc-pgs.md`.

## WHAT THE CERTIFICATE ENABLES

Completing this roadmap gives you a legally recognized PGS Scope Certificate that:
- Allows you to label and sell produce as "PGS Organic" at any APMC
- Qualifies your farm for government organic farming subsidy schemes
- Enables premium pricing: 20 to 40% above standard APMC rate
- Serves as the documentation foundation for INATrace traceability QR codes
- Establishes your demonstration farm's credibility with visitors and media
- Is the prerequisite for NPOP (national organic certification) if you pursue export markets later

## WHAT CAN GO WRONG -- AND HOW TO PREVENT IT

Three things fail PGS applications most often:

**1. Documentation gaps**
Weeks where no Farm Calendar entries were made look like weeks where synthetic inputs
may have been used. Reviewers cannot distinguish silence from hiding.
Prevention: log at minimum once per week during the growing season, even if nothing happened
("Week of [date]: no applications, crops growing normally, earthworm count X per sq ft").

**2. Group member with synthetic input history**
If any peer group member used synthetic inputs after the group's declaration date, the
entire group's certification is void for that season.
Prevention: choose group members carefully. Have an honest conversation before forming
the group. One bad member costs all members a full season.

**3. Missing baseline soil test**
PGS reviewers expect a soil test taken before or at the start of the conversion period.
Without it, there is no scientific starting point for the "transition to organic" claim.
Prevention: get a soil test done in April-May 2026 before any inputs are applied.
Cross-reference results with `soil-test-interpreter` skill.
