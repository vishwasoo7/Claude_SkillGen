## 1. PEER INSPECTION PROTOCOL

### What a Peer Farm Visit Actually Looks Like

A PGS peer inspection is an informal but documented visit between group members.
It is not an official audit by a government inspector. It is neighbors checking on
each other -- which is exactly what makes PGS efficient and low-cost.

A typical visit takes 60 to 90 minutes and covers:

**Section 1: Farm walk (30-45 min)**
The visiting member walks the entire field with the farmer:
- Look at crop condition: healthy, consistent growth expected from natural inputs
- Check for any synthetic input containers anywhere on or near the farm
  (fertilizer bags, pesticide bottles, chemical labels)
- Observe soil surface: mulch present, earthworms visible, no chemical burn patterns
- Check the borewell / irrigation area: no chemical mixing equipment
- Note anything unusual or inconsistent with what the farmer has declared

**Section 2: Record review (15-20 min)**
The visiting member reviews the Farm Calendar entries:
- Are entries present for all weeks of the active season?
- Do the inputs listed match what the farmer says they applied?
- Are input quantities reasonable for the area?
- Are there any entries that seem inconsistent with what was just observed?

**Section 3: Discussion and sign-off (10-15 min)**
- Visiting member notes any questions or concerns
- Farmer explains anything that seemed unusual
- If everything is consistent: visiting member signs the inspection record
- If a concern exists: it is noted in writing and the group facilitator is informed

### What the Visiting Member Is NOT Expected to Do
- Conduct a scientific test of soil or inputs
- Verify every number in the Farm Calendar
- Act as a government inspector or make official rulings
- Travel more than a reasonable distance (group members should be from the same area)

### What Makes an Inspection Fail

An inspection should be documented as "finding inconsistency" if:
- Synthetic input containers are found anywhere on the farm
- Crop condition shows clear signs of synthetic fertilizer use (unnaturally uniform,
  excessive green color inconsistent with natural inputs)
- Farm Calendar records are missing for extended periods during the active season
- The farmer cannot explain inputs that appear in records
- The physical farm does not match what is described in the farm declaration

If an inconsistency is found, the visiting member records it factually without accusation.
The group facilitator is notified. The group decides as a body whether certification
continues for that member.

## 4. NCOF PORTAL GUIDE

### First-Time Group Registration

1. Go to pgsindia-ncof.gov.in
2. Click "New Registration" -> "Local Group"
3. Enter: group name, state (Gujarat), district (Junagadh), taluka (Keshod)
4. Enter KVK Local Group registration number (from KVK visit)
5. Create group admin login (email + password)
6. Add all members using their Aadhaar numbers
7. Upload: group constitution PDF, KVK registration letter

### Annual Application Submission

1. Log in as group admin
2. Navigate to "Annual Application" -> "New Application"
3. Fill farm details for each member
4. Upload documents per member (see documentation checklist in ref 01)
5. Pay application fee online (credit/debit card or UPI)
6. Submit and note application reference number
7. Download acknowledgment -- keep this

### Checking Application Status

Log in and go to "Application Status" -> enter reference number.
Statuses:
- Under Review: normal -- wait
- Information Required: check portal messages, respond within the stated deadline
- Approved: certificate will be available for download
- Rejected: read reason carefully, address it, reapply next season

## 5. USING THE CERTIFICATE

### What the PGS Scope Certificate Contains

- Certificate number (unique, traceable)
- Farmer name and farm address
- Scope: which crops are certified and for which season
- Group name and Local Group number
- Issuing authority (NCOF)
- Valid period (usually one season / one year)
- KVK facilitator signature

### How to Use It

**At APMC:**
Present original certificate when asked by trader or commission agent.
You may label your produce bags as "PGS Organic" with your certificate number.
Traders at APMC Keshod and Junagadh are increasingly familiar with PGS --
show them the certificate and the premium conversation becomes much easier.

**For government schemes:**
Schemes that require organic certification accept PGS as valid.
Attach a certified copy (self-attested photocopy) with any scheme application.
Your Gram Sevak or Taluka Agriculture Officer can advise on which schemes
currently require or recognize PGS.

**For direct and premium buyers:**
Share your certificate number. Premium buyers (urban markets, organic stores,
institutional buyers) can verify the number on the NCOF portal.
Combine with your AgStack GeoID and INATrace QR code for maximum credibility:
the buyer can trace produce from field to purchase with a phone scan.

**For your demonstration farm:**
Display the certificate prominently. It is the first verifiable proof that your
natural farming is formally recognized -- not just a claim.

## 6. ANNUAL RENEWAL REQUIREMENTS

PGS Scope Certificate is valid for one season. Renewal is required every year.

### What Changes at Renewal (vs First Application)

Renewal is faster because the baseline is already established.

| Item | First Application | Renewal |
|---|---|---|
| Farm description document | Full new document | Update if land or area changed; otherwise carry forward |
| Baseline soil test | Required | Recommended but not always required -- KVK may request if OC% was very low |
| Land records | Required | Required only if changed |
| Peer inspection records | Both Kharif + Rabi required | At minimum one per season |
| Farm Calendar log | Full Kharif + Rabi | Full current season |
| Application fee | Rs 500-2,000 | Same or reduced (confirm with KVK) |

### Annual Renewal Timeline

| Month | Action |
|---|---|
| April (start of new season) | Update input stock declaration, log opening season declaration |
| July-October (Kharif) | Maintain weekly logs, peer inspection during season |
| October | Kharif harvest log + season summary |
| November | Start Rabi logs |
| January | Peer inspection for Rabi season |
| February | Compile renewal documentation package |
| February-March | Submit renewal application on NCOF portal |
| March-April | Receive renewed certificate |

### What Can Cause Renewal to Be Denied

- Farm Calendar records show gaps exceeding 3 to 4 weeks during active season
- New soil test shows EC or synthetic chemical markers inconsistent with organic claim
- Peer inspection record missing for either season
- Group member found using synthetic inputs in the previous year
- Change in land area or survey number not updated with KVK before renewal

## 7. HANDLING PROBLEMS AFTER CERTIFICATION

### If a Group Member Uses Synthetic Inputs

Procedure:
1. The member discloses this to the group facilitator (honesty is the correct path)
2. The group holds an emergency meeting
3. The member is removed from the group for that season
4. Their farm is excluded from the group's PGS scope for that certificate period
5. The remaining group members' certificates are unaffected if they had clean records
6. The removed member can re-apply to join a group (this one or another) in the next season
   after a full clean season with documentation

**Never conceal a group member's violation.** If NCOF discovers a concealed violation,
the entire group loses certification -- not just the offending member.

### If Your Farm Is Spot-Checked by NCOF or KVK

Occasionally NCOF or KVK conducts surprise physical inspections, especially in the
first 1 to 2 years of a new group.

What to have ready at all times:
- Farm Calendar printout or phone access (up to date)
- All signed peer inspection records in a physical file on the farm
- No synthetic input containers anywhere on or adjacent to the farm
- Ability to explain every input in your log

A clean surprise inspection strengthens your standing and accelerates future renewals.

### If Your Application Is Rejected

Read the rejection reason carefully from the NCOF portal.
The most common reasons and responses:

| Rejection Reason | Response |
|---|---|
| Incomplete documentation | Identify missing item, add it, resubmit in the next season |
| Farm Calendar gaps | Cannot retroactively fix; maintain better records next season |
| Peer inspection record unsigned or informal | Use the formal template from Section 1 this file |
| Land record mismatch (survey number, area) | Verify 7/12 Utara, update at KVK, resubmit |
| Application fee payment not confirmed | Check portal payment status, contact NCOF helpline |

One rejection does not bar future applications. Document the lesson and reapply.
Most first-time groups that fail get certified on the second attempt.
