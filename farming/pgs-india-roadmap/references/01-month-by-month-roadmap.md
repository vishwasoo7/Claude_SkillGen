## PHASE A -- FOUNDATION (April to June 2026)

This phase runs for approximately 8 weeks after Kali Jeeri harvest.
It is the most important phase. Everything else depends on getting this right.

### Week 1-2: Baseline Documentation (April, immediately after harvest)

**Action 1: Soil test**
- Collect soil samples from 5 to 7 spots across all 3 Vigha (0 to 6 inch depth)
- Send to: GSFC soil testing lab (Junagadh), or KVK Junagadh, or any accredited lab
- Request: full 12-parameter test (pH, EC, OC, N, P, K, Zn, Fe, Mn, Cu, B, S)
- Keep the original lab report -- it is a PGS document
- Enter results into Farm Calendar as baseline observation
- Cross-reference with `soil-test-interpreter` skill for organic amendment prescription
- Why this matters: proves the "before" state; shows transition from chemical use

**Action 2: Farm description document**
Write a one-page farm description covering:
- Your name, address, Aadhaar number
- Survey number and land record reference (7/12 Utara)
- Total area: 3 Vigha (state as approx. 1.2 acres or 0.49 hectares)
- Soil type: alkaline loamy-clay, pH approximately 8.0-8.3 (from test)
- Water source: electric submersible borewell, grid power, 500m from field
- Previous crop history: Kali Jeeri, previous farmer used synthetic fertilizers and pesticides
- Transition start date: [date of Kali Jeeri harvest]
- Intended crops: Bajra, Sesame, Cowpea, Cluster Bean (Kharif); Cumin, Methi (Rabi)
- Statement: "No synthetic fertilizers, herbicides, or pesticides have been or will be
  used on this farm from [transition start date] onward."
- Sign and date this document. Keep two copies.

**Action 3: Input stock declaration**
List everything currently stored on or near the farm:
- Cow dung (quantity, source -- desi cow, which neighbor/source)
- Cow urine (quantity, source)
- Neem leaves or neem cake (if any)
- Any other organic inputs
- Explicit statement: no synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, or herbicides in stock
- Sign and date. Keep with farm description document.

**Action 4: AgStack Farm Calendar setup**
- Register field at asset-registry.agstack.org (get your GeoID)
- Create farm record: name, address, area, soil type
- Create 3 parcels (one per Vigha, or combined) linked to your GeoID
- This GeoID is your permanent farm identity for all future documentation

### Week 3-4: Peer Group Formation (April to May)

**Action 5: Identify group members**
Minimum 5 farmers total (you + 4 others).
Each member must:
- Be farming with organic or natural methods, or committing to transition now
- Have land records in their name (or family member)
- Be willing to make a formal declaration of no synthetic inputs
- Be able to visit each other's farms for annual peer inspection

Where to find members:
- Neighboring farmers in Rangpur village -- ask directly
- KVK Junagadh: ask for list of natural farming practitioners in Keshod Taluka
- Gram Panchayat / Gram Sevak: knows who is doing ZBNF or organic farming nearby
- Subhash Palekar Natural Farming network in Gujarat: contact through district agriculture office

**Action 6: Group constitution meeting**
Hold a meeting with all 5+ members. Agree on and document:
- Group name (example: "Rangpur Prakrutik Kheti Juth" -- choose your own)
- Member list with names, Aadhaar numbers, village, survey number, area
- Group declaration: all members commit to no synthetic inputs from [date]
- Peer inspection schedule: who visits whom, when (once per season minimum)
- Consequence agreed: if any member is found using synthetic inputs, that member
  exits the group and loses certification for that season
- Everyone signs the group declaration document

**Action 7: Appoint group roles**
- Group Facilitator (usually the most active member -- this can be you)
- Record Keeper (maintains group file)
- These are informal roles -- no formal election needed

### Week 5-8: KVK Registration (May to June)

**Action 8: KVK Junagadh registration**

Visit KVK Junagadh in person with:
- Group constitution document (from Action 6)
- Each member's Aadhaar copy
- Each member's 7/12 Utara (land record)
- Group declaration signed by all members
- Your individual farm description document (from Action 2)
- Your baseline soil test report (from Action 1)

KVK Junagadh address and contact:
- Krishi Vigyan Kendra, Junagadh Agricultural University
  Moti Baug, Junagadh -- 362001, Gujarat
- Phone: 0285-2672072 (verify current number before visiting)
- Best time to visit: Tuesday or Thursday, 10am to 1pm (office hours)
- Ask for: PGS-India Local Group registration, organic farming section

What KVK will give you:
- Local Group registration number
- PGS member IDs for each member
- Guidance on NCOF portal registration
- Possibly: a KVK resource person assigned to your group as a facilitator

**Action 9: NCOF portal registration**

After KVK registration, register on the PGS-India national portal:
- URL: pgsindia-ncof.gov.in
- Create group account using Local Group registration number from KVK
- Upload: group constitution, member list, land records
- Each member creates individual login
- This portal is where the final certificate application is submitted

**Phase A Completion Checklist:**
- [ ] Baseline soil test completed and report in hand
- [ ] Farm description document signed and filed
- [ ] Input stock declaration signed and filed
- [ ] AgStack GeoID registered
- [ ] Peer group of 5+ members formed and constitution signed
- [ ] Group roles assigned
- [ ] KVK Junagadh registration completed -- Local Group number received
- [ ] NCOF portal accounts created for group and all members

## PHASE B -- KHARIF SEASON (July to October 2026)

This is the active logging phase. Quality of records here determines whether
your application succeeds.

### July: Sowing and Opening Season Log

**Action 10: Kharif season opening declaration**
On or before first sowing, create a Farm Calendar entry:
```
Type: Seasonal Declaration
Date: [sowing date]
Season: Kharif 2026
Parcels: [all GeoIDs]
Crops planned: [list with area per crop]
Statement: No synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, or herbicides used or planned.
Inputs to be used: Jeevamrut, Beejamrut, Neem cake, Vermicompost, cover crops.
Signed: [your name]
```

**Action 11: Beejamrut seed treatment log**
For every crop sown:
```
Type: Seed Treatment
Date: [date]
Crop: [name]
Seed quantity: X kg
Treatment: Beejamrut (cow dung 100g + cow urine 100ml + lime + water)
Notes: Batch prepared [date], dried in shade X hours before sowing
```

**Action 12: First Jeevamrut application log**
```
Type: Soil Amendment
Date: [date]
Parcel: [GeoID]
Input: Jeevamrut (double dose -- 400L/Vigha, first application)
Prepared: [batch preparation date]
Cow dung source: [neighbor/own]
Cow urine source: [neighbor/own]
Application method: Drip irrigation / surface drench
```

Log every subsequent Jeevamrut application the same way.

### August to September: Active Season Logging

**Action 13: Weekly minimum log entries**
Even weeks with no inputs require an entry:
```
Type: Observation
Date: [date of observation]
Crop: [crop name, growth stage]
Notes: [anything observed -- earthworm count, crop height, pest sightings,
        weather anomaly, soil moisture check result, anything notable]
No inputs applied this week.
```

This "nothing happened" log entry is as important as an input log.
Reviewers look for continuity. Gaps raise questions.

**Action 14: Every input application logged**
For every ZBNF preparation applied (Neemastra, Agniastra, Dashaparni Ark, etc.):
```
Type: Crop Protection
Date: [date]
Parcel: [GeoID]
Product: [preparation name]
Quantity: X litres
Method: Foliar spray / soil drench
Reason: [pest or disease observed, or preventive]
```

For every mulching event:
```
Type: Soil Amendment
Date: [date]
Input: Mulch (dry grass / crop residue)
Quantity: X kg per Vigha
Area covered: X sq ft
```

**Action 15: First peer inspection (August or September)**
One group member visits your farm during the Kharif season.
Schedule this in advance. It should happen while crops are visible and growing.

What the visiting member checks:
- No synthetic input containers on or near the farm
- Crop appearance consistent with natural farming (no signs of chemical burn, unusual uniformity)
- Your Farm Calendar records (they should review and sign off on what they see)
- Soil condition: smell, earthworm presence, mulch cover

After the visit:
```
Type: Peer Inspection Record
Date: [date of visit]
Visiting member: [name, member ID, group name]
Observations: [what they saw -- crop condition, soil condition, input storage]
Finding: No synthetic inputs observed. Records reviewed and consistent.
Signed: [visiting member signature or name]
```

This signed peer inspection record is required for your certificate application.

### October: Kharif Harvest Log

**Action 16: Harvest documentation**
For every harvest event:
```
Type: Harvest
Date: [date]
Parcel: [GeoID]
Crop: [name]
Quantity: X kg
Quality: A / B / mixed (describe any issues)
Sold at: APMC Keshod / Junagadh / local
Price received: Rs X per kg
Buyer/Agent: [name]
Receipt number: [APMC receipt if available]
```

**Action 17: Kharif season closing summary**
After last harvest, create a summary entry:
```
Type: Season Summary
Season: Kharif 2026
Total harvest by crop: [table]
Total inputs applied: [list with quantities]
Water use: [approximate irrigation events and volume]
Observations: [soil improvement signs, earthworm count trend, pest pressure]
Statement: No synthetic inputs used during Kharif 2026.
```

**Phase B Completion Checklist:**
- [ ] Kharif opening declaration logged
- [ ] All seed treatments logged
- [ ] Every Jeevamrut application logged with batch details
- [ ] Weekly observation entries -- no gaps longer than 10 days
- [ ] All ZBNF preparations logged
- [ ] Mulching events logged
- [ ] First peer inspection completed and record signed
- [ ] All harvests logged with APMC sale details
- [ ] Kharif season summary logged

## PHASE C -- RABI AND APPLICATION (November 2026 to March 2027)

### November: Rabi Season Opening

**Action 18: Rabi opening declaration**
Same format as Action 10, but for Rabi 2026-27:
```
Type: Seasonal Declaration
Date: [sowing date]
Season: Rabi 2026-27
Crops planned: Cumin X Vigha, Methi X Vigha
Statement: No synthetic inputs used or planned.
```

**Action 19: Continue logging through Rabi**
Same discipline as Phase B: every input, every observation, every week.
Cumin requires especially careful documentation -- it is your highest value crop
and premium buyers will scrutinize the record.

### December to January: Group Peer Review Preparation

**Action 20: Compile group evidence file**
Each member compiles their own documentation package:
- Farm description document (from Phase A)
- Baseline soil test report
- Complete Farm Calendar print-out or PDF export (all entries Kharif 2026 + Rabi so far)
- Peer inspection record (signed by visiting member)
- Input stock declaration updated with current season inputs used

If using AgStack Farm Calendar: use the Reporting Service to generate a PDF of all
operations for the season. This is your primary submission document.

**Action 21: Conduct group review meeting**
All members meet to review each other's documentation before submitting:
- Each member presents their season log summary
- Other members review for completeness and consistency
- Group facilitator compiles group-level summary: total area under PGS, all members
- Any gaps or inconsistencies corrected before submission

### February: Second Peer Inspection (Pre-Application)

**Action 22: Second peer farm visits**
Before application, a second round of farm visits confirms Rabi season status:
- One member visits each farm during active Rabi crop growth
- Same documentation format as Action 15
- This visit specifically checks: Rabi crop appears consistent with stated inputs,
  no synthetic input containers present, records are current

### February to March: Certificate Application

**Action 23: NCOF portal submission**
Log in to pgsindia-ncof.gov.in with your group account.

Documents to upload:
- [ ] Group constitution and member list
- [ ] All land records (7/12 Utara) for all members
- [ ] All Aadhaar copies
- [ ] Baseline soil test reports (all members)
- [ ] Farm Calendar PDF -- full Kharif 2026 + Rabi 2026-27 operations log
- [ ] Both peer inspection records (Kharif + Rabi visits)
- [ ] Input stock declarations
- [ ] Season summary entries
- [ ] KVK Local Group registration number and letter

Application fee: Rs 500 to 2,000 (varies by state; Gujarat fee -- confirm with KVK at
time of application as fees may be revised)

**Action 24: KVK submission (parallel)**
Submit a physical copy of the same package to KVK Junagadh.
KVK acts as a facilitating body and can expedite if there are portal delays.
Keep acknowledgment receipt from KVK.

**Action 25: Follow up**
PGS-India processing time: 4 to 12 weeks after complete submission.
If no response after 8 weeks, contact KVK Junagadh for status.
Certificate is issued as a "PGS Scope Certificate" covering specific crops for one season.

**Phase C Completion Checklist:**
- [ ] Rabi opening declaration logged
- [ ] Rabi season logs maintained weekly through harvest
- [ ] Each member's documentation package compiled
- [ ] Group review meeting conducted
- [ ] Second peer inspection completed and record signed
- [ ] NCOF portal submission complete -- all documents uploaded
- [ ] KVK physical submission with acknowledgment receipt
- [ ] Follow-up contact logged

## IF YOU MISS A STEP -- RECOVERY OPTIONS

| What was missed | Can you recover? | How |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline soil test not done before first input | Partially -- get one now | Test soil before next season. Acknowledge in farm description that test was taken [X months] after transition start. Explain why. |
| Several weeks of Farm Calendar gaps | Depends on gap length | If under 3 weeks: write a catch-up entry explaining no inputs were applied during that period. If over 4 weeks: document gap honestly, add extra observation entries going forward, be transparent with KVK. |
| Peer group has fewer than 5 members | Yes | Find additional members before application. KVK can sometimes help with introductions to other natural farmers. |
| KVK registration not done yet | Yes if before application | Go immediately. Do not apply to NCOF before KVK registration is complete. |
| Group member admitted to synthetic input use | Yes for others | That member must exit the group and their farm is excluded from this season's certificate. The group can continue with remaining members (minimum 5 must remain). |
| Application rejected by NCOF | Yes | Request written reason. Usually documentation gap or inconsistency. Address the specific issue and reapply next season. One rejection does not prevent future certification. |
