## 1. WHY pH MUST BE FIXED FIRST

Every nutrient correction assumes pH is in the workable range (6.5-8.0).
Outside that band, inputs are chemically locked and money is wasted.

**Concrete example from Keshod soil:**
- pH 8.4 → apply 50 kg rock phosphate → P stays locked in soil. Zero plant uptake.
- pH 8.4 → apply gypsum → pH drops to 7.9 → apply 50 kg rock phosphate → P becomes available.

**What pH correction actually does:**
Lowering pH by even 0.3-0.5 units in alkaline soil unlocks P, Zn, Fe, and B simultaneously --
making all those nutrients suddenly available without adding any new inputs.
It is often the single highest-leverage action on an alkaline Gujarat farm.

**Rule without exception:** Correct pH before correcting nutrients.

## 2. pH 7.5-8.5 -- SAURASHTRA NORMAL RANGE

**Do not try to aggressively "fix" this range -- it is normal here.**
Most Keshod farms fall here. Management strategy, not emergency correction.

### What to do:

| Action | Per Vigha | Timing | Effect on pH |
|--------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Jeevamrut drench | 200 litres | Every 15 days | Produces organic acids -- gentle long-term buffering |
| Vermicompost | 250-400 kg | Pre-sowing | Humic acids gently lower pH over seasons |
| Green manure incorporation | 5 kg seed crop | Pre-season | Organic acid release during decomposition |
| Mulching | 600 kg | Continuous | Prevents hardpan that worsens alkalinity |
| Gypsum (light dose if pH 8.0-8.5) | 25 kg | Pre-sowing | Preventive -- keep pH from rising further |

**Expected OC-driven pH improvement:**
Each 0.1% rise in OC typically reduces pH by approximately 0.1-0.2 units over time.
Raising OC from 0.5% → 1.0% can bring pH from 8.3 down to 7.9-8.1 over 3 seasons.
This is a gradual, stable, sustainable improvement -- not a quick chemical fix.

## 3. pH > 8.5 -- ACTIVE CORRECTION NEEDED

At pH above 8.5 you must act before the season starts.
Applying nutrients at this pH is wasteful -- correct first.

### Primary Corrections (apply together for best result):

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Mechanism |
|-------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O) | 50 kg | Pre-sowing -- rake 4 inches deep | Displaces sodium from clay particles; adds Ca and S; directly lowers pH |
| Elemental sulphur | 20-25 kg | Pre-sowing -- mix into soil | Oxidizes to sulphuric acid through microbial activity → lowers pH |
| Vermicompost | 400-500 kg | Pre-sowing | Humic + fulvic acid production; long-term pH buffer |
| Green manure | 5 kg seed → 45-day crop → incorporate | Pre-season | Organic acid release on decomposition |
| Jeevamrut drench | 200 litres fortnightly | Throughout season | Continuous organic acid production by microbes |

**⚠️ Note on elemental sulphur:** Requires active soil microbes to oxidize into acid.
Apply Jeevamrut at the same time -- microbes in Jeevamrut do the oxidation work.
Elemental sulphur alone in dead soil will not lower pH.

### What to Avoid at pH > 8.5:
- Do not apply wood ash (raises pH further)
- Do not apply lime or calcium carbonate
- Do not apply high-sodium inputs (some industrial composts)
- Minimize bore well irrigation if water EC is high -- salt water worsens alkalinity

## 4. pH < 6.5 -- ACIDITY (RARE IN SAURASHTRA)

Included for completeness. Very unlikely in Keshod area.
May appear in low-lying areas with heavy rainfall or acidic parent rock.

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Agricultural lime (CaCO₃) | 50 kg | Pre-sowing | Raises pH -- standard correction |
| Dolomite lime (CaMg(CO₃)₂) | 50 kg | Pre-sowing | Adds Mg along with pH correction |
| Wood ash | 50 kg | Pre-sowing | Mild alkalizer -- also adds K |
| Vermicompost | 250 kg | Pre-sowing | Buffers pH in both directions |

**Target pH when correcting acidity:** 6.5-7.0. Do not over-lime to reach 8.0.
Re-test after one full season before applying another lime dose.

## 5. EC -- SALINITY MANAGEMENT

### EC 0.75-2.0 dS/m -- Caution Zone

| Action | Method / Quantity |
|--------|-----------------|
| Reduce manure inputs temporarily | Stop poultry manure, reduce cow dung if applied in excess |
| Deep irrigation leaching | Apply 2× normal irrigation volume once before sowing to flush salts below root zone |
| Gypsum | 25 kg per Vigha -- improves soil structure and drainage for leaching |
| Mulching | 600 kg -- reduces salt capillary rise to surface in summer |
| Test irrigation water | Get bore well water EC tested separately |

### EC > 2.0 dS/m -- Active Salinity Problem

| Action | Method / Quantity |
|--------|-----------------|
| Heavy leaching | 3-4× normal irrigation volume to push salts down before sowing |
| Gypsum | 50 kg per Vigha -- replaces sodium (causes salinity) with calcium |
| Raised bed planting | Build beds with drainage furrows -- salt migrates to furrows, away from root zone |
| Crop selection | Choose salt-tolerant crops: barley, cotton, dates, beet |
| Eliminate high-salt inputs | Stop poultry manure, avoid saline water |

### Testing irrigation water EC
If EC >1.5 dS/m in bore well water -- the water is contributing salt every time you irrigate.
Options: blend with lower-EC water, switch source, or reduce frequency + irrigate deeply.
Contact local water testing at GSFC or KVK Junagadh to test your bore well water.

## 6. NUTRIENT AVAILABILITY VS pH CHART

| Nutrient | Peak Availability pH | Starts Locking | Fully Locked At |
|----------|---------------------|---------------|----------------|
| Nitrogen (N) | 6.0-8.0 | <5.0 and >8.5 | <4.0 and >9.0 |
| Phosphorus (P) | 6.5-7.5 | >7.5 | >8.5 🚨 |
| Potassium (K) | 6.0-8.5 | <4.5 | <4.0 |
| Zinc (Zn) | 5.0-7.0 | >7.5 | >8.0 🚨 |
| Iron (Fe) | 4.5-6.5 | >7.0 | >8.0 🚨 |
| Boron (B) | 5.0-7.0 | >8.0 | >8.5 🚨 |
| Sulphur (S) | 6.0-8.0 | <4.0 only | Rare |
| Manganese (Mn) | 5.0-6.5 | >7.5 | >8.5 |
| Copper (Cu) | 5.0-7.0 | >7.5 | >8.5 |

**Key Saurashtra takeaway:** At pH 7.8-8.3 (typical range), P, Zn, Fe, B, and Mn
are all partially to fully locked. Every 0.1 pH unit you reduce releases more of these
nutrients to plants -- without adding a single new input. This is why pH correction
gives the best return on effort of any single soil management action.

## 7. THREE-SEASON pH CORRECTION PLAN

For farms starting at pH 8.5+ with OC <0.5%:

### Season 1 -- Foundation
- Gypsum: 50 kg per Vigha pre-sowing
- Elemental sulphur: 20 kg per Vigha (mixed in with Jeevamrut application)
- Green manure: Dhaincha 5 kg seed → incorporate at 45 days
- Vermicompost: 400 kg per Vigha pre-sowing
- Jeevamrut drench: every 15 days through season
- Mulch: 600 kg immediately after sowing
- Expected pH movement: 8.5 → ~8.1-8.2

### Season 2 -- Build
- Gypsum: 25 kg per Vigha (reduce dose as pH improves)
- Vermicompost: 300 kg
- Jeevamrut: every 15 days
- Green manure: rotational
- Expected pH movement: ~8.1 → ~7.8-8.0

### Season 3 -- Stabilize
- No gypsum needed if pH now below 8.0
- Vermicompost: 250 kg maintenance
- Jeevamrut: monthly
- OC should now be approaching 0.75-1.0% -- self-buffering begins
- Expected pH: stable at 7.8-8.0 -- workable for all Saurashtra crops
