## 1. pH

pH measures acidity or alkalinity on a scale of 0-14.
**7.0 = neutral. Below 7 = acidic. Above 7 = alkaline.**

| pH Range | Classification | Saurashtra Context |
|----------|---------------|-------------------|
| <5.5 | Strongly Acidic | Extremely rare in Keshod area |
| 5.5-6.5 | Acidic | Uncommon -- some coastal plots |
| 6.5-7.5 | Neutral ✅ Ideal | Rare -- most farms are above this |
| 7.5-8.0 | Mildly Alkaline | **Very common in Keshod -- normal, manage with OC** |
| 8.0-8.5 | Moderately Alkaline | **Common -- gypsum + OC building** |
| >8.5 | Strongly Alkaline 🚨 | Active correction needed before any nutrient input |

**What pH controls:** At pH >8.0, phosphorus, zinc, iron, and manganese become
chemically locked -- even when present in soil, plants cannot absorb them.
This is why Saurashtra farms show P and Zn deficiency even when lab values look "medium."
Fixing pH unlocks nutrients already in soil -- often more effective than adding new inputs.

## 2. EC -- ELECTRICAL CONDUCTIVITY

EC measures total dissolved salts. Unit: dS/m (same as mmho/cm).

| EC (dS/m) | Classification | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| <0.25 | Very Low | Fine -- no concern |
| 0.25-0.75 | Low -- Optimal ✅ | Ideal for all crops |
| 0.75-2.0 | Medium -- Caution | Monitor; some salt-sensitive crops affected |
| 2.0-4.0 | High 🚨 | Yield reduction likely; leach + gypsum |
| >4.0 | Very High 🚨 | Severe -- only salt-tolerant crops viable |

**Common cause in Gujarat:** Brackish bore well irrigation, excess manure, or
sea-proximity in coastal Saurashtra. Always test irrigation water EC separately.
If irrigation water EC >1.5 dS/m -- water source is contributing to the problem.

## 3. ORGANIC CARBON AND ORGANIC MATTER

Labs report either OC% or OM%. They are related:
**OM% = OC% × 1.724** (Van Bemmelen factor -- standard globally)

If your report shows OM%: OC% = OM% ÷ 1.724

| OC % | OM % | Classification | Meaning for Your Farm |
|------|------|---------------|----------------------|
| <0.5 | <0.86 | Low 🚨 Critical | Soil biologically depleted -- top priority |
| 0.5-0.75 | 0.86-1.29 | Medium-Low ⚠️ | Active improvement needed |
| 0.75-1.0 | 1.29-1.72 | Medium | Acceptable -- keep building |
| 1.0-1.5 | 1.72-2.58 | Good ✅ | Healthy -- maintain |
| >1.5 | >2.58 | Excellent 🌟 | Demonstration farm target level |

**Your farm target:** OC >1.0% (OM >1.72%) within 3 Kharif-Rabi cycles.
Each consistent Jeevamrut season adds approximately 0.03-0.06% OC.

## 4. MACRONUTRIENTS

### Available Nitrogen (N)
Labs typically report as kg/ha. Divide by 2.47 to get per Vigha equivalent.

| N (kg/ha) | Per Vigha equiv. | Status |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| <280 | <113 kg | Low ⚠️ |
| 280-560 | 113-227 kg | Medium ✅ |
| >560 | >227 kg | High |

**Note:** "Available N" (what plants absorb) ≠ "Total N" (everything in soil).
Always confirm your report shows Available N, not Total N.

### Available Phosphorus (P)
**Important:** Many Indian labs report P as P₂O₅ (phosphorus pentoxide), not elemental P.
P₂O₅ ÷ 2.29 = elemental P. Confirm with your lab which form they report.

| P (kg/ha) | Per Vigha equiv. | Status |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| <10 | <4 kg | Low ⚠️ |
| 10-25 | 4-10 kg | Medium ✅ |
| >25 | >10 kg | High |

**Saurashtra caution:** At pH >8.0, treat P as functionally deficient even if medium
on the report -- alkaline chemistry locks P from plant uptake.

### Available Potassium (K)
**Note:** Labs may report K or K₂O. K₂O ÷ 1.2 = elemental K.

| K (kg/ha) | Per Vigha equiv. | Status |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| <108 | <44 kg | Low ⚠️ |
| 108-280 | 44-113 kg | Medium ✅ |
| >280 | >113 kg | High |

**Saurashtra note:** K is usually Medium-High in Junagadh area soils.
True K deficiency is uncommon here. Verify before applying K inputs.

## 5. MICRONUTRIENTS

All reported in mg/kg (= ppm -- same unit, no conversion needed).

| Micronutrient | Deficient 🚨 | Sufficient ✅ | High | Priority in Saurashtra |
|---------------|------------|-------------|------|----------------------|
| Zinc (Zn) | <0.6 | 0.6-1.2 | >1.2 | 🔴 Very high -- endemic deficiency |
| Iron (Fe) | <4.5 | 4.5-9.0 | >9.0 | 🟡 Locked at high pH |
| Manganese (Mn) | <2.0 | 2.0-5.0 | >5.0 | 🟢 Less common |
| Copper (Cu) | <0.2 | 0.2-0.5 | >0.5 | 🟢 Rarely deficient |
| Boron (B) | <0.5 | 0.5-1.0 | >1.0 | 🔴 High for groundnut, cotton |
| Sulphur (S) | <10 | 10-20 | >20 | 🟡 Common after oilseed cropping |

**Fix priority for Keshod:** Zn → B → S → Fe → Mn → Cu

## 6. UNIT CONVERSION TABLE

| From | To | Operation |
|------|----|-----------|
| kg/ha | kg per Vigha | ÷ 2.47 |
| kg per Vigha | kg/ha | × 2.47 |
| OC% | OM% | × 1.724 |
| OM% | OC% | ÷ 1.724 |
| P₂O₅ (kg/ha) | Elemental P (kg/ha) | ÷ 2.29 |
| K₂O (kg/ha) | Elemental K (kg/ha) | ÷ 1.2 |
| mg/kg | ppm | 1:1 (identical) |
| dS/m | mmho/cm | 1:1 (identical) |
| μS/cm | dS/m | ÷ 1000 |
| meq/100g (K) | mg/kg | × 391 |
| g/kg (OC) | OC% | ÷ 10 |

## 7. WHAT LABS DON'T ALWAYS TELL YOU

**Available N ≠ Total N.**
Total N is everything locked in soil organic matter + minerals.
Available N is what plants can absorb this season.
Always confirm which is reported -- prescription is based on Available N only.

**P is often underestimated in alkaline soils.**
The standard Olsen method (used by most Gujarat labs) underestimates P availability
in high-pH soils. Your actual plant-available P may be lower than reported.

**Zn and B are almost always functionally deficient in Junagadh area**
regardless of what the report shows, because alkaline pH locks them even when soil
content appears "sufficient" on paper.

**If EC is missing** from your report -- ask the lab to add it.
It is a fast, inexpensive test and critical for Saurashtra where bore well water
quality varies significantly across the district.

**Lab report date matters.**
Soil chemistry changes with irrigation, rainfall, and crop uptake.
Test at the same time each year (pre-sowing, post-harvest) to track trends accurately.
