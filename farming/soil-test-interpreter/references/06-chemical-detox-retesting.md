## 1. READING A SOIL TEST ON CHEMICALLY ABUSED LAND

A soil test taken in Year 1 of transition from heavy chemical farming
must be interpreted differently from a test on a healthy or virgin farm.
The numbers are real but their causes are different -- and so are the fixes.

### What Each Parameter Is Telling You in Year 1

| Parameter | Typical Year 1 Reading | Real Cause | Correct Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------|----------------------|
| OC% | 0.2-0.4% (critically low) | Microbial biomass destroyed by pesticides and synthetic N | Genuine deficiency. Priority 1. Double Jeevamrut dose. |
| EC (dS/m) | 0.75-2.5 (elevated) | Accumulated fertilizer salt residue -- especially from urea and DAP | Will drop naturally as salts leach. Gypsum + deep irrigation accelerate this. |
| Available N | Very low (under 200 kg/ha) | Soil biology is inert -- cannot cycle N from organic matter | Not a real N deficit. Fix biology first. N numbers improve automatically. |
| Available P | Low (even if historically applied) | Disrupted mycorrhizal network + pH lock | Fix pH and OC first. Do not over-apply P inputs -- they will sit unused. |
| Zn (mg/kg) | <0.4 (deficient) | pH drift to 8.5+ from calcium carbonate in urea + dead microbes | Functional deficiency is real. Neem cake + Jeevamrut + ZnSO4 protocol applies. |
| Fe (mg/kg) | Low on report | High pH locking Fe that is physically present | Correct pH. Do not add Fe inputs until pH is below 8.0. |
| B (mg/kg) | Often deficient | Alkalinity lock + disrupted plant uptake systems | Borax foliar only. Same protocol as healthy soil. |
| Germination % | Not on lab report but observable | Herbicide residue hotspots in field | Note patchy zones. Extra Jeevamrut in those areas. Heavy mulch. |

### The Two-Step Interpretation Rule for Year 1

**Step 1:** Classify each parameter using the standard tables in `01-parameter-ranges.md`.
**Step 2:** Apply this overlay -- did this reading come from a chemically treated farm?

- If YES and OC <0.5%: adjust all nutrient prescriptions down by 30-40%.
  Reason: inputs applied to biologically dead soil are only partially effective.
  Wait for biology to activate (Season 2) before applying full doses.

- If YES and EC >1.5: gypsum + leaching takes priority over all nutrient corrections.
  Reason: high salt stress suppresses plant uptake regardless of what else you apply.

- If YES and pH >8.5: gypsum + green manure takes priority.
  Reason: every other input is partially or fully locked at this pH.

## 2. YEAR 1 ADJUSTED PRESCRIPTION RULES

These override the standard prescriptions in `02-macronutrient-corrections.md`
for Season 1 only on chemically transitioned farms.

| Standard Prescription | Year 1 Adjustment | Reason |
|----------------------|------------------|--------|
| Vermicompost 400-500 kg | Reduce to 150-200 kg | Dead soil biology cannot break down heavy organic loads |
| Rock phosphate 50 kg | Defer to Season 2 | Mycorrhizal network destroyed -- cannot access rock phosphate yet |
| Neem cake 50 kg | Reduce to 25 kg | Effective at 25 kg; excess creates N lock in biologically inert soil |
| Ghanjeevamrut 50 kg | Apply as normal | Solid inoculum -- good for transition |
| Jeevamrut 200 L fortnightly | INCREASE to 400 L | First 3 applications only. Most important Year 1 action. |
| Green manure (Dhaincha 5 kg) | Apply as normal | Critical for Year 1 -- do not reduce |
| Micronutrient foliar sprays | Apply as normal | Foliar bypasses soil biology limitation |
| Wood ash (if K low) | Defer to Season 2 | Adds alkalinity -- counterproductive if pH already high |

**The one-sentence Year 1 rule:**
Jeevamrut + green manure + gypsum (if EC/pH high) + cover crop.
Everything else is Season 2.

## 3. RE-TESTING SCHEDULE AND RECOVERY TRACKING

Soil recovery cannot be managed without data. This schedule ensures
every test contributes to a trajectory, not just a snapshot.

### Minimum Testing Schedule

| Test # | Timing | Parameters to Test | Purpose |
|--------|--------|-------------------|---------|
| Baseline | Pre-Kharif Year 1 (April-May) | Full 12-parameter | Starting point. Reference for all future comparisons. |
| Test 2 | Post-Rabi Year 1 (Feb-March) | pH, EC, OC, N, Zn | First recovery check after 2 seasons of inputs |
| Test 3 | Pre-Kharif Year 2 (May) | Full 12-parameter | Year 1 complete -- compare to baseline. Adjust Year 2 prescriptions. |
| Test 4 | Post-Rabi Year 2 (Feb-March) | pH, EC, OC, Zn | Trend confirmation |
| Annual | Pre-Kharif every year | Full 12-parameter | Ongoing benchmark and prescription adjustment |

### Rules for Valid Comparison

All comparisons between tests are only valid if:
- Same lab (GSFC, KVK, or consistent private lab)
- Same time of year (pre-sowing season)
- Samples from the same field locations
- Same depth (0-15 cm standard)

Mark 5-7 GPS or landmark-identified sampling spots in your field at baseline.
Return to the same spots every season. Keep a diagram or photo reference.

### Reading the Trend -- What to Look For

**Positive signals (recovery is working):**
- EC dropping each season, even if still elevated
- OC rising, even if slowly (0.03-0.06% per season is normal)
- pH gradually declining toward 7.8-8.0
- Zn and Fe readings improving as pH and OC improve

**Warning signals (something in the protocol needs adjustment):**
- EC not dropping after 2 seasons: test bore well water EC -- the water may be resalting
- OC flat after 2 seasons: green manure may not be incorporating deep enough; mulch may be insufficient
- Zn still deficient despite neem cake applications: pH still above 8.0; fix pH first
- N not improving after 2 seasons: Jeevamrut frequency may need to increase to every 10 days

## 4. SEASON-ON-SEASON IMPROVEMENT TARGETS

These are realistic targets for a farm starting from chemical-transition baseline.
Do not expect to reach healthy farm values in Year 1. These are trajectories, not deadlines.

| Parameter | Year 0 Baseline (Chemical) | End Year 1 | End Year 2 | Healthy Farm Target |
|-----------|---------------------------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| OC% | 0.2-0.4% | 0.35-0.50% | 0.5-0.75% | >1.0% |
| pH | 8.2-8.5 | 7.9-8.3 | 7.8-8.0 | 7.5-8.0 |
| EC (dS/m) | 0.75-2.5 | 0.5-1.2 | 0.25-0.75 | <0.75 |
| Available N (kg/ha) | <200 | 200-280 | 250-350 | >280 |
| Zn (mg/kg) | <0.4 | 0.35-0.6 | 0.5-0.8 | >0.6 |
| B (mg/kg) | <0.4 | 0.4-0.6 | 0.5-0.8 | >0.5 |
| Earthworms per sq ft | 0-1 | 2-4 | 5-8 | >5 |

**Important context:**
These targets assume consistent Jeevamrut (fortnightly), green manure every season,
vermicompost pre-sowing, and mulching. If any of these are skipped, the trajectory slows.
The earthworm count is a more reliable real-time indicator than lab values.
It updates monthly; a lab test only updates once or twice a year.

## 5. COVER CROP PRESCRIPTIONS FOR SOIL HEALING

Cover crops are soil medicine in Year 1. They heal chemically abused land
through root action, nitrogen fixation, and bulk organic matter addition.
Sow them in any gap period, any bare patch, and pre-sowing each season.

### Cover Crop Selection Guide

| Crop | Botanical Name | Best For | When to Sow | Seed per Vigha | Incorporate At |
|------|---------------|---------|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Dhaincha | Sesbania bispinosa | Best all-round choice for Saurashtra. Tolerates alkaline pH, waterlogging. Deepest roots (4-6 ft) break compaction. Fixes 25-30 kg N. | May-June | 5 kg | 40-45 days (at flowering) |
| Sunn Hemp | Crotalaria juncea | Fastest biomass builder. Roots release organic acids that mobilize locked P from fertilizer residue. High OC addition. | June | 8-10 kg | 45-50 days |
| Cowpea | Vigna unguiculata | Lighter crop -- can harvest early pods for income while still building soil. Fixes 15 kg N. Tolerates partial salinity. | June-July | 6-8 kg | 40 days (after pod harvest if desired) |
| Cluster Bean | Cyamopsis tetragonoloba | Guar gum root exudates improve soil aggregation and water retention. Seed is easy to source in Junagadh. | June-July | 8 kg | 45 days |
| Mustard | Brassica juncea | Best for Rabi gap. Biofumigant -- roots release glucosinolates that suppress soil pathogens. Also adds S. | October-November | 2-3 kg | 30-35 days (before flowering) |

### Cover Crop Protocol -- Step by Step

**Pre-Kharif (April-May after Kali Jeeri harvest):**

1. Leave all crop residue on surface -- do not burn
2. Shallow till 4-6 inches to break surface crust
3. Apply Jeevamrut double dose (400 L/Vigha) -- let absorb for 3 days
4. Broadcast Dhaincha seed at 5 kg/Vigha -- rake lightly into top 1 inch
5. Irrigate once to establish germination
6. At 40-45 days (when flowering begins): cut stems, leave wilted 2-3 days on surface
7. Shallow incorporate (4 inches) -- do not go deeper
8. Apply Jeevamrut immediately (200 L/Vigha) to activate decomposition
9. Wait 12-15 days before main crop sowing

**During Kharif main crop season:**
- Any bare patches that fail to germinate: broadcast cowpea seed immediately
- Do not leave any soil bare during rainy season -- cover crop or mulch

**Post-Kharif / Pre-Rabi (October):**
- If time permits before Rabi sowing: quick mustard cover crop
- 2-3 kg/Vigha, 30 days, incorporate before flowering
- Mustard biofumigation helps clear herbicide residue zones from Kharif

### Where to Source Seeds in Junagadh Area

## 6. BIOREMEDIATION INPUTS -- ORGANIC DETOX TOOLS

These inputs have specific detoxification effects on chemically degraded soil,
beyond their standard nutritional roles.

| Input | Detox Mechanism | Year 1 Protocol | Source in Keshod Area |
|-------|----------------|----------------|----------------------|
| Jeevamrut (double dose) | Bacillus and Pseudomonas species break down organochlorine pesticide residues and neutralize fertilizer salts through ion exchange | 400 L/Vigha for first 3 applications, then 200 L standard | Make on farm (cow dung + urine) |
| Neem cake | Azadirachtin inhibits harmful nematodes; encourages beneficial fungi; slow N release | 25-50 kg/Vigha pre-sowing | APMC Keshod or Junagadh -- affordable |
| Vermicompost (humic acids) | Humic and fulvic acids chelate heavy metal residues and fertilizer salts, reducing bioavailability | 150-200 kg/Vigha Year 1 only (full dose Year 2+) | KVK Junagadh or organic input suppliers |
| Gypsum (CaSO4) | Displaces accumulated sodium from clay exchange sites; improves drainage; lowers EC | 50 kg/Vigha if EC >1.0 or pH >8.5 -- first 2 seasons | GSFC depot or any agri shop |
| Wood vinegar (Kashtha Saar) | Promotes beneficial soil fungi, inhibits soil-borne pathogens, accelerates breakdown of chemical residues | 1:500 dilution in water (2 mL/L) as soil drench -- once per season | Byproduct of biochar production; inquire at local pottery or brick kilns |
| Green manure (Dhaincha) | Root exudates solubilize locked nutrients; bulk OM addition reactivates microbial community | 5 kg seed per Vigha -- every pre-season in Year 1 and Year 2 | See sourcing table above |

## 7. WHEN RECOVERY IS SLOWER THAN EXPECTED

If your Year 2 test does not show the expected improvements, work through this checklist
before changing the prescription:

| Parameter not improving | Check first | Likely cause | Adjustment |
|------------------------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| EC still >1.5 dS/m | Test bore well water EC | Irrigation water resalting the field every cycle | Reduce irrigation frequency; apply deep irrigation to push salts below root zone |
| OC not rising past 0.5% | Is green manure being incorporated at the right time? | Green manure seeding too late or incorporated after seed set (most carbon lost) | Incorporate at flowering -- before seed forms |
| Zn still deficient | Check pH reading | pH still above 8.0 -- Zn is locked regardless of application | Increase gypsum; delay Zn correction until pH below 8.0 |
| N still very low | Is Jeevamrut being made correctly? | Incorrect preparation -- no active microbiology | Cross-check Jeevamrut recipe in vedic-farming `01-liquid-preparations.md` |
| Germination still patchy in same spots | Same patch failing 2+ seasons | Persistent herbicide residue (trifluralin, atrazine can persist 18-24 months) | Extra Jeevamrut in patches; mustard biofumigation; physical soil removal as last resort |
| pH not dropping | Is gypsum being raked in? | Gypsum left on surface does not lower pH effectively | Rake gypsum 3-4 inches into moist soil immediately after application |

## 8. CONNECTING SOIL TESTS TO THE FARM JOURNAL

Every soil test result and every seasonal observation should be logged
in the farm journal skill to build a searchable improvement record.

**What to log after each soil test:**

```
Date of test:        [date sample was collected]
Lab name:            [GSFC / KVK / private]
Season:              [Pre-Kharif YYYY / Post-Rabi YYYY]
Sample locations:    [landmarks or GPS spots used]

Key readings:
  pH:      [value] -- [Low / Normal / High]
  EC:      [value] -- [OK / Caution / High]
  OC%:     [value] -- [status]
  N:       [value] kg/ha
  Zn:      [value] mg/kg

Change from last test:
  pH:  [+/- X units]
  EC:  [+/- X dS/m]
  OC%: [+/- X%]
  Zn:  [+/- X mg/kg]

Prescriptions applied this season:
  [list inputs, quantities, dates]

Observations:
  [earthworm count, soil smell, germination %, root depth]

Next season action:
  [what to change based on this test]
```

**Cross-skill logging:**
- Log test results and corrections in agstack-farming Farm Calendar
  for PGS certification documentation trail
- Record earthworm counts and soil physical checks in farm-journal skill
  under SOIL-OBS entry type
- Match any cover crop sowing or green manure events to the lunar calendar
  in vedic-farming `03-lunar-calendar.md` for optimal timing
