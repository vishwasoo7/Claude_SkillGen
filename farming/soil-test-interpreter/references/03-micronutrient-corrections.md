## 1. VISUAL SYMPTOM IDENTIFIER

Use this table before a lab report arrives -- to start interim organic treatment:

| What You See on the Crop | Most Likely Deficiency | Confirm With |
|--------------------------|----------------------|--------------|
| New leaves yellow, green veins remain (interveinal chlorosis) | Zinc or Iron | Lab test Zn + Fe |
| Stunted new growth, crinkled / thick / cupped young leaves | Boron | Lab test B |
| Hollow stems in vegetables (cauliflower, cabbage) | Boron | Visual + lab |
| Yellowing from leaf tips inward on older leaves | Sulphur | Lab test S |
| Pale green / yellow young leaves, older leaves stay green | Iron | Lab test Fe |
| Brown necrotic spots + scorched edges on older leaves | Manganese | Lab test Mn |
| Twisted young leaves, poorly developed roots | Copper | Lab test Cu |
| Interveinal chlorosis + small leaves + poor fruiting | Zinc (classic) | Lab test Zn |
| Poor pod filling in groundnut | Boron or Calcium | Lab test B + Ca |
| Boll shedding in cotton early | Boron | Lab test B |

## 2. ZINC (Zn)

**Most common deficiency in Junagadh district -- check this first.**
Alkaline pH (>7.5) locks Zn even when soil contains adequate amounts.
Most effective fix is combining Jeevamrut (microbes unlock Zn) + direct Zn input.

### If Zn < 0.6 mg/kg -- Deficient

**Soil application (per Vigha):**

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Neem cake | 50 kg | Pre-sowing -- mix into soil | Contains Zn + activates Zn-solubilizing microbes |
| Vermicompost | 300 kg | Pre-sowing | Microbial activity releases locked Zn |
| Jeevamrut drench | 200 litres | Every 15 days | Microbial solubilization of Zn from soil reserves |
| Zinc sulphate (ZnSO₄) | 5 kg | Pre-sowing, mixed with vermicompost | Permitted in organic transition -- fast-acting |

**Foliar spray (per Vigha per application):**

| Input | Mix | Frequency |
|-------|-----|-----------|
| Panchgavya | 3L in 100L water | Every 15 days |
| Seaweed extract (if available) | 500 ml in 100L water | Every 15 days |

**Recovery time:** 1-2 seasons with consistent protocol.
OC building is the long-term Zn fix -- as OC rises above 1.0%, Zn availability improves naturally.

### If Zn 0.6-1.2 mg/kg -- Sufficient but pH >8.0
Treat as functionally borderline. Apply neem cake 25 kg + Jeevamrut fortnightly.
The pH is still locking some of the Zn from plant access.

## 3. BORON (B)

**Critical for:** Groundnut (pod filling), cotton (boll development and retention),
all fruiting vegetables, and cumin. Boron is immobile in plants -- deficiency hits
the youngest, fastest-growing tissues first.

### If B < 0.5 mg/kg -- Deficient

**Foliar spray -- preferred method (safer than soil for boron):**

| Input | Mix | Timing | Notes |
|-------|-----|--------|-------|
| Borax (sodium tetraborate) | 1 kg in 200L water per Vigha | Once at flower initiation + once at fruit set | Do NOT over-apply |
| Dashaparni Ark | 3L in 100L water | Every 15 days | General plant strengthener with trace B |

**Soil application:**

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Vermicompost | 300 kg | Pre-sowing -- provides background B |
| Neem cake | 50 kg | Pre-sowing |

⚠️ **Boron warning -- narrow safety margin:**
The gap between deficiency and toxicity in boron is very small.
Never apply borax to soil more than once per season.
Never increase foliar concentration above 0.5% (1 kg per 200L water).
When in doubt -- apply less, observe, then apply again.

## 4. SULPHUR (S)

**Deficiency common in Saurashtra** -- particularly after continuous groundnut, cumin,
or vegetable cropping without mustard or brassica crops in rotation.
Critical for: groundnut protein and oil quality, onion/garlic flavour, all brassicas.

### If S < 10 mg/kg -- Deficient

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Gypsum (calcium sulphate CaSO₄) | 40-50 kg | Pre-sowing, rake into top 4 inches | Primary organic S source -- also corrects high pH |
| Mustard cake (rai khali) | 50 kg | Pre-sowing | Excellent S source + N + growth stimulants |
| Jeevamrut drench | 200 litres | Every 15 days | Activates S-cycling bacteria |
| Vermicompost | 200 kg | Pre-sowing | Background S supply |

**Dual benefit of gypsum:** If your report shows pH >8.0 AND S deficiency --
gypsum is your single most effective input because it corrects both simultaneously.
Prioritize gypsum in your pre-sowing application in that scenario.

## 5. IRON (Fe)

**Common in alkaline soils -- but Fe is almost always present.**
The problem is not lack of Fe, it is pH locking it.
Fixing pH is more effective than adding Fe inputs directly.

### If Fe < 4.5 mg/kg -- Deficient (or pH >8.0 with symptoms)

**Soil application:**

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Jeevamrut drench | 200 litres | Every 15 days -- iron-reducing bacteria |
| Vermicompost + farm compost | 300 kg | Pre-sowing |
| Dhaincha green manure | 5 kg seed → incorporate | Pre-season |

**Foliar (during active deficiency symptoms):**

| Input | Mix | Notes |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Ferrous sulphate (FeSO₄) | 500g in 100L water | Transitional use during severe symptoms -- foliar bypasses pH lock |

**Note:** If pH is above 8.5, foliar Fe is far more effective than soil application.
Soil Fe inputs are rendered unavailable at that alkalinity level.

## 6. MANGANESE (Mn)

Less common deficiency in Saurashtra. More likely to appear in
heavily irrigated plots or after waterlogging events.

### If Mn < 2.0 mg/kg -- Deficient

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Vermicompost | 250 kg | Pre-sowing |
| Jeevamrut drench | 200 litres | Every 15 days |
| Farm compost | 500 kg | Pre-sowing |

**Primary fix:** OC building. Mn availability improves automatically as OC rises
and soil biology activates Mn-cycling. No dedicated Mn organic input is widely available.
Focus corrections on OC + pH management.

---

## 7. COPPER (Cu)

Rarely deficient in Saurashtra unless soil has been over-limed or heavily leached
over many seasons. Confirm before treating.

### If Cu < 0.2 mg/kg -- Deficient

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Vermicompost | 300 kg | Pre-sowing |
| Jeevamrut drench | 200 litres | Every 15 days |
| Panchgavya foliar | 3L in 100L water | Every 15 days |

Cu availability normalizes within 1-2 seasons through OC building.
Dedicated Cu applications are almost never required in Junagadh area soils.

## 8. SAURASHTRA CORRECTION PRIORITY

Based on observed deficiency patterns in Junagadh district soils:

```
Priority 1 -- Zinc (Zn)
  Most widespread deficiency. Almost every farm. Fix first, every season.
  Primary method: Neem cake + Jeevamrut + ZnSO₄ (transitional)

Priority 2 -- Boron (B)
  Critical for groundnut (your likely Kharif crop) and cumin (Rabi).
  Primary method: Borax foliar spray at flowering.

Priority 3 -- Sulphur (S)
  Fix alongside pH correction -- gypsum handles both simultaneously.
  Primary method: Gypsum pre-sowing.

Priority 4 -- Iron (Fe)
  Fix through pH reduction + OC building. Rarely needs direct input.
  Primary method: pH correction first. Foliar FeSO₄ if symptomatic.

Priority 5 -- Manganese (Mn)
  Fix through OC building only. No direct organic input available.

Priority 6 -- Copper (Cu)
  Monitor only. Almost never needs active correction in Keshod area.
```
