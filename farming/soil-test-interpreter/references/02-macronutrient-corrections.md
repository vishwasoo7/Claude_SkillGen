## 1. ORGANIC CARBON -- THE FOUNDATION

**Fix OC before correcting any specific nutrient.**
Low OC means soil microbes are starving -- they cannot cycle any nutrients you apply.
Money spent on rock phosphate or neem cake without adequate OC is partially wasted.

### If OC < 0.5% -- Critical

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Cross-reference |
|-------|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Jeevamrut (soil drench) | 200 litres | Every 15 days -- first 3 months | `vedic-farming` `01-liquid-preparations.md` |
| Ghanjeevamrut | 50 kg | Pre-sowing base dressing | `vedic-farming` `01-liquid-preparations.md` |
| Vermicompost | 400-500 kg | Pre-sowing, mixed into top 6 inches | `vedic-farming` `04-soil-amendments.md` |
| Green manure (Dhaincha / Sunn Hemp) | 5 kg seed | Sow 45 days before main crop, incorporate | `vedic-farming` `04-soil-amendments.md` |
| Mulch | 600+ kg material | Immediately after sowing -- never bare soil | `vedic-farming` `04-soil-amendments.md` |

**Expected improvement:** +0.05-0.10% OC per season with full protocol.
Target OC >1.0% typically reached in 4-6 consistent seasons.

### If OC 0.5-0.75% -- Medium-Low

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Jeevamrut (drench) | 200 litres | Monthly |
| Vermicompost | 250 kg | Pre-sowing |
| Mulch | 400-600 kg | After sowing |
| Green manure | Every alternate season | Rotational |

### If OC > 0.75% -- Maintain
Jeevamrut monthly + mulch continuously + green manure rotational.
No heavy inputs needed -- protect what you have built.

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|

| Neem cake | 50 kg | Pre-sowing -- mix into top 4 inches | Slow-release N + soil pest deterrent |
| Green manure (Dhaincha) | 5 kg seed | Pre-Kharif / pre-Rabi | Fixes 20-35 kg N per Vigha atmospherically |

## 3. PHOSPHORUS (P)

**Critical note for Saurashtra:** At pH >8.0, P is chemically locked even when
lab values show "medium." Always treat P as functionally deficient if your pH is above 8.0.
**Fix pH first** (see `04-ph-ec-corrections.md`), then apply P inputs -- otherwise inputs are wasted.

### If P < 10 kg/ha -- Low (or P medium + pH >8.0)

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Rock phosphate | 50 kg | Pre-sowing, always mixed with vermicompost | Most effective organic P source |
| Vermicompost | 200 kg | Pre-sowing -- mix with rock phosphate | Microbes solubilize phosphate, making it plant-available |
| Bone meal | 25 kg | Pre-sowing | Faster-acting P than rock phosphate |
| Panchgavya (foliar) | 3 litres in 100L water | Every 15 days at flowering | P uptake enhancer |
| Jeevamrut (drench) | 200 litres | Every 15 days | Activates P-solubilizing bacteria in soil |

**Key:** Rock phosphate must always be mixed with vermicompost before application --
never applied alone. The microbes in vermicompost are what release phosphate from rock.

### If P 10-25 kg/ha -- Medium AND pH < 8.0
Rock phosphate 25 kg + vermicompost 150 kg + Jeevamrut monthly.
No aggressive correction needed -- maintain.

### If P 10-25 kg/ha -- Medium BUT pH > 8.0
Treat as Low deficiency. Fix pH with gypsum first, then apply full Low-P protocol above.

## 4. POTASSIUM (K)

**Saurashtra note:** K is usually Medium-High in Junagadh district.
Confirm your value before applying K inputs -- over-applying K competes with Mg and Ca uptake.

### If K < 108 kg/ha -- Low (Rare in Saurashtra)

| Input | Per Vigha | Timing | Notes |
|-------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Wood ash | 50-75 kg | Pre-sowing, rake into top 2 inches | Fast K release -- widely available |
| Banana peel compost | 100 kg | Pre-sowing | Excellent organic K source |
| Vermicompost | 200 kg | Pre-sowing | Contains K + amplifies microbial K cycling |
| Jeevamrut (drench) | 200 litres | Every 15 days | Activates K-releasing microbes in soil |
| Granite dust | 50 kg if available | Annual base dressing | Very slow-release background K |

### If K 108-280 kg/ha -- Medium (Maintain)
Wood ash 25 kg per season + vermicompost 100 kg. No aggressive correction needed.

### If K > 280 kg/ha -- High
Do not apply any K inputs. Focus entirely on N, P, and OC building.

## 6. MULTI-DEFICIENCY PROTOCOL

When multiple parameters are deficient simultaneously -- very common in first-season testing --
apply inputs in priority order and do not try to fix everything in one application:

**If the farm has a history of heavy chemical use:**
Do not apply the full multi-deficiency protocol in Season 1.
Chemically abused soil has disrupted biology -- it cannot process heavy organic inputs effectively.
Applying 400 kg vermicompost + 50 kg rock phosphate + 50 kg neem cake all at once on
biologically inert soil wastes inputs. The microbes needed to break them down are not there yet.

Season 1 priority for chemical-transition farms:
1. Jeevamrut double dose (400L/Vigha) -- reactivate biology first
2. Gypsum if EC >1.0 or pH >8.5 -- 50 kg, rake in
3. Green manure sowing (Dhaincha 5 kg) -- build biomass before adding purchased inputs
4. After green manure incorporation: neem cake 25 kg + vermicompost 150 kg only
5. Full multi-deficiency protocol below starts Season 2 when biology is active

See `references/06-chemical-detox-retesting.md` for the complete transition protocol.

**Standard multi-deficiency protocol (Season 2+ or farms without chemical history):**

**Season 1 -- Foundation:**
1. Gypsum (if pH >8.5) -- 50 kg, rake in
2. Green manure sowing → incorporate after 45 days
3. Vermicompost 400 kg + rock phosphate 50 kg + neem cake 50 kg -- mix, apply pre-sowing
4. Ghanjeevamrut 50 kg -- base dressing
5. Jeevamrut drench -- every 15 days through season

**Season 2 -- Build:**
1. Reduce gypsum to 25 kg (if pH improving)
2. Vermicompost 300 kg + rock phosphate 50 kg
3. Jeevamrut every 15 days
4. Add micronutrient corrections (Zn, B) -- see `03-micronutrient-corrections.md`

**Season 3 -- Stabilize:**
Vermicompost 250 kg + Jeevamrut monthly + green manure rotational.
Soil OC should now be above 0.75-1.0%. Nutrient availability improving automatically.
