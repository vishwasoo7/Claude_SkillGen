# 02 -- i-Khedut Portal Guide, Documents Checklist, and Offline Fallback
## Step-by-Step Application Process for Gujarat Farmers

i-Khedut (ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in) is the single portal for all Gujarat agriculture
scheme applications. Understanding it well saves multiple trips to government offices.

## 1. WHAT i-KHEDUT IS AND HOW IT WORKS

i-Khedut (meaning "i-Farmer" in Gujarati) is the Gujarat Government's unified
scheme application portal launched to eliminate the need for farmers to visit
multiple offices for different schemes.

**What it covers:**
- All Gujarat state agriculture schemes
- Central schemes operated through Gujarat (PM-KUSUM, PKVY, NHM, MIDH, Soil Health Card)
- Animal husbandry schemes (if relevant later)
- Horticulture schemes
- Water conservation schemes (farm pond, check dam)

**How it works:**
1. You create a profile once using Aadhaar and land record number
2. Your profile persists across seasons and scheme applications
3. You apply for each scheme from your profile
4. Documents uploaded once can be reused for multiple scheme applications
5. Application status, approval, and subsidy release tracked on portal
6. Subsidy amount is released directly to your bank account via DBT (Direct Benefit Transfer)

**Portal URL:** ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in
**Mobile app:** "i-Khedut" available on Google Play Store (useful for status tracking)

## 2. STEP-BY-STEP APPLICATION PROCESS

### Step 1: First-Time Registration

Go to ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in and click "Registration" or "New Farmer Registration".

Required at registration:
- Aadhaar number (must be linked to mobile for OTP)
- 7/12 Utara survey number and district/taluka/village
- Bank account number and IFSC code (for DBT subsidy transfer)
- Mobile number linked to Aadhaar

After registration you receive a Farmer Registration Number (FRN).
Keep this number -- it is your identity across all future applications.

If your mobile is not linked to Aadhaar: visit the nearest Aadhaar centre or
Common Service Centre (CSC/Jan Seva Kendra) in Keshod to link first.

### Step 2: Login and Profile Completion

Login with FRN and mobile OTP.

Complete your profile:
- Land details: all survey numbers, area in hectares and Vigha
- Irrigation source: borewell (submersible electric pump)
- Farm category: small / marginal / other (this determines subsidy percentage)
- Caste category: General / OBC / SC / ST
- Bank details: account number, IFSC, branch

Upload base documents (uploaded once, reused for all applications):
- [ ] 7/12 Utara scan (PDF or clear photo)
- [ ] Aadhaar copy (PDF or photo)
- [ ] Bank passbook front page (PDF or photo)
- [ ] Passport photo (JPG, under 100KB)

### Step 3: Browse and Select Scheme

From the dashboard, go to "Yojana" (Schemes) section.
Schemes are listed by category:
- Pik Suraksha (Crop Protection)
- Sinchai (Irrigation) -- PM-KUSUM is here
- Jamin Sudharna (Soil Health) -- Soil Health Card is here
- Jevik Kheti (Organic Farming) -- PKVY, Mukhyamantri Prakrutik Kheti
- Tal Vistaran (Technology Extension) -- ATMA linked programmes

Select the scheme you want to apply for.
The portal will show: scheme description, eligibility criteria, benefit amount,
required documents, and application window (open/closed status).

**If the scheme shows "Closed" or "Window Not Open":**
Note the expected next opening date if shown.
Set a calendar reminder and check back.
Do not contact the office -- the portal is the official window; offices cannot
override the digital window.

### Step 4: Fill Application Form

Each scheme has a form. Most fields auto-fill from your profile.
Fields that require specific input per application:
- Scheme-specific details (e.g., pump HP for PM-KUSUM, crop type for PKVY)
- Any supporting declaration or statement
- Whether you have received the same or similar scheme benefit before

### Step 5: Upload Scheme-Specific Documents

In addition to base documents (already uploaded), each scheme may need:
- PM-KUSUM: electricity bill or borewell certificate
- PKVY: cluster constitution, group member list
- Mukhyamantri Prakrutik Kheti: ZBNF declaration, training attendance (if any)
- NHM / MIDH: quotation from empanelled drip irrigation supplier

File size limits: typically 200KB per document. Compress if needed.
Format: PDF preferred; JPG accepted for photos.

### Step 6: Submit and Save Application Number

After submission, you receive an Application Reference Number.
Screenshot or note this number immediately.
This is the only way to track your application -- there is no email confirmation.

### Step 7: Physical Verification (if required)

Some schemes require a physical inspection of your farm before approval.
For PM-KUSUM: PGVCL technician may visit to verify borewell location.
For PKVY: agriculture officer may visit to verify cluster farms.
For NHM drip: officer verifies existing irrigation infrastructure.

When contacted for inspection:
- Be at the farm at the scheduled time
- Have physical copies of all documents ready
- Show your Farm Calendar (agstack) or farm journal as supporting evidence
- Physical copies of peer inspection records (pgs-india-roadmap) add credibility

### Step 8: Track and Follow Up

After submission: check portal every 10-14 days for status update.
Statuses to watch for:
- Submitted: received, waiting for review
- Under Verification: assigned to district officer
- Inspection Scheduled: visit expected soon
- Approved: subsidy amount confirmed, bank transfer pending
- Disbursed: amount credited to your account
- Rejected: see rejection reason, address it, reapply in next window

## 3. MASTER DOCUMENTS CHECKLIST

Prepare these documents once and keep digital copies ready.
All documents should be self-attested (sign and write "Self Attested" on photocopy).

### Identity and Land (required for ALL schemes)
- [ ] Aadhaar card -- original + clear photocopy + digital scan (PDF/JPG)
- [ ] 7/12 Utara -- certified copy from Talati office (renew if over 3 months old)
- [ ] 8-A (land record extract) -- available at Talati or online at anyror.gujarat.gov.in
- [ ] Passport-size photograph -- 4 copies physical + digital (under 100KB JPG)

### Financial
- [ ] Bank passbook -- front page scan (account number, IFSC, name clearly visible)
- [ ] Cancelled cheque (some schemes require this in addition to passbook)

### Farm-Specific (prepare these proactively)
- [ ] Borewell/water source certificate -- electricity bill in farmer's name,
      or Gram Panchayat certificate confirming borewell existence
- [ ] Soil Health Card (once received -- useful as baseline documentation)
- [ ] PGS Local Group registration letter (from KVK Junagadh, once obtained)

### Category-Specific (if applicable)
- [ ] Caste certificate (if applying under SC/ST/OBC -- issued by Mamlatdar office)
- [ ] Small/Marginal farmer certificate (if not auto-determined from land area)

### Scheme-Specific
- [ ] PKVY: signed cluster constitution + all member land records
- [ ] PM-KUSUM: existing pump electricity connection bill (PGVCL)
- [ ] NHM drip: supplier quotation from i-Khedut empanelled vendor
- [ ] Mukhyamantri Prakrutik Kheti: ZBNF declaration signed

## 4. HOW TO FIND NEW AND UPDATED SCHEMES ON THE PORTAL

The portal lists all currently active schemes. This is faster than web search
for confirming whether a specific scheme is open right now.

**Steps to browse all active schemes:**
1. Go to ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in
2. Click "Yojana" or "Schemes" in the top menu
3. Do not filter -- view all categories
4. Look for schemes with "Window Open" status
5. Any scheme you do not recognise: click for details, note name and benefit
6. Report new unfamiliar schemes to yourself for investigation

**What to look for that signals a new scheme:**
- Scheme name you have not seen before
- Category that is relevant: Jevik Kheti (organic), Sinchai (irrigation),
  Jamin Sudharna (soil), Tal Vistaran (technology/training)
- Benefit amount that seems significant (above Rs 5,000 per Vigha or above Rs 50,000 total)

**Quarterly portal check habit:**
Set a recurring reminder every 3 months (April, July, October, January) to:
1. Log in to i-Khedut
2. Check all scheme statuses for schemes you have applied for
3. Browse for any new schemes in the farming categories
4. Run a web search: `ikhedut new scheme Gujarat [current month year]`

## 5. TRACKING YOUR APPLICATION STATUS

After applying, track status at:
ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in -> Login -> "My Applications" -> Application Reference Number

**If status is stuck at "Submitted" for more than 3 weeks:**
Visit Taluka Agriculture Office in person with your Application Reference Number.
They have backend access to identify if the application is stalled and why.

**If status shows "Rejected":**
Read the rejection reason carefully (displayed on portal).
Most common reasons and fixes: see Section 6 below.
Do not reapply until you have addressed the specific reason.

**If subsidy is "Approved" but not "Disbursed" after 4 weeks:**
Check your bank account DBT status at:
pfms.nic.in (Public Financial Management System -- tracks government payments)
Enter your Aadhaar number to see pending government payments.

## 6. COMMON REJECTION REASONS AND HOW TO AVOID THEM

| Rejection Reason | Prevention |
|---|---|
| 7/12 Utara name mismatch with Aadhaar | Ensure 7/12 and Aadhaar have identical name spelling. If different: get mutation done at Talati office first. |
| Mobile number not linked to Aadhaar | Link mobile at Aadhaar centre before applying. OTP cannot be received otherwise. |
| Bank account name does not match Aadhaar | Use a bank account that is in your exact name as per Aadhaar. |
| Duplicate application (same scheme, same year) | Do not apply twice. Check "My Applications" before reapplying. |
| Area exceeds scheme eligibility | Some schemes have maximum area limits. State your actual cultivated area, not total land. |
| Already received same scheme benefit | You cannot receive PM-KUSUM solar pump twice. If you had a prior scheme pump, declare it. |
| Document scan quality poor | Scan documents at 300 DPI minimum. Photograph in good light. Ensure text is readable. |
| Cluster not meeting minimum members (PKVY) | Do not submit PKVY application until you have minimum 20 members and 50 acres confirmed. |

## 7. OFFLINE FALLBACK -- WHEN THE PORTAL DOES NOT WORK

The i-Khedut portal can be slow during peak application periods (April-May).
If the portal is down or repeatedly failing:

**Option A: Common Service Centre (CSC / Jan Seva Kendra)**
CSC operators in Keshod have i-Khedut portal access and can apply on your behalf.
Small facilitation fee (Rs 50-100) is typical and worth paying during portal congestion.
Bring physical copies of all documents from the master checklist.

**Option B: Taluka Agriculture Office, Keshod**
The Taluka Agriculture Office has a counter for assisted applications.
Bring all original documents and copies.
Office hours: 10am to 5pm weekdays (Saturdays 10am to 1pm -- verify locally).
Best days: Tuesday or Thursday (typically less crowded).

**Option C: KVK Junagadh**
KVK facilitates scheme applications for farmers in their training network.
If you are registered with ATMA or attending KVK programmes, this is
the most supportive path -- they know the system well and can identify
which schemes you qualify for beyond what you have asked about.

## 8. KEY CONTACTS FOR JUNAGADH DISTRICT

Verify current contact numbers before visiting -- these may change.

| Office | Purpose | Location / Contact |
|---|---|---|
| Taluka Agriculture Office, Keshod | Scheme applications, ATMA registration, Mukhyamantri ZBNF | Keshod town, ask locally for current location |
| District Agriculture Office, Junagadh | PKVY cluster, ATMA district level, PM-KUSUM district quota | Junagadh city, near collectorate |
| KVK Junagadh | PGS registration, PKVY cluster support, ATMA programmes, soil testing | Junagadh Agricultural University, Moti Baug |
| PGVCL Keshod Subdivision | PM-KUSUM technical verification, electricity connection | Keshod -- ask at electricity office |
| District Horticulture Officer, Junagadh | NHM / MIDH applications | Junagadh city |
| Talati, Rangpur Village | 7/12 Utara, land mutations, Gram Panchayat certificates | Rangpur village office |
| Mamlatdar Office, Keshod | Caste certificates, income certificates | Keshod taluka headquarters |
| Nearest nationalised bank branch | Kisan Credit Card application | SBI / Bank of Baroda / Bank of India -- Keshod |

**General approach for any government contact:**
Go in person on a weekday morning (10am to 12pm) rather than calling.
Bring physical copies of all relevant documents.
Know your Application Reference Number if following up on an existing application.
If redirected to another office: get the name of the person redirecting you
and ask them to write the referral on a slip -- makes the next office more responsive.
