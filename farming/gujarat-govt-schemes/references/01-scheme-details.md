## 1. PM-KUSUM COMPONENT B -- SOLAR PUMP SUBSIDY {#1-pm-kusum}

**VERIFY LIVE search query:** `PM-KUSUM Component B Gujarat 2026 solar pump subsidy application`
**Also search:** `ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in PM-KUSUM 2026`

**Change indicators -- re-search if any of these appear in results:**
- New Union Budget announcement (February each year)
- Gujarat state budget (March each year)
- MNRE (Ministry of New and Renewable Energy) circular on revised subsidy percentage
- Any mention of "PM-KUSUM revised guidelines" or "Component B restructured"

### What It Is
Central Government scheme under Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE).
Component B specifically covers standalone solar pumps for individual farmers.
Converts your electric borewell pump to solar-powered -- solving the grid-outage
vulnerability that is the single largest risk on your farm right now.

### Benefit Amount (verify live before applying)
- Central Government subsidy: 30% of benchmark cost
- State Government (Gujarat) top-up: 30% additional (total 60% in most cases)
- Some categories (SC/ST/small/marginal farmers): up to 90% covered
- Benchmark cost for a 3-5 HP solar pump: approximately Rs 1.5-2.5 lakh
- Your net cost after subsidy (60%): approximately Rs 60,000-1,00,000
- Your net cost after subsidy (90%): approximately Rs 15,000-25,000

Category that likely applies to you: smallholder (under 2 hectares).
Verify your category with the Taluka Agriculture Officer -- it determines your subsidy percentage.

### Eligibility
- Farmer with own agricultural land and existing water source (borewell qualifies)
- Land must be in farmer's name (or close family member with shared agriculture)
- No prior solar pump received under any government scheme
- Farm in Gujarat state

### Documents Required
- [ ] Aadhaar card
- [ ] Land record: 7/12 Utara
- [ ] Passbook of nationalised bank (for subsidy direct transfer)
- [ ] Existing borewell proof (electricity connection bill or Gram Panchayat certificate)
- [ ] Passport-size photograph
- [ ] Caste certificate (if applying under SC/ST/OBC category)
- [ ] Mobile number linked to Aadhaar

### Where to Apply
- Primary: ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in (online application, Component B)
- Secondary: PGVCL (Paschim Gujarat Vij Company Ltd) Keshod subdivision office
- Also: District Agriculture Office, Junagadh -- they often have facilitation camps

### Typical Application Window
January to May (pre-Kharif window). This is when most states process applications.
Exact Gujarat window: verify at ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in or call PGVCL Keshod.
Do not wait -- this scheme is oversubscribed and applications are processed on
a first-come-first-served basis within each district allocation.

### Why This Is Your Highest-Priority Scheme
Your farm has a single electric borewell on grid power with no backup.
A 3-day power cut during critical irrigation can kill Cumin germination or
cause irreversible Sesame wilting. The solar pump eliminates this risk entirely
and pays for itself in electricity savings within 3-4 years even without the subsidy.
With the subsidy, payback is under 1 year.

## 2. SOIL HEALTH CARD SCHEME {#2-soil-health-card}

**VERIFY LIVE search query:** `Soil Health Card Scheme Gujarat 2026 apply free soil test`
**Also search:** `soilhealth.dac.gov.in Gujarat farmer`

**Change indicators:** Mention of "SHC Phase III", new testing parameters added,
change in testing frequency mandate, new portal URL.

### What It Is
Central scheme providing every farmer with a Soil Health Card covering 12 parameters.
Testing is done free of charge at government soil testing labs.
Card is issued every 2 years with crop-specific fertiliser recommendations.

### Benefit
- Free 12-parameter soil test (same parameters you pay Rs 500-800 for at private lab)
- Official government document -- useful as scheme eligibility evidence
- Recommendation letter for fertiliser use (organic or inorganic)
- Uploaded to national portal: soilhealth.dac.gov.in (traceable, verifiable)

### Eligibility
All farmers with agricultural land. No minimum area. No category restriction.

### Documents Required
- [ ] Aadhaar card
- [ ] 7/12 Utara (land record)
- [ ] Mobile number

### Where to Apply
- Gram Sevak (village level worker) -- simplest entry point
- Taluka Agriculture Office, Keshod
- Online: soilhealth.dac.gov.in (register, request test)

### Why This Matters for Your Farm
Your April 2026 baseline soil test (prescribed in `soil-test-interpreter` ref06 and
`pgs-india-roadmap` ref01) is ideally done through SHC scheme for two reasons:
1. It is free -- saves Rs 500-800 lab cost
2. The government-issued card is stronger documentation evidence for PKVY and
   PGS-India applications than a private lab receipt

Get your SHC test done in April 2026, then cross-reference results with
`soil-test-interpreter` for organic amendment prescriptions.

## 3. PARAMPARAGAT KRISHI VIKAS YOJANA (PKVY) {#3-pkvy}

**VERIFY LIVE search query:** `PKVY Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana 2025 2026 benefit amount cluster`
**Also search:** `PKVY Gujarat Junagadh organic cluster application 2026`

**Change indicators:** New PKVY guidelines (Ministry of Agriculture), revised benefit
amount (was Rs 50,000/ha -- check if changed), cluster size requirement revision,
mention of "PKVY merged with other scheme" or "PKVY restructured under PM Krishi Yojana".

### What It Is
Central Government's flagship organic farming support scheme.
Provides financial support to clusters of farmers transitioning to organic farming
over a 3-year period.

### Benefit Amount (verify live -- amount subject to annual revision)
- Rs 50,000 per hectare over 3 years (approximately Rs 25,000 per Vigha over 3 years)
- Disbursed in three tranches: Year 1, Year 2, Year 3
- Covers: input costs (seeds, bio-inputs), certification cost, post-harvest management

For your 3-Vigha (1.2 hectare) farm:
- Total potential PKVY benefit: approximately Rs 60,000 over 3 years
- Per year: approximately Rs 20,000

### Eligibility
- Minimum cluster: 20 farmers, 50 acres aggregate (approximately 20 hectares)
- You alone with 3 Vigha do not qualify -- requires forming or joining a cluster
- Cluster must be in contiguous or nearby geography
- Farmers must commit to no synthetic inputs for the 3-year period
- PGS-India or organic certification documentation strengthens application significantly

### Documents Required (cluster level)
- [ ] Cluster constitution (similar to PGS group constitution)
- [ ] All member land records (7/12 Utara)
- [ ] All member Aadhaar cards
- [ ] Baseline soil test reports for all cluster farms
- [ ] Declaration of no synthetic inputs signed by all members
- [ ] PGS Local Group registration number (if available -- not mandatory but helps)

### Where to Apply
- District Agriculture Office, Junagadh (PKVY district nodal officer)
- KVK Junagadh -- can help form clusters and submit applications
- Online component via: agricoop.nic.in (central portal)

### Timing
Applications typically open September-October (post-Kharif harvest, pre-Rabi).
Cluster formation needs to happen before the application window.
Start cluster formation discussion with KVK Junagadh in July-August 2026.

### Practical Path for Your Farm
Your PGS peer group (5 members) is a nucleus. PKVY needs 20.
Ask KVK Junagadh to connect you with other natural farming practitioners
in Keshod Taluka who could join a PKVY cluster.
Your demonstration farm positioning makes you a natural cluster anchor --
other farmers are more likely to join a cluster you lead.

## 4. MUKHYAMANTRI PRAKRUTIK KHETI YOJANA {#4-mpky}

**VERIFY LIVE search query:** `Mukhyamantri Prakrutik Kheti Yojana Gujarat 2026 benefit registration`
**Also search:** `Gujarat ZBNF scheme 2025 2026 farmer registration ikhedut`

**Change indicators:** State budget allocation change (Gujarat state budget, March),
scheme renamed, benefit amount revised, registration process changed, scheme paused
due to budget reallocation, new eligibility criteria.

### What It Is
Gujarat state government scheme supporting Subhash Palekar Natural Farming (SPNF/ZBNF).
Provides financial support and training to farmers transitioning to natural farming methods.
This scheme has had variable budget allocation year to year -- always verify current status.

### Benefit Amount (verify live -- historically variable)
- Approximately Rs 2,500 to Rs 5,000 per Vigha per year (verify current rate)
- Training programmes at no cost
- Exposure visits to successful natural farms
- Access to ZBNF resource persons (Jeevamrut preparation guidance, etc.)

### Eligibility
- Gujarat farmer with own or leased agricultural land
- Committed to SPNF/ZBNF methods (no synthetic inputs)
- Registration with district agriculture office typically required

### Documents Required
- [ ] Aadhaar card
- [ ] 7/12 Utara
- [ ] Declaration of ZBNF commitment
- [ ] Photo with farm

### Where to Apply
- Taluka Agriculture Office, Keshod
- District Agriculture Office, Junagadh
- ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in (check for current online application)

### Important Caveat
This scheme has been actively funded in some years and dormant in others
depending on Gujarat state budget priorities. Always search for current status
before planning your income around it. If active: register immediately.
If dormant: check the PKVY route which provides similar support through central funds.

## 5. ATMA -- AGRICULTURAL TECHNOLOGY MANAGEMENT AGENCY {#5-atma}

**VERIFY LIVE search query:** `ATMA scheme Gujarat 2026 farmer training organic`
**Also search:** `ATMA Junagadh district farmer registration 2026`

**Change indicators:** ATMA restructured under new scheme name, district ATMA office
contact changed, training programme calendar updated.

### What It Is
Government agency facilitating farmer training, technology transfer, and demonstration.
Present in every district. Operates through Block Technology Teams (BTT) and
Farmer Advisory Committees (FAC).

### Benefit
- Free training programmes (organic farming, ZBNF, soil health, water management)
- Farmer field school participation
- Exposure visits to model farms in Gujarat and other states (travel subsidised)
- Demonstration farm recognition -- your farm can become an ATMA demonstration site
- Connects you to KVK, agriculture universities, and government resource persons
- No cash benefit but significant in-kind value (training, network, recognition)

### Eligibility
Any farmer. No minimum area. Register with the district ATMA.

### Where to Register
- District ATMA Office, Junagadh
- Block Technology Team at Taluka Agriculture Office, Keshod
- KVK Junagadh often coordinates ATMA programmes

### Why This Matters Beyond Training
ATMA demonstration farm recognition gives your farm:
- Official government endorsement as a model natural farm
- Visibility to agriculture department officials (helps with all other scheme applications)
- Platform to invite other farmers -- advances your community education mission
- Your farm visits become potentially funded through ATMA's farmer exposure programme

Register with ATMA early (before Kharif 2026 if possible).
The process is simple -- visit the Taluka Agriculture Office and ask to register
as an interested farmer for training and demonstration activities.

## 6. KISAN CREDIT CARD (KCC) {#6-kcc}

**VERIFY LIVE search query:** `Kisan Credit Card 2026 interest rate eligibility small farmer`
**Also search:** `KCC modified interest subvention scheme 2025 2026`

**Change indicators:** Interest subvention rate changed (currently 3% subvention bringing
effective rate to 4%), credit limit revised, new KCC for animal husbandry announced,
PM Kisan link to KCC changed.

### What It Is
Revolving credit facility for farmers to meet crop cultivation expenses.
Backed by Central Government interest subvention -- makes it the cheapest
institutional credit available to farmers in India.

### Benefit
- Credit limit: up to Rs 3 lakh for crop loans (higher for allied activities)
- Interest rate: 7% per annum nominal, but Central Government pays 3% subvention
- Effective interest rate: 4% per annum (provided repayment within due date)
- Additional 3% incentive for prompt repayment: effective rate drops to 1% (verify live)
- Revolving credit -- repay after harvest, redraw before next season

### Eligibility
- Individual farmer or joint farmer with agricultural land
- 7/12 Utara in applicant's name (or family member with joint farming arrangement)
- No overdue loans at the applying bank
- Credit history check (but no prior credit required)

### Documents Required
- [ ] Aadhaar card
- [ ] 7/12 Utara (land record)
- [ ] Passbook of existing bank account (or new account at applying bank)
- [ ] Passport-size photograph
- [ ] Address proof
- [ ] Two references (neighbours or known persons at bank)

### Where to Apply
Any nationalised bank (State Bank of India, Bank of Baroda, Bank of India,
Punjab National Bank) with a branch in Keshod or Junagadh.
Also: regional rural banks (Baroda Gujarat Gramin Bank).
NABARD has simplified the KCC process -- branches are familiar with it.

### Practical Use for Your Farm
Rs 3 lakh at 4% for a 12-month crop cycle costs Rs 12,000 in interest.
This covers: cow dung procurement, Dhaincha seed, APMC transport, ZBNF input
materials, any emergency irrigation cost, and harvest-season labour.
Much cheaper than informal credit (sahukar) at 18-36%.
Apply before March 2026 to have credit available for Kharif 2026 sowing.

## 7. NHM / MIDH -- HORTICULTURE AND DRIP IRRIGATION {#7-nhm-midh}

**VERIFY LIVE search query:** `NHM MIDH Gujarat 2026 drip irrigation subsidy horticulture`
**Also search:** `ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in drip irrigation subsidy 2026 small farmer`

**Change indicators:** MIDH restructured (was NHM + HMS), subsidy percentage changed,
new component for organic horticulture added, drip irrigation component budget exhausted
for current year.

### What It Is
National Horticulture Mission (NHM) / Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture
(MIDH) provides subsidies for horticulture infrastructure including drip irrigation.

### Benefit
- Drip irrigation system: 55% subsidy for small and marginal farmers (verify current %)
- Micro-sprinkler: similar subsidy
- Horticulture crop-specific support: vegetable cultivation, spice crops, medicinal plants
- Post-harvest infrastructure (storage): 50% subsidy

For your farm (drip irrigation for 3 Vigha):
- Total drip system cost: approximately Rs 40,000-80,000 depending on specification
- After 55% subsidy: Rs 18,000-36,000 net cost

### Eligibility
- Smallholder farmer (under 2 hectares -- you qualify)
- Horticulture or vegetable crop must be grown (vegetables, spices, medicinal plants qualify)
- Drip irrigation installed by empanelled supplier (supplier list at ikhedut portal)

### Why Year 3+ Timing
You need horticulture crops growing (or firmly planned) to justify the drip
infrastructure application. Year 3 rotation plan includes vegetables on P3.
Install drip before that season with MIDH subsidy.
Additionally, drip irrigation with solar pump (PM-KUSUM) is the ideal combination:
apply PM-KUSUM in Year 1, drip in Year 3. Together they create a water-efficient,
grid-independent irrigation system appropriate for a demonstration farm.

### Where to Apply
- ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in (MIDH / NHM component)
- District Horticulture Officer, Junagadh
- Apply October-November for installation before Rabi season

## 8. HOW TO IDENTIFY NEWLY ANNOUNCED SCHEMES {#8-new-schemes}

Government announces new schemes at multiple points each year. These are the
trigger events to run a fresh search even if no specific scheme was asked about:

**Union Budget (February each year):**
Search: `Union Budget 2026 new agriculture scheme farmer organic natural`
New central schemes are typically announced here.

**Gujarat State Budget (March each year):**
Search: `Gujarat state budget 2026 agriculture farmer scheme announcement`
Gujarat-specific new schemes and funding allocations announced here.

**Kharif / Rabi season notifications (April-May and October-November):**
Search: `Gujarat agriculture department circular 2026 Kharif scheme`
Many schemes open their application windows with seasonal announcements.

**i-Khedut portal scan (quarterly):**
Visit ikhedut.gujarat.gov.in and look at the "New Schemes" or "All Schemes" section.
Any scheme listed there that is not in this skill's ref file should be presented
to the user with a note: "New scheme found on i-Khedut not yet in skill records."

**Key search terms for discovering new schemes:**
- `ikhedut new scheme Gujarat farmer 2026`
- `agri.gujarat.gov.in new yojana 2026`
- `Gujarat CM agriculture announcement 2026`
- `RKVY new component Gujarat 2026`
- `PM Kisan linked scheme Gujarat 2026`

**After identifying a new scheme:**
Present its name, benefit, and source URL to the user.
Describe what is known from search results.
Recommend verifying directly at the portal or Taluka Agriculture Office before applying.
Do not present unverified benefit amounts as confirmed -- prefix with "reportedly" if
source is a news article rather than an official government portal.
