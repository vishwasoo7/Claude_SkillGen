# 02 -- Crop Profiles and Intercropping Combinations
## 10 Keshod-Calibrated Crops + Proven Companion Planting for Saurashtra

All profiles calibrated for alkaline loamy-clay soil, pH 7.8-8.3, Keshod Taluka,
Junagadh district. Yield estimates are for ZBNF natural farming, not chemical agriculture.
Year 1 yields will be lower than stated. Year 3+ yields approach or exceed stated ranges.

## KHARIF CROP PROFILES

### BAJRA (Pearl Millet) {#bajra}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | June 15 to July 15 (after first monsoon rains) |
| Harvest window | September to October (85-110 days) |
| Seed rate | 1.5-2 kg/Vigha |
| Spacing | 45 x 15 cm or broadcast (broadcast for fodder/cover use) |
| Water need | Low. Survives 7-10 days without irrigation (water-irrigation-emergency ref01). |
| Soil impact | Deep fibrous roots (2-3 ft) break compaction. Heavy straw biomass for mulch. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut seed treatment. Jeevamrut fortnightly 200L/Vigha. |
| ZBNF yield | 3-6 quintals/Vigha (grain) + 15-25 quintals/Vigha straw |
| Soil preference | Very tolerant. Grows in pH 7-8.5. Tolerates poor drainage. |
| Rotation role | Pioneer crop. Best for Year 1 compacted soil. Breaks ground for subsequent crops. |
| APMC notes | Sold at APMC Keshod. MSP floor applies. Not a premium crop but stable. |
| Risk | Low. Most forgiving crop for a recovering chemical farm. |

**Keshod-specific notes:** Bajra is the safest Year 1 Kharif choice on chemically abused soil.
Straw value is high -- do not sell or burn. Chop and leave as mulch on the parcel.
One Vigha of Bajra produces enough straw to mulch the rest of the farm.

### SESAME (Til) {#sesame}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | June 20 to July 10 (requires warm, moist seedbed) |
| Harvest window | September to October (75-90 days) |
| Seed rate | 1-1.5 kg/Vigha (mix with sand for even broadcast) |
| Spacing | 30 x 10 cm or broadcast thinned to 10 cm |
| Water need | Moderate-low. Drought tolerant once established. Sensitive to waterlogging. |
| Soil impact | Light shallow roots. Does not compact or loosen significantly. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut. Jeevamrut fortnightly. Neemastra if pod borer appears. |
| ZBNF yield | 1.5-3 quintals/Vigha |
| Soil preference | Well-drained. pH 7-8. Struggles if EC above 1.0 dS/m. |
| Rotation role | Income crop. Best placed after a legume or Bajra (nitrogen and structure ready). |
| APMC notes | High value at APMC Junagadh. Peak price October-November post-harvest. |
| Risk | Moderate in Year 1 if soil is compacted or EC elevated. |

**Keshod-specific notes:** Sesame is your second-highest Kharif income crop after Groundnut.
Avoid planting in low-lying spots where water pools -- Sesame roots rot quickly.
After Sesame harvest: the parcel is lightly worked and ideal for Rabi sowing with minimal prep.

### COWPEA (Lobia) {#cowpea}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | June 20 to July 20 |
| Harvest window | August to September (60-75 days for grain; 45-55 days for green pods) |
| Seed rate | 5-6 kg/Vigha (grain); 8-10 kg/Vigha (green fodder/cover) |
| Spacing | 45 x 20 cm for grain; broadcast for cover |
| Water need | Low-moderate. Tolerates 5-8 days without irrigation. |
| Soil impact | N fixation 15-20 kg/Vigha. Root nodules active from 3 weeks. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut. Light Jeevamrut (biology from N fixation reduces need). |
| ZBNF yield | 2-4 quintals/Vigha (dry grain) |
| Soil preference | Wide tolerance. Handles pH up to 8.5. Tolerates moderate salinity. |
| Rotation role | Core legume for Kharif rotation. Should appear somewhere on farm every Kharif. |
| APMC notes | APMC Keshod and Junagadh. Green pods sell well locally for vegetables. |
| Risk | Very low. Reliable performer on degraded soil. |

**Keshod-specific notes:** Cowpea is the workhorse of the Year 1-2 rotation.
Its N fixation directly reduces Jeevamrut requirement on the following crop.
Consider harvesting 50% for grain income and incorporating 50% as green manure.

### CLUSTER BEAN (Guar) {#cluster-bean}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | June 25 to July 15 |
| Harvest window | September to October (75-90 days) |
| Seed rate | 6-8 kg/Vigha |
| Spacing | 45 x 15 cm |
| Water need | Very low. Extremely drought tolerant. Grows in 300-500mm rainfall conditions. |
| Soil impact | N fixation 10-15 kg/Vigha. Galactomannan root exudates improve soil aggregation. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut. Minimal Jeevamrut (self-sufficient in N). |
| ZBNF yield | 3-5 quintals/Vigha (dry pods) |
| Soil preference | Sandy loam to loamy-clay. pH 7-8.5. Highly tolerant of poor conditions. |
| Rotation role | Supporting legume. Good intercrop filler. Thrives where Cowpea might struggle. |
| APMC notes | Guar seed has industrial demand (guar gum export). APMC Junagadh. |
| Risk | Very low. Performs even when other crops struggle. |

### GROUNDNUT (Moongfali) {#groundnut}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | June 15 to July 10 (prefers early sowing for full pod fill before dry spell) |
| Harvest window | September to October (90-110 days) |
| Seed rate | 30-35 kg/Vigha (shelled seed) |
| Spacing | 30 x 10 cm |
| Water need | Moderate. Critical at pegging and pod fill stages. 4-6 day drought tolerance. |
| Soil impact | N fixation 20-30 kg/Vigha (highest Kharif legume). Deep peg action loosens subsoil. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut. Jeevamrut fortnightly. Gypsum 20 kg/Vigha at pegging (even in ZBNF). |
| ZBNF yield | 4-8 quintals/Vigha (pods) |
| Soil preference | Well-drained loamy soil. pH 6.5-8.0. Struggles above pH 8.2 (Ca lockout). |
| Rotation role | High-value Kharif crop. Suited for Years 2-5 after soil recovery is underway. |
| APMC notes | APMC Junagadh (larger market, better price discovery for Groundnut). |
| Risk | Moderate-high in Year 1 if pH above 8.2 or EC above 1.0. Best from Year 2. |

**Keshod-specific notes:** Junagadh district is one of India's leading Groundnut regions.
Quality expectations are high from traders. Invest in Beejamrut seed treatment -- it matters
for uniformity. Haulm (leaves and stems) is valuable cattle fodder -- good barter asset
with the farming family who provides cow dung.

## RABI CROP PROFILES

### CUMIN (Jeera) {#cumin}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | October 25 to November 20 (temperature must drop below 28C at night) |
| Harvest window | February to March (90-110 days from sowing) |
| Seed rate | 3-4 kg/Vigha |
| Spacing | Broadcast; thin to 10 x 5 cm if possible |
| Water need | Low-moderate. 6-9 days drought tolerance. Critical at germination and seed fill. |
| Soil impact | Light shallow roots. Low biomass return. Soil-demanding, not soil-building. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut (essential -- wilt prevention starts at seed). Jeevamrut every 10 days. Panchgavya at flowering. |
| ZBNF yield | 1-2.5 quintals/Vigha (with soil recovery; Year 1 expect 0.5-1 quintal) |
| Soil preference | Well-drained. pH 7-8. OC above 0.5% strongly preferred. EC below 0.75 dS/m. |
| Rotation role | Primary income crop at maturity. Should follow a legume or green manure crop. |
| APMC notes | APMC Junagadh (national price discovery; traders from across India present at peak). |
| Risk | High in Year 1 if soil is not ready. Fusarium wilt is the main threat on stressed soil. |

**Keshod-specific notes:** Cumin is the farm's long-term anchor crop.
Every rotation decision in Years 1-3 is oriented toward making the soil ready for Cumin.
Do not rush it. One failed Cumin crop due to unready soil costs more (seed, irrigation, time)
than the income from a successful legume crop that builds the soil for next season's Cumin.
Wilt (Fusarium oxysporum): do not grow Cumin on the same parcel two consecutive Rabi seasons.
Always follow a Kharif crop on any parcel before replanting Cumin there.

### METHI (Fenugreek) {#methi}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | October 15 to November 30 |
| Harvest window | December to January (green leaves: 40-55 days; seed: 90-110 days) |
| Seed rate | 20-25 kg/Vigha (dense broadcast for leaf harvest) |
| Water need | Low. Very cold-tolerant. Drought tolerant once established. |
| Soil impact | N fixation 10-15 kg/Vigha. Light root. Excellent gap-filler. |
| ZBNF inputs | Minimal. Beejamrut. One Jeevamrut application at 21 days. |
| ZBNF yield | 4-6 quintals/Vigha (leaves, multiple cuts); 1.5-2.5 quintals/Vigha (seed) |
| Soil preference | Very wide tolerance. Grows in poor, alkaline soil. Reliable on recovering land. |
| Rotation role | Resting and N-building crop. Use when a parcel needs a low-stress season. |
| APMC notes | Good local market for seed at APMC Keshod. Leaf sales direct or local. |
| Risk | Very low. Best recovery crop if another Rabi failed. |

### CORIANDER (Dhaniya) {#coriander}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | October 15 to November 15 |
| Harvest window | December to February (60-80 days for green; 90-110 days for seed) |
| Seed rate | 12-15 kg/Vigha (split seed for better germination) |
| Water need | Moderate. Sensitive to water stress at germination. |
| Soil impact | Very light shallow root. Minimal biomass. Resting crop. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut. Jeevamrut at 21 days and 45 days. |
| ZBNF yield | 1.5-2.5 quintals/Vigha (seed); green bundles separately |
| Soil preference | Well-drained. pH 6.5-8.0. Prefers moderate fertility. |
| Rotation role | Income diversifier. Use alongside Methi or Cumin, not as primary crop. |
| APMC notes | Dual market: green bundles sell locally; seed at APMC. |
| Risk | Low. Unremarkable on soil but also undemanding. |

### CHICKPEA (Chana) {#chickpea}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | October 20 to November 20 |
| Harvest window | February to March (90-120 days) |
| Seed rate | 20-25 kg/Vigha |
| Spacing | 30 x 10 cm |
| Water need | Low. One of the most drought-tolerant Rabi crops. |
| Soil impact | N fixation 40-50 kg/Vigha (highest of any Rabi crop). Deep tap root. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut. One Jeevamrut at 21 days. Rhizobium inoculant at seed (even in ZBNF). |
| ZBNF yield | 3-6 quintals/Vigha |
| Soil preference | Well-drained. Wide pH tolerance. Excellent on alkaline loamy-clay. |
| Rotation role | Soil-building Rabi anchor. Appears every 2 seasons on each parcel ideally. |
| APMC notes | Stable demand at APMC Keshod and Junagadh. MSP support. |
| Risk | Very low. Performs well across all recovery stages. |

**Keshod-specific notes:** Chickpea is the nitrogen bank of the rotation.
When Cumin is not planned for a parcel: grow Chickpea instead.
The N it deposits is directly usable by the following Kharif crop without any additional input.
Do not burn the residue -- chop and incorporate or use as surface mulch.

### ISABGOL (Psyllium Husk) {#isabgol}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | November 1 to November 20 (strict -- later sowing drops yield sharply) |
| Harvest window | February to March (90-100 days) |
| Seed rate | 4-5 kg/Vigha |
| Spacing | Broadcast; light rake to cover |
| Water need | Very low after establishment. Extremely sensitive to excess moisture at maturity. |
| Soil impact | Very light root. Minimal biomass. High-value cash crop with low soil demand. |
| ZBNF inputs | Beejamrut. Jeevamrut at 21 days. No foliar sprays at maturity (contaminates husk). |
| ZBNF yield | 2-4 quintals/Vigha (husk value crop) |
| Soil preference | Well-drained, low-organic soil acceptable. pH 7-8.5. Do not waterlog. |
| Rotation role | Premium income diversifier in Years 3-5. Do not introduce before soil is stable. |
| APMC notes | Specialty market -- APMC Junagadh or direct to processing units. Verify buyer before sowing. |
| Risk | Moderate. Timing-sensitive. Weather risk at harvest (rain spoils husk). Trial 0.5 Vigha first. |

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|

### MUSTARD (Rai/Sarson) {#mustard}

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| Sowing window | October to November (or any gap between seasons) |
| Use as gap crop | 30-45 day cycle before Rabi; incorporate or harvest leaf |
| Seed rate | 1-1.5 kg/Vigha |
| Soil impact | Biofumigant: glucosinolate breakdown suppresses Fusarium and soil pathogens |
| OC contribution | Modest -- primary value is pest/pathogen suppression |
| Income option | Seed harvest possible if allowed to full maturity (60-70 days) |
| Rotation role | Use before Cumin on parcels with wilt history. Use in gaps. Allelopathic for weeds. |

## PROVEN INTERCROPPING COMBINATIONS

All combinations validated for Saurashtra climate and loamy-clay soil.
Row ratios listed are starting points -- adjust to your irrigation capacity.

| Combination | Ratio | Season | What it achieves | Key note |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bajra + Cowpea | 2:1 rows | Kharif | Bajra gives structure, Cowpea fixes N. Combined yield higher than monocrop. | Classic Saurashtra companion. Very low risk. |
| Sesame + Cowpea border | Main + border rows | Kharif | Cowpea border fixes N at edge, reduces Sesame N demand. | Keep Cowpea border 1 row wide only -- Sesame is light-sensitive. |
| Groundnut + Bajra | 3:1 rows | Kharif | Bajra windbreak protects Groundnut. Bajra roots prevent waterlogging stress on Groundnut. | Works well in exposed fields with wind. |
| Cumin + Fennel | 6:2 rows | Rabi | Fennel taller rows shelter Cumin from cold wind. Slight income addition. | Fennel competes for moisture -- only use if borewell reliable. |
| Methi + Coriander | 50:50 broadcast | Rabi | Both short-cycle, compatible root zones, similar irrigation needs. | Harvest methi leaves early (Day 45), let coriander go to seed. |
| Chickpea + Mustard | 4:1 rows | Rabi | Mustard biofumigant reduces Chickpea soil pathogens. Minor income from mustard. | Keep Mustard rows sparse -- Chickpea should dominate. |
| Bajra + Cluster Bean | 2:1 rows | Kharif | Both extremely drought-tolerant. Cluster Bean N feeds Bajra. Best for water-scarce years. | Ideal combination if borewell reliability is uncertain. |

## SUCCESSION WINDOWS -- KHARIF TO RABI TRANSITIONS

The gap between Kharif harvest and Rabi sowing is critical.
What happens in this 2-6 week window determines Rabi crop quality.

| Kharif crop harvested | Latest Rabi sowing date | Recommended gap action |
|---|---|---|
| Bajra (October) | November 20 (Cumin) | Chop Bajra straw into mulch. Apply Ghanjeevamrut. Rest 10 days. Sow. |
| Sesame (September-October) | November 20 (Cumin, Methi) | Light parcel preparation. Sesame leaves soil in good condition for Rabi. |
| Cowpea (August-September) | November 15 (Cumin, Methi, Chickpea) | Cowpea root nodules continue N release 3-4 weeks after harvest. Minimal prep needed. |
| Groundnut (October) | November 25 (Chickpea, Methi) | Leave Groundnut haulm on parcel as mulch. Soil N is high -- Chickpea benefits directly. |
| Cluster Bean (September-October) | November 15 | Short harvest-to-sow window possible. Parcel ready quickly. |

**Window narrowing risk:** If Kharif is delayed by late rains, Rabi sowing window compresses.
Cumin is the most sensitive -- sowing after November 20 loses yield to rising temperatures.
In a compressed window year: prioritise Cumin sowing on the most ready parcel first,
even if that parcel's Kharif crop is still standing on other parcels.
