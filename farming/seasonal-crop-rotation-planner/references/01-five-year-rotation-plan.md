# 01 -- Five-Year Crop Rotation Plan
## Keshod Farm, Saurashtra -- Season-by-Season from Kharif 2026 to Rabi 2030-31

All plans are per 3-Vigha farm divided into three parcels of approximately 1 Vigha each.
Parcel labels used: P1, P2, P3 (assign to your actual field sections as you prefer).
All plans assume alkaline loamy-clay soil, single borewell, ZBNF inputs only.

## HOW TO READ THE ROTATION TABLES

Each season table shows:
- What to plant on each of the 3 parcels
- The soil purpose of that planting (income / N-fixation / detox / structure)
- The income expectation (realistic for chemical-transition farm)
- The soil recovery action happening underneath the income crop

**Soil recovery status key:**
- Stage 1: Biology largely inactive, herbicide residue possible, OC 0.2-0.4%
- Stage 2: Biology activating, first earthworms appearing, OC 0.4-0.6%
- Stage 3: Structure improving, earthworm count rising, OC 0.6-0.8%
- Stage 4: Near healthy baseline, OC 0.8-1.0%+, EC normalised

## YEAR 1

### Soil recovery status entering Year 1: Stage 1
Previous use: heavy synthetic inputs (Kali Jeeri). Biology disrupted. Compaction likely.
Herbicide residue possible in patches. EC may be elevated.
Strategy: bioremediation first, modest income second. Every crop choice is a soil action.

### Kharif 2026 (June to October)

**Pre-sowing (April-May, immediately after Kali Jeeri harvest)**
Before any main crop: apply Dhaincha green manure on all 3 parcels.
Sow Dhaincha at 5 kg/Vigha. Incorporate at 40-45 days. Rest 15 days. Then sow main crop.
This one action delivers: N fixation (25-30 kg N/Vigha), compaction break, OC start, herbicide residue reduction.

| Parcel | Main Crop | Area | Soil Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Bajra (Pearl Millet) | 1 Vigha | Deep roots break compaction. Highly drought-tolerant. Year 1 safe choice. | Intercrop with Cowpea (see ref02). Bajra stalks become mulch post-harvest. |
| P2 | Cowpea (Lobia) | 0.6 Vigha | N fixation (15 kg N/Vigha). Tolerates salt stress. Forgiving on degraded soil. | Fill remaining 0.4 Vigha with Cluster Bean (Guar) for additional N + income |
| P3 | Sesame (Til) | 1 Vigha | Light root, low input, tolerates poor soil. High APMC value per kg. | Riskier in Year 1 if soil is severely compacted -- monitor germination closely |

**Year 1 Kharif income estimate (realistic, not optimistic):**
- Bajra: 3-5 quintals/Vigha x Rs 26-32/kg = Rs 7,800-16,000
- Cowpea: 2-3 quintals/Vigha x Rs 55-80/kg = Rs 11,000-24,000
- Sesame: 1.5-2.5 quintals/Vigha x Rs 85-120/kg = Rs 12,750-30,000
- Total range (3 Vigha): Rs 31,550-70,000
- Expect lower end in Year 1. Year 2 will be meaningfully higher.

**Soil actions happening this Kharif:**
- Dhaincha pre-crop fixed N, broke compaction, added first OC pulse
- Jeevamrut (fortnightly) reactivating soil biology
- Mulch (Bajra straw + any available dry material) covering all bare ground
- Cowpea fixing additional N through root nodules throughout season

### Rabi 2026-27 (November to March)

| Parcel | Main Crop | Area | Soil Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Methi (Fenugreek) | 0.5 Vigha | Short cycle (60-70 days). Tolerant. Tests P1 soil response after Bajra. | Fill other 0.5 Vigha: Coriander or Chickpea |
| P2 | Cumin (Jeera) | 0.5 Vigha | First Cumin test on P2 which had Cowpea (best N status). Low area, low risk. | If germination is poor (herbicide patches): sow Methi on failed spots immediately |
| P3 | Chickpea (Chana) | 1 Vigha | N fixation (40-50 kg N/Vigha -- highest of any Rabi crop). Builds P3 for Year 2. | Chickpea residue is excellent organic matter. Do not burn. Chop and incorporate. |

**Year 1 Rabi income estimate:**
- Methi: 1.5-2 quintals/Vigha x Rs 60-100/kg = Rs 9,000-20,000
- Cumin (0.5 Vigha): 0.5-0.8 quintals x Rs 280-350/kg = Rs 14,000-28,000
- Chickpea: 3-4 quintals/Vigha x Rs 45-70/kg = Rs 13,500-28,000
- Total range (3 Vigha): Rs 36,500-76,000

**Get a soil test at end of Rabi 2026-27.** Compare OC, EC, N to your April 2026 baseline.
This test tells you whether the Year 1 strategy worked and calibrates Years 2-3.

## YEAR 2

### Soil recovery status entering Year 2: Stage 2 (expected)
Biology should be activating. First earthworms visible. Soil smell improving.
OC should have moved from 0.2-0.4% toward 0.4-0.6%.
EC should be dropping. If EC still above 1.0: add gypsum before Kharif 2027.

### Kharif 2027 (June to October)

**Rotation logic:** Move each crop to the parcel where the previous crop built the most for it.
Cumin goes to P1 in Rabi (after Bajra broke P1 compaction + Methi added short biomass).
Legume stays on P3 (building on Chickpea's N legacy).

| Parcel | Main Crop | Area | Why This Parcel Now | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Sesame (Til) | 1 Vigha | P1 had Bajra (deep roots loosened) + Methi (light fallow). Ready for Sesame. | Intercrop with a short-cycle Cowpea strip along borders |
| P2 | Bajra + Cowpea | 1 Vigha | P2 had Cowpea Kharif + Cumin Rabi. Bajra deepens P2 roots further. | Bajra:Cowpea ratio 70:30. Cowpea in alternating rows. |
| P3 | Groundnut | 1 Vigha | P3 had Chickpea (high N). Groundnut is N-demanding but Chickpea's legacy covers it. | Year 2 first Groundnut trial. Avoid if EC still above 1.0 dS/m. |

**Year 2 Kharif income estimate:**
- Sesame (P1): 2-3.5 quintals/Vigha x Rs 90-130/kg = Rs 18,000-45,500
- Bajra (P2, 0.7 Vigha): 3.5-5.5 quintals x Rs 26-32/kg = Rs 9,100-17,600
- Cowpea (P2, 0.3 Vigha): 1.5-2.5 quintals x Rs 55-80/kg = Rs 8,250-20,000
- Groundnut (P3): 4-7 quintals/Vigha x Rs 55-85/kg = Rs 22,000-59,500
- Total range: Rs 57,350-142,600 (significant improvement over Year 1)

### Rabi 2027-28 (November to March)

| Parcel | Main Crop | Area | Why This Parcel Now | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Cumin (Jeera) | 1 Vigha | P1 fully prepared: Bajra compaction-break -> Sesame light touch -> Cumin. | Full Vigha Cumin for first time. Monitor closely -- soil at critical readiness stage. |
| P2 | Methi + Coriander | 1 Vigha | P2 had Bajra/Cowpea. Light Rabi crop rests P2 before heavier Kharif 2028. | 0.5 Vigha each or as preferred. Both short cycle, easy market. |
| P3 | Chickpea | 1 Vigha | P3 had Groundnut (N-demanding). Chickpea rebuilds N before Year 3 rotation. | Chickpea every 2 years on a parcel maintains N without purchased inputs. |

**Year 2 Rabi income estimate:**
- Cumin (P1, 1 Vigha): 1.5-2.5 quintals x Rs 280-350/kg = Rs 42,000-87,500
- Methi + Coriander: 2-3.5 quintals/Vigha x Rs 60-140/kg = Rs 12,000-49,000
- Chickpea: 3-5 quintals/Vigha x Rs 45-70/kg = Rs 13,500-35,000
- Total range: Rs 67,500-171,500

## YEAR 3

### Soil recovery status entering Year 3: Stage 3 (expected)
Soil structure visibly improving. Earthworm count 3-5 per sq ft.
OC approaching 0.6-0.75%. EC normalised on most parcels.
This is the shift year: soil stops being the constraint and starts being an asset.

### Kharif 2028 (June to October)

| Parcel | Main Crop | Area | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Groundnut | 1 Vigha | P1's first Groundnut trial. P1 now has 2 years of recovery. High value if quality is good. |
| P2 | Sesame + Cowpea | 1 Vigha | P2 rested last Rabi (Methi). Sesame benefits from the light touch. Cowpea border rows. |
| P3 | Bajra | 0.5 Vigha | P3 deepened by Chickpea roots. Bajra gives structural diversity and straw for mulch. |
| P3 | Cluster Bean (Guar) | 0.5 Vigha | Guar gum root exudates continue improving P3 aggregation. APMC Keshod market. |

### Rabi 2028-29 (November to March)

| Parcel | Main Crop | Area | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | Cumin | 1 Vigha | Second year of Cumin on P1. Soil confirmed ready. Monitor for wilt (Fusarium) -- avoid if seen. |
| P2 | Cumin | 0.5 Vigha | First Cumin on P2. P2's biology is strong now (2 years of legumes + Bajra/Cowpea). |
| P2 | Isabgol (Psyllium) | 0.5 Vigha | Trial crop. High value specialty. Thrives in Saurashtra cool-dry Rabi. |
| P3 | Methi + Chickpea | 1 Vigha | P3 rests from Kharif intensity. Chickpea rebuilds N. Methi for income. |

**Year 3 note:** By Rabi 2028-29, 2 of 3 parcels are carrying Cumin. This is only viable because
the previous 2 years of legumes and green manure have genuinely rebuilt N and structure.
Do not accelerate this -- if soil tests at start of Year 3 still show OC below 0.5%,
keep P2 in Chickpea + Methi Rabi and push the second Cumin parcel to Year 4.

## YEAR 5

### Soil recovery status: Demonstration Farm Maturity
Soil is the farm's primary asset. Organic matter is self-sustaining with ZBNF maintenance.
PGS certificate in second or third renewal. INATrace QR codes generating premium sales.

### Kharif 2030 (June to October)

At Year 5, the rotation is stable and self-reinforcing. The basic 3-parcel cycle is:
- One parcel: Sesame or Groundnut (income Kharif)
- One parcel: Bajra + Legume (structure maintenance)
- One parcel: Vegetables or specialty crop (premium market exploration)

Adjust each Kharif based on:
- Soil test from previous Rabi (cross-reference soil-test-interpreter)
- APMC price forecast for the coming season (cross-reference agstack ref10)
- Water availability outlook (cross-reference water-irrigation-emergency for planning mode)

### Rabi 2030-31 (November to March)

Stable rotation at maturity:
- Two parcels: Cumin (confirmed high-performing on your specific soil by now)
- One parcel: rotates between Chickpea (N maintenance), Isabgol, Methi, Coriander

**The Year 5 principle:** You are no longer recovering soil. You are maintaining it.
The intervention intensity decreases (Jeevamrut every 21 days vs every 10-14 in Year 1).
The income intensity increases. This is the payoff of the 5-year investment.

## BETWEEN-SEASON GAP CROPS

Any time there is a 3-6 week gap between Kharif harvest and Rabi sowing:

| Gap duration | Best gap crop | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 2-3 weeks | Mustard | Biofumigant -- suppresses soil pathogens. Incorporate before Rabi sow. |
| 3-5 weeks | Methi (early harvest variety) | Fastest income gap crop. 45-55 day cycle if harvested as green leaves. |
| 4-6 weeks | Dhaincha | Biomass addition + N fixation before any Rabi crop. Best gap investment. |
| Any length | Mulch application | If no gap crop: heavy mulch immediately after harvest. Never leave bare soil. |

## ADAPTING THE PLAN WHEN SOMETHING GOES WRONG

### Crop failure mid-season

Do not leave the parcel bare. Sow a gap/cover crop on the failed area within 7 days.
Options in order of speed:
1. Cowpea (fastest establishment, any season, any parcel)
2. Methi (Rabi or post-monsoon, 45-day cycle)
3. Dhaincha (pre-monsoon or early monsoon, best biomass return)

### Soil test shows recovery is slower than expected

Do not accelerate the income crop timeline. Instead:
- Add a full green manure season on the lagging parcel (Dhaincha, no income crop)
- Increase Jeevamrut frequency on that parcel (every 10 days instead of 14)
- Cross-reference soil-test-interpreter ref06 for specific amendment prescription
- Delay Cumin on that parcel by one season

### Drought / borewell failure during critical growth stage

Immediate triage using water-irrigation-emergency skill.
For rotation implications: if a Kharif crop is lost to drought, treat it as a failed season.
Sow Rabi as normal -- the soil still received some Jeevamrut and green manure benefit
even from a failed Kharif crop. Do not abandon the rotation plan.

### Pest or disease on a rotation crop

If a crop suffers severe pest pressure that requires removing it:
- Do not replant the same crop on that parcel in the following season
- Give the parcel a rest season: cover crop only, no income crop
- Cross-reference vedic-farming for ZBNF pest remedies before abandoning

### APMC price collapse for a planned crop

Check APMC price for your planned Rabi crop before sowing (October check).
If Cumin price is very low: shift that parcel to Chickpea + Methi.
Chickpea is soil-building and always has a stable local market.
Never grow a high-input crop at low price -- the margin disappears.
