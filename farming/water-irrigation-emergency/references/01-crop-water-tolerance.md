## 1. CROP SURVIVAL WINDOWS

How long each crop survives without any irrigation at all.
Assumes: alkaline loamy-clay soil (Keshod type), soil was adequately irrigated before outage.

### Kharif Crops (June to October)

| Crop | Days Survives Without Irrigation | Stress Signs Appear At | Point of No Return |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bajra (Pearl Millet) | 7 to 10 days | Day 5 to 6 | Day 9 to 11 |
| Sesame (Til) | 5 to 7 days | Day 3 to 4 | Day 7 to 8 |
| Cowpea (Lobia) | 5 to 8 days | Day 4 to 5 | Day 8 to 9 |
| Cluster Bean (Guar) | 6 to 9 days | Day 5 to 6 | Day 9 to 10 |
| Groundnut | 4 to 6 days | Day 3 | Day 6 to 7 |
| Cotton | 5 to 7 days | Day 4 | Day 7 to 8 |
| Dhaincha / cover crop | 8 to 12 days | Day 7 | Day 12+ |
| Seedlings (any crop, under 30 DAS) | 2 to 3 days | Day 1 to 2 | Day 3 to 4 |

### Rabi Crops (November to March)

| Crop | Days Survives Without Irrigation | Stress Signs Appear At | Point of No Return |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cumin (Jeera) | 6 to 9 days | Day 5 | Day 9 to 10 |
| Methi (Fenugreek) | 7 to 10 days | Day 6 | Day 10 to 11 |
| Coriander | 6 to 8 days | Day 5 | Day 8 to 9 |
| Chickpea (Chana) | 8 to 12 days | Day 7 to 8 | Day 12+ |
| Mustard | 7 to 10 days | Day 6 | Day 10 |
| Wheat | 5 to 8 days | Day 4 | Day 8 to 9 |

### Summer / Year-Round

| Crop | Days Survives | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Vegetables (any) | 2 to 4 days | Most sensitive -- always top priority |
| Fruit trees (if any) | 10 to 20 days | Deep roots -- lower emergency priority |
| Nursery / seedling trays | 1 to 2 days | Highest priority -- no soil buffer |

**Important adjustment for season:**
- Monsoon Kharif (July-August): add 2 to 3 days to all figures above -- humidity reduces
  crop transpiration demand significantly
- Peak summer (April-June): subtract 2 days from all figures above -- heat increases
  demand and soil dries faster
- Mulched soil: add 1 to 2 days to all figures -- mulch cuts evaporation by 30 to 40%

## 2. GROWTH STAGE VULNERABILITY

Same crop behaves differently depending on its growth stage.
A crop at flowering is 3x more vulnerable than the same crop at 60+ DAS.

| Growth Stage | Days After Sowing (DAS) | Vulnerability | Missing Irrigation Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Germination / emergence | 0 to 7 DAS | Extreme | Complete crop failure possible |
| Seedling | 7 to 30 DAS | Very High | 50 to 80% stand loss if 2+ days missed |
| Vegetative growth | 30 to 60 DAS | Medium | Yield reduction 10 to 30% |
| Flower initiation | Crop-specific | Very High | 40 to 60% yield loss possible |
| Flowering | Crop-specific | Extreme | Flower drop, yield loss 50 to 80% |
| Pod fill / grain fill | Crop-specific | High | Shriveled grain, reduced weight |
| Crop maturity | Final 2 weeks | Low | Minimal -- crop can usually dry naturally |

**Key dates for Keshod crops:**
- Bajra flowering: 45 to 55 DAS
- Sesame flowering: 35 to 45 DAS
- Cowpea flowering: 40 to 50 DAS
- Cumin flowering: 60 to 75 DAS
- Groundnut pegging: 30 to 45 DAS (critical for pod formation)

## 3. TRIAGE PRIORITY BY SEASON

Use this when you cannot irrigate all 3 Vigha. Save in this order.

### Kharif Triage (June to October)

```
PRIORITY 1 (save at any cost):
  - Any crop at flowering stage
  - Seedlings under 30 DAS
  - Groundnut at pegging stage (30-45 DAS)

PRIORITY 2 (save if possible):
  - Cowpea in vegetative phase
  - Sesame in vegetative phase
  - Any crop showing first wilt signs

PRIORITY 3 (reduce water, monitor):
  - Bajra in established vegetative phase (30-60 DAS, deep roots)
  - Cluster bean in vegetative phase
  - Guar in established vegetative phase

PRIORITY 4 (defer, accept stress):
  - Dhaincha / cover crops
  - Crops past 70% maturity
  - Bare patches / fallow
```

### Rabi Triage (November to March)

```
PRIORITY 1 (save at any cost):
  - Cumin at any stage -- highest value per kg at APMC
  - Any seedling under 30 DAS
  - Flowering stage of any crop

PRIORITY 2 (save if possible):
  - Methi in active growth
  - Coriander in vegetative phase
  - Chickpea at flowering

PRIORITY 3 (reduce water, monitor):
  - Chickpea in vegetative phase (nitrogen-fixing -- also improves soil)
  - Mustard in vegetative phase

PRIORITY 4 (defer):
  - Cover crops / green manure
  - Crops at near-maturity (last 2 weeks before harvest)
```

## 4. WATER RATIONING CALCULATOR

Use when supply is limited -- e.g., one tanker load per day.

### Normal Irrigation Demand (3 Vigha full farm)

| Season | Normal Irrigation Frequency | Full Demand per Cycle (3 Vigha) |
|---|---|---|
| Peak summer (Apr-Jun) | Every 3 to 4 days | 40,000 to 50,000 L |
| Kharif active (Jul-Sep) | Every 6 to 8 days (monsoon supplement) | 20,000 to 30,000 L |
| Rabi cool season (Nov-Feb) | Every 8 to 12 days | 15,000 to 25,000 L |

### Emergency Ration Amounts (minimum to prevent crop loss)

| Crop | Minimum Ration Per Vigha (survival dose) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cumin | 800 to 1,000 L | Apply at root zone only -- cumin roots are shallow |
| Sesame | 1,000 to 1,500 L | Drench root zone, not broadcast |
| Bajra | 1,500 to 2,000 L | Deep-rooted -- ration is still large |
| Cowpea | 1,000 to 1,500 L | Tolerates partial drought if soil is moist below |
| Groundnut (at pegging) | 2,000 to 2,500 L | Do not skimp at this stage -- direct pod yield impact |
| Vegetable beds | 500 L per 100 sq m | Daily minimum -- no flexibility |
| Seedlings (any) | 300 to 500 L per Vigha | Small but non-negotiable |

**One tanker load (8,000 to 10,000 L) covers:**
- Critical crops on 1 Vigha at full ration, OR
- 3 Vigha at survival ration (bare minimum for all crops)
- Seedlings across all 3 Vigha for 2 to 3 days

## 6. RECOVERY ACTIONS AFTER WATER STRESS

When irrigation is restored after a 3+ day gap, do not irrigate heavily all at once.
Stressed plants absorb water poorly and sudden flooding can cause root rot.

### Immediate recovery (first 24 hours after irrigation restored):

1. Irrigate at 50% of normal dose first -- let soil absorb for 4 to 6 hours
2. Apply Jeevamrut at standard dose (200 L/Vigha) -- microbes help root recovery
3. Apply Panchgavya foliar spray (3 L in 100 L water) -- activates stomata, reduces
   recovery time by 30 to 40%
4. Do NOT apply any solid inputs (neem cake, vermicompost) immediately -- root zone
   is stressed and cannot process

### 48 hours after recovery:

5. Resume normal irrigation schedule
6. Apply second Jeevamrut dose
7. Check for secondary pest pressure -- stressed plants attract sucking pests within
   48 to 72 hours after water stress. Check underside of leaves.
8. If crop shows flowering delay -- apply Panchgavya again at next irrigation

### Signs that crop did not recover fully:

- Leaves remain wilted 24 hours after irrigation restored: apply heavy Panchgavya
- Flowers dropping after recovery: potassium stress secondary to water stress --
  apply wood ash 25 kg/Vigha as foliar (dissolve in water, strain, spray)
- New growth appears but old leaves stay yellow: magnesium deficiency triggered
  by stress -- apply Panchgavya + extra Jeevamrut for 3 consecutive applications

## 7. PREVENTION AND PREPAREDNESS CHECKLIST

Do these before the next irrigation season begins. Not after the emergency.

### Infrastructure

- [ ] Generator rental contact saved in phone -- call now to confirm availability and
      rate before season starts
- [ ] Two neighbor farmer contacts saved -- ask each season if they are willing to share
      borewell in emergency. Reciprocate when they need help.
- [ ] Motor mechanic contact saved -- ideally one who knows your pump model
- [ ] PGVCL Keshod complaint number saved: for power outage status
- [ ] Water tanker contractor contact: at least one number confirmed active

### On-Farm

- [ ] Motor control panel inspected before each season -- clean terminals, check capacitor
      (Rs 200 to 500 part, major cause of failures)
- [ ] Delivery pipe from borewell to field checked for leaks or blockages
- [ ] Field check valve in good condition (prevents backflow when pump stops)
- [ ] At least 3 days of mulch cover maintained at all times -- reduces emergency
      irrigation demand by 30 to 40%
- [ ] Farm journal: log each irrigation date and duration -- this tells you exactly
      how much soil moisture buffer you have when outage hits

### Financial Buffer

- [ ] Keep Rs 3,000 to 5,000 liquid and available through each Kharif peak period
      (May to August) -- this covers one tanker + generator day if needed

### Long-Term Risk Reduction (Phase 3 plan)

- [ ] Solar pump installation: eliminates power cut risk entirely. PGVCL / GGRC
      solar pump scheme covers 60 to 90% of cost for farmers. Apply at nearest
      PGVCL office or through your Gram Sevak.
- [ ] Rainwater harvesting: farm pond (khet talavdi) -- collects Kharif runoff for
      Rabi supplemental irrigation. Scheme available through Gujarat Agriculture Dept.
- [ ] Second bore point: assess with local bore rig operator if a second point is
      viable for your plot. Provides redundancy.

## 8. BOREWELL AND MOTOR MAINTENANCE CALENDAR

Preventive maintenance eliminates 60 to 70% of unplanned failures.
Schedule these every year without waiting for a problem.

### Pre-Kharif (April -- before sowing)

| Task | Who Does It | Cost Estimate | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Motor capacitor check and replacement if needed | Motor mechanic | Rs 200 to 600 | Capacitor is the #1 cause of summer pump failures |
| Control panel and starter box inspection | Motor mechanic | Rs 200 | Heat and humidity corrode terminals |
| Delivery pipe pressure test | You + mechanic | Labor only | Catches slow leaks before they become outages |
| Pump lift test: measure flow rate | Mechanic | Rs 300 to 500 | Declining flow = blocked impeller or water table drop |
| Borewell water level measurement | Mechanic with wire measure | Rs 200 to 400 | Baseline before summer -- track each year |

### Post-Kharif (October -- after monsoon)

| Task | Who Does It | Cost Estimate | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Motor winding insulation check | Mechanic | Rs 200 | Monsoon humidity can degrade winding insulation |
| Borewell silt check | Mechanic | Rs 300 to 500 | Post-monsoon silt can block lower intake |
| Pipe joint inspection | You | Nil | Check for monsoon damage |

### Annual

| Task | Who Does It | Cost Estimate |
|---|---|---|
| Full motor service (pull, clean, bearings) | Motor mechanic | Rs 1,500 to 3,000 |
| Borewell camera inspection (every 3 years) | Bore rig operator | Rs 2,000 to 4,000 |

**Total annual maintenance cost:** Rs 2,500 to 5,000
**Cost of one motor failure that could have been prevented:** Rs 8,000 to 25,000
**Return on preventive maintenance:** 3x to 5x cost avoided

## 9. LONG-TERM RISK REDUCTION -- PHASE UPGRADE PATH

The current single-borewell-on-grid-power system has three vulnerability points:
grid electricity, motor/pump, and water table depth.
Each phase below removes one failure point.

### Phase 1 (Now -- no cost)
- Establish neighbor water-sharing agreement
- Keep generator contact confirmed
- Maintain mulch at all times (reduces emergency irrigation need by 30%)
- Run preventive maintenance calendar (Section 8)

### Phase 2 (Year 2 to 3 -- moderate investment)
- Solar pump installation on existing borewell: Rs 40,000 to 80,000
  After GGRC / PGVCL subsidy (60 to 90% covered): Rs 4,000 to 16,000 net
  Eliminates power cut vulnerability entirely
- Farm pond (khet talavdi): Rs 15,000 to 50,000 depending on size
  Gujarat Govt scheme covers 50% for small and marginal farmers
  Collects monsoon runoff for Rabi supplemental irrigation

### Phase 3 (Year 4 to 5 -- farm expansion phase)
- Second borewell point: Rs 60,000 to 1,20,000
  Subsidy available under Sujalam Sufalam Jal Sanchay Yojana
  Eliminates single-point-of-failure on water source

**Priority:** Solar pump subsidy first. It is the highest-impact single action
and the subsidy window can be accessed in Year 1. Ask at PGVCL Keshod subdivision
or through your Gram Sevak for the current scheme application process.
