# Water and Irrigation Emergency Protocol
## Keshod Farm -- Borewell, Motor, Power Cut Response

Your irrigation system has one critical vulnerability: a single electric submersible
borewell on grid power, 500m from the field, with no backup.
When it fails, every hour matters. This skill tells you exactly what to do.

---

## THE THREE FAILURE MODES

Identify which failure mode you are in before taking any action:

| Failure Mode | Signs | Most Likely Cause | Average Resolution Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Power cut** | No electricity in area, neighbors also affected | PGVCL grid outage | 2 hours to 2 days |
| **Motor / pump failure** | Power available but pump not running, no water flow, unusual sound | Burnt motor, capacitor failure, stuck impeller | 1 to 5 days (repair or replacement) |
| **Borewell / water level** | Motor runs but little or no water output | Water table dropped, borewell casing issue, summer depletion | 1 day to 2 weeks |

**If you are not sure which mode:** call your motor mechanic first. They can diagnose
in 30 minutes on-site. Do not attempt electrical repairs yourself.

**Emergency contact to have saved:**
- Motor mechanic (local -- Keshod or Visavadar area)
- PGVCL Keshod subdivision office: for power cut status and restoration timeline
- Nearest bore rig operator: for borewell assessment

---

## IMMEDIATE ACTION PROTOCOL -- FIRST 60 MINUTES

Work through these steps in order. Do not skip to sourcing water before assessing.

### Step 1 -- Assess (0-15 min)

- [ ] Check if neighbors have power -- if yes, your power connection or motor is the problem
- [ ] Check the motor control panel / starter box -- blown fuse, tripped breaker, burnt smell
- [ ] Check the water level indicator if you have one -- or run motor briefly and listen for cavitation
- [ ] Call your mechanic -- give him the symptoms before he arrives

### Step 2 -- Classify and estimate downtime (15-30 min)

| What you find | Estimated downtime | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Area-wide power cut | 2 hours to 48 hours | Go to Step 3. Check PGVCL app or call lineman. |
| Blown fuse / tripped breaker | 30 minutes to 2 hours | Ask mechanic -- simple fix but do not do it yourself |
| Burnt motor smell | 2 to 5 days | Motor rewind or replacement. Start sourcing backup water now. |
| Motor runs, no water | 1 to 3 days | Water table or pipe issue. Call bore rig operator. |
| Unknown | Treat as 2 days minimum | Start backup sourcing now, assess in parallel |

### Step 3 -- Triage crops immediately (30-45 min)

Do not wait for repair confirmation. Start triage now.

Read the crop survival windows in `references/01-crop-water-tolerance.md`.
Identify which crops are in your field right now and their days-without-water limit.

**Quick triage rule for 3 Vigha:**
Priority 1 -- Any crop at critical growth stage (flowering, pod fill, fruit set)
Priority 2 -- Crops under 30 days old (seedling stage -- most vulnerable)
Priority 3 -- Established crops with deep roots (bajra, dhaincha) -- most resilient

### Step 4 -- Source emergency water (45-60 min)

See Emergency Water Sources section below. Make one call now.
Do not wait until crops show stress -- visible wilting means you are already 12 to 24 hours late.

## EMERGENCY WATER SOURCES -- KESHOD AREA

Use the source that is fastest, not cheapest. Crop loss costs more than any of these.

| Source | How to Access | Volume Available | Approx. Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Neighbor farmer with borewell | Ask directly -- most will share during emergency | 10,000 to 30,000 L per session | Free or reciprocal favor | 1 to 2 days cover while repair happens |
| Water tanker (private) | Call any tanker transport in Keshod / Visavadar | 5,000 to 10,000 L per trip | Rs 800 to 1,500 per trip | Multi-day outage, critical crops |
| Village pond / talav | Check with Gram Panchayat for permission | Seasonal -- best in Kharif | Free or nominal | Bajra, sesame, cowpea (not sensitive crops) |
| Diesel generator rental + your pump | Generator rental shops in Keshod market | Continuous while rented | Rs 500 to 900 per day rental + diesel | Power cut only -- motor still working |
| Hand-carried water | Buckets or cans from any nearby source | Limited -- 500 to 1,000 L/day manually | Labor cost only | Seedlings, small nursery, spot rescue |
| Canal or nala (seasonal) | Check with neighboring farmers -- seasonal availability | High in Kharif, none in summer | Free with pump | Kharif season only |

**Realistic minimum to save 3 Vigha in peak Kharif:**
25,000 to 35,000 L per week at full demand.
One tanker trip buys you 1 day maximum. Generator rental is the only multi-day solution
if your motor is working and only power is the problem.

## WATER RATIONING -- WHEN SUPPLY IS LIMITED

If you cannot fully irrigate all 3 Vigha, ration in this order:

**Full irrigation priority:**
1. Any crop at flowering or pod-fill stage -- yield loss is permanent if missed here
2. Seedlings under 30 days old -- they die within 48 hours without water
3. Cumin or other high-value Rabi crops -- economic loss disproportionate to area

**Reduced irrigation (50% of normal):**
4. Established bajra, sesame, guar -- deep-rooted, tolerate 5 to 7 days stress
5. Dhaincha / cover crops -- they will recover even if partially wilted

**Defer irrigation (accept stress, monitor):**
6. Any crop past harvest maturity -- let it stand, it does not need more water
7. Bare soil / fallow areas -- no irrigation needed

**Technique for rationing:**
Apply water only to the root zone, not broadcast.
Use furrow or drip to root zone rather than flood irrigation.
Irrigating at 50% area coverage uses half the water but reaches 100% of plant roots.

## SOIL MOISTURE CHECK -- DO THIS BEFORE EACH IRRIGATION DECISION

Before calling a tanker or running a generator: check actual soil moisture.
Alkaline loamy-clay holds water significantly longer than sandy soil.

**How to check (no tools needed):**
1. Dig 4 to 6 inches with your hand or a stick
2. Take a pinch of soil and press it into a ball

| Soil ball result | Moisture status | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Forms firm ball, feels cool and moist | Sufficient moisture | Wait 24 more hours before emergency sourcing |
| Crumbles partially, slightly moist | Getting dry | Source water within 12 hours |
| Powdery, no ball forms, warm | Critically dry | Emergency irrigation needed NOW |

**Your loamy-clay soil advantage:**
After a full irrigation, your soil holds usable moisture for approximately:
- Summer (April-June): 3 to 5 days
- Kharif monsoon (July-September): 5 to 8 days
- Rabi cool season (November-February): 7 to 10 days

This means a power cut under 3 days in Kharif season is not automatically a crisis
if the soil was irrigated within the last 2 days.
