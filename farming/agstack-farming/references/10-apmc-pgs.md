## 1. APMC STRUCTURE

### Two Primary Markets for Keshod Farm

**APMC Keshod (primary)**
- Location: Keshod town, Junagadh district
- Best for: Daily vegetables, local pulses (Cowpea, Cluster Bean), Methi, Coriander
- Advantage: short transport, lower handling loss, relationship-based sales to local traders
- Limitation: smaller market, fewer buyers for specialty crops, lower price discovery
- Commodity arrivals and price: eauction.apmc.in (Gujarat APMC e-auction portal)

**APMC Junagadh (secondary)**
- Location: Junagadh city, ~25 km from Keshod
- Best for: Cumin, Sesame, Groundnut, Bajra, bulk pulses
- Advantage: much larger buyer pool, better price discovery, national trader participation
- Limitation: transport cost, minimum lot sizes often required
- Market days: daily for most crops; check ahead for Cumin which has peak season trading windows

**Keshod Local Market**
- For small daily harvest quantities: roadside / weekly haat market at Keshod
- No formal registration needed for small volumes
- Useful for vegetables, leafy greens, herbs -- immediate cash, no transport cost

### Natural Farming Premium Potential

Organic or chemical-free produce fetches a premium at:
- Direct-to-consumer: 20 to 40% above APMC price
- Specialty organic stores (Junagadh / Rajkot): 25 to 50% premium
- Institutional buyers (hotels, hospitals, schools): 15 to 30% premium
- Online platforms (INATrace QR code enables this market)

**The INATrace traceability certificate is the key to accessing premium buyers.**
Without documentation, premium claims are verbal only.

## 3. HOW TO SELL AT APMC -- STEP BY STEP

### First-Time Registration

1. Get a licensed commission agent (Arhatiya) -- most farmers work through one
   Ask neighboring farmers for a recommendation at APMC Keshod or Junagadh
2. Bring: Aadhaar card, bank passbook, land records (7/12 Utara)
3. Obtain seller registration at APMC office if selling directly (not required if using agent)
4. Open a separate bank account for farm income -- simplifies income tracking

### Each Sale

1. Harvest, clean, and grade produce (remove damaged, wet, or immature items)
2. Weigh at home -- note weight in farm journal before transport
3. Transport to APMC -- note time of arrival, lot number
4. Produce is weighed and graded at APMC -- check the weigh-in ticket
5. Auction or direct negotiation -- price agreed with buyer or agent
6. Payment within 1 to 3 working days (APMC rules require same-day or next-day payment)
7. Collect receipt with: date, crop, quantity, price, buyer name

### What to Record in Farm Calendar After Each Sale

Log as a "Harvest + Sale" activity entry:
```
Activity type: Harvest
Parcel: [your GeoID parcels]
Date: [harvest date]
Crop: [crop name]
Quantity harvested: X kg
Quality grade: A / B / mixed
Sold at: APMC Keshod / APMC Junagadh / local market
Quantity sold: X kg
Price received: Rs Y per kg
Total income: Rs Z
Buyer / agent name: [name]
Receipt number: [number from APMC]
Notes: [any quality issues, price context]
```

This log becomes your income record AND your PGS certification evidence.

## 4. LOGGING SALES IN FARM CALENDAR FOR MARKET INTELLIGENCE

Track every sale so that over 3 to 5 seasons you have a real price trend for your specific farm.

### What a Useful Multi-Season Price Log Looks Like

| Season | Crop | Qty (kg) | APMC Price (Rs/kg) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Kharif 2026 | Sesame | 120 | 92 | First chemical-free season, mixed quality |
| Rabi 2026-27 | Cumin | 85 | 310 | Good quality, Feb sale |
| Kharif 2027 | Sesame | 180 | 108 | Soil improving, better grade |
| Rabi 2027-28 | Cumin | 140 | 285 | Slightly lower price, higher quantity |

Over time this table answers: is natural farming increasing yield enough to offset any price variation?

### Generating a Seasonal Market Report

After each season, use the AgStack Reporting Service to compile:
- Total harvest per crop per Vigha
- Total income per crop
- Price received vs MSP vs APMC average
- Season-on-season yield trend
- Input costs vs income ratio

This report is also your demonstration farm's evidence file for visitors and potential PGS certification reviewers.

## 5. PGS-INDIA CERTIFICATION -- WHAT IT IS AND WHY IT MATTERS

### What PGS-India Is

PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System) is a domestic organic certification program
run by the Government of India under the Ministry of Agriculture. It is:
- Peer-reviewed: a group of farmers certifies each other through observation visits
- Low cost: Rs 500 to 2,000 for the entire process (vs Rs 20,000+ for third-party certification)
- Farmer-controlled: no external inspector required for each season
- Legally recognized: PGS certificate is accepted at all APMCs and government schemes
- Easier to access premium buyers: many organic stores and direct buyers accept PGS

**PGS-India is the right first step for your demonstration farm.**
Third-party organic certification (NPOP) is suitable after 3+ years of documented records.

### PGS vs NPOP vs Third-Party

| Feature | PGS-India | NPOP (National) | Third-Party |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cost | Rs 500-2,000 | Rs 15,000-30,000 | Rs 20,000-50,000+ |
| Time to certificate | 3 to 6 months | 2 to 3 years | 1 to 3 years |
| Audit method | Peer farmer group | External inspector | External inspector |
| Recognition | Domestic market | Domestic + limited export | International export |
| Best for | Smallholder, demonstration farm | Established organic farm | Export-oriented farm |

### What PGS-India Requires

1. Minimum group size: 5 farmers (you can form a group with nearby natural farming neighbors)
2. Conversion period documentation: records showing no synthetic inputs for minimum 1 season
3. Farm description: land records, soil type, water source, crop history
4. Input log: what was applied, when, how much (must be organic inputs only)
5. Peer inspection: one annual visit by group members to each farm
6. Record keeping: harvest quantities, input costs, sales -- all documented
7. Local Group registration at nearest Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK Junagadh) or APMC

## 6. PGS DOCUMENTATION USING FARM CALENDAR

The Farm Calendar is your primary PGS evidence file if you log correctly from Day 1.

### What to Log and When

**Before each season (mandatory):**
```
Seasonal declaration entry in Farm Calendar:
- Season: Kharif / Rabi YYYY
- Parcels: [GeoID and area]
- Crops planned: [list]
- Statement: No synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, or herbicides used or planned
- Soil status: [OC%, pH from last test if available]
```

**Every input application:**
```
Activity: Fertilization / Soil Amendment
Date: [exact date]
Parcel: [GeoID]
Input name: Jeevamrut / Beejamrut / Neem cake / Vermicompost / Gypsum / etc.
Quantity: X litres or kg per Vigha
Method: Drip / spray / broadcast / soil application
Prepared by: On-farm
Notes: Batch number, preparation date if applicable
```

**Every pest/disease intervention:**
```
Activity: Crop Protection
Date: [exact date]
Parcel: [GeoID]
Product: Neemastra / Agniastra / Dashaparni Ark / etc.
Quantity: X litres
Method: Foliar spray
Reason: [pest or disease observed]
```

**Every observation (at minimum weekly during crop season):**
```
Activity: Observation
Date: [exact date]
Notes: Crop growth stage, earthworm count, soil moisture visual, pest pressure, weather anomaly
```

**Every harvest:**
```
Activity: Harvest
Date, parcel, crop, quantity, quality grade, sales details (as per Section 3 above)
```

### What PGS Reviewers Look For in Your Records

- Continuity: no unexplained gaps in records during growing season
- No synthetic inputs in any log entry
- Consistent with what was observed during peer inspection visit
- Harvest quantities plausible for natural farming on your area
- Input quantities consistent with what you told them you applied

If you log everything in Farm Calendar from Day 1 of Phase 1 (April-May 2026),
you will have a complete, timestamped record by the time you apply for PGS
(earliest: after Rabi 2026-27 season -- approximately March 2027).

## 7. PGS PEER GROUP FORMATION

You cannot get PGS-India certification as an individual. A group of at least 5 farmers is required.

### How to Form a Group

1. Identify 4+ neighboring farmers doing or interested in natural farming
   (ZBNF practitioners, even part-time, qualify)
2. Approach KVK Junagadh -- they maintain a list of natural farming practitioners in the district
3. Approach Gram Sevak or Gram Panchayat -- they know who is farming organically nearby
4. Contact Subhash Palekar Natural Farming network -- active in Gujarat with farmer groups

### Group Responsibilities

- Each member visits at least 2 other members' farms per season for peer inspection
- Group maintains a shared record file submitted to KVK or APMC annually
- Any member found using synthetic inputs removes the certification for the entire group
  (This is the peer accountability mechanism -- choose group members carefully)

### Registration

- Register at KVK Junagadh: bring group member list, land records, initial farm declarations
- KVK issues Local Group registration number
- Apply for PGS-India certificate at National Centre of Organic Farming (NCOF) portal
  or through KVK as facilitator
- Website: pgsindia-ncof.gov.in

## 8. INATRACE VS PGS -- WHICH TO USE WHEN

These are two different systems with different purposes. Both are valuable and complementary.

| Situation | Use | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Selling to domestic Indian buyers who want "organic" claim | PGS-India certificate | Legally recognized domestic organic label |
| Selling to premium urban buyers who want to verify farm identity | INATrace QR code | Shows farm location, inputs, practices -- verifiable on phone |
| Export market or international buyer | INATrace + NPOP (future) | International buyers need third-party certification |
| Demonstration farm visitors who want to see your farm data | INATrace + AgStack GeoID | Links to public Farm Calendar report |
| Government scheme benefits requiring organic certification | PGS-India certificate | Government schemes recognize PGS as valid organic certificate |
| Building a multi-year soil improvement evidence record | Farm Calendar + Reporting Service | Internal and external auditable record |

**Recommended workflow for your farm:**
- Start Farm Calendar logging immediately (from Day 1, April-May 2026)
- Form PGS group in Year 1, apply for certificate after Rabi 2026-27
- Set up INATrace after first certified harvest for premium market access
- Keep both running in parallel from Year 2 onward
